## Summary

Use this guide when an electronic title needs to be printed or flipped into the dealership’s name.

## When This Applies

* Vehicle has an electronic Florida title.
* Dealer needs the title printed into the dealership’s name.
* Dealer needs the title for wholesale, resale, or correction.

## Our Office Workflow

1. Confirm the vehicle is titled in Florida.
2. Confirm the title is electronic.
3. Confirm the customer is the titled owner of record.
4. Confirm there is a proper reassignment from the titled owner to the dealership.
5. Confirm the reassignment has the correct date and mileage.
6. Prepare the title application in the dealership’s name with proper dealership information.
7. Confirm the date on the title application matches the reassignment date.
8. Confirm the mileage on the title application matches the reassignment mileage.
9. Confirm Section 7 is signed.
10. Confirm Section 12 is signed.
11. If there is no proper reassignment from customer to dealer, complete the reassignment by POA on specialty paper/rainbow reassignment when allowed.
12. Confirm POA is valid if signing by POA.
13. Submit through appropriate tag agency.
14. Save support documents and confirmation.

## Common Mistakes

* No reassignment from customer/titled owner to dealership.
* Reassignment does not list the dealership correctly.
* Date on title application does not match reassignment date.
* Mileage on title application does not match reassignment mileage.
* Section 7 is not signed.
* Section 12 is not signed.
* POA is missing, incomplete, or does not match.
* Specialty paper/rainbow reassignment is missing when needed.
* Customer signing does not match titled owner of record.
* Title application is not completed in the dealership’s name.
* Trying to process an ELT print when the title is not electronic.

## When To Escalate

* Owner does not match.
* Reassignment from customer to dealership is missing.
* Reassignment is incomplete.
* Date or mileage does not match.
* Section 7 is not signed.
* Section 12 is not signed.
* POA is unclear, missing, or incomplete.
* Customer is deceased.
* Vehicle is not Florida-titled.
* There is a stop, brand, fraud concern, or unusual title status.
* You are unsure whether specialty paper/rainbow reassignment is required.

## Quick Checklist

* Florida title checked.
* Electronic title confirmed.
* Ownership checked.
* Reassignment to dealership checked.
* Title application completed in dealership’s name.
* Dealership information checked.
* Date matches reassignment.
* Mileage matches reassignment.
* Section 7 signed.
* Section 12 signed.
* POA checked, if used.
* Rainbow reassignment checked, if used.
* Forms complete.
* Fees confirmed.
* Rejection risk reviewed.
