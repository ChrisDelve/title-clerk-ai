============================================================
WYOMING SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Teton County, Wyoming says:

- If customer recently moved to Wyoming and is employed, they are required to register vehicle within 30 days of arrival.
- Customer must have vehicle VIN inspected by local law enforcement.
- Local law enforcement has the Wyoming VIN inspection form and completes it.
- VIN inspection cost listed by source: $10.
- Customer brings VIN inspection form to county office with:
  - Vehicle Title
  - Current out-of-state registration
  - Proof of Insurance
- If customer is making payments on vehicle and has title, customer also needs loan agreement with bank.
- If bank holds title, customer should fill out Lender Request Form and email or fax it to county office.
- Once county receives Lender Request Form, county sends it to bank to request title.
- Once title is received, county contacts customer to complete transfer.
- If customer does not have Vehicle Title, current out-of-state registration, or proof of insurance, customer should contact county office for help obtaining Wyoming plates and registration.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR WYOMING CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Wyoming customer and the customer wants Wyoming title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Vehicle title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder/bank information if financed
   - Loan agreement if customer is making payments and has title

2. Wyoming-specific items to review:
   - VIN inspection by local law enforcement
   - Wyoming VIN inspection form
   - Vehicle title
   - Current out-of-state registration
   - Proof of insurance
   - 30-day registration deadline if customer recently moved to Wyoming and is employed
   - Lender Request Form if bank holds title

3. If financed:
   - Determine whether customer has title or bank holds title.
   - If customer has title and is making payments, include loan agreement.
   - If bank holds title, complete/review Lender Request Form and send to county office.
   - Do not promise transfer completion until county receives title from bank.

4. If customer lacks title/registration/insurance:
   - Hold and escalate.
   - Contact county office/title agency before promising Wyoming plates/registration.

5. If registering:
   - Verify proof of insurance.
   - Confirm county-specific registration fees and requirements.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Wyoming customer buying from a Florida dealership, Wyoming/Teton County says the customer needs a VIN inspection by local law enforcement, then must bring the completed VIN inspection form, vehicle title, current out-of-state registration, and proof of insurance to the county office. If the customer recently moved to Wyoming and is employed, they must register within 30 days of arrival. If the customer is making payments and has the title, they also need the loan agreement. If the bank holds the title, the customer must complete the Lender Request Form and send it to the county office so the county can request the title from the bank. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder/bank information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Wyoming tax handling. Escalate if VIN inspection, title, out-of-state registration, insurance, lender request, loan agreement, or tax handling is unclear.
