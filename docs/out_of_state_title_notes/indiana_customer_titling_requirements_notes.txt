============================================================
INDIANA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Indiana FAQ says:

A vehicle may be registered in Indiana, but it must first be titled in Indiana.

Requirements listed:
- Proof of valid Social Security number; OR
- Proof of lawful status I-94 with Social Security Number Ineligibility Letter issued by Social Security Administration, dated within past 60 days of transaction
- Two proofs of address dated within past 60 days
- Valid and current title for the vehicle
- Physical inspection
- Proof of current insurance

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR INDIANA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Indiana customer and the customer wants Indiana title/registration:

1. Confirm whether Indiana title and registration are both requested.
   - Indiana FAQ says vehicle must first be titled in Indiana before registration.

2. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

3. Indiana-specific items to tell customer/agency to verify:
   - Valid/current title
   - Physical inspection
   - Proof of current insurance
   - Proof of valid SSN or lawful-status/I-94 + SSA ineligibility letter
   - Two proofs of address dated within past 60 days

4. If customer is not Indiana resident:
   - Use the Indiana nonresident FAQ carefully.
   - Escalate if residency/address requirements are unclear.
   - Do not promise Indiana registration without title-first review.

5. If financed:
   - Confirm lender/lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm Indiana title/lien recording requirements.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Indiana title-first requirement is missed.
- Physical inspection is not completed.
- Proof of current insurance is missing.
- Two proofs of address are missing or older than 60 days.
- SSN or lawful-status/I-94 + SSA ineligibility letter is missing.
- SSA ineligibility letter is older than 60 days.
- Valid/current title is missing.
- Customer’s Indiana/nonresident eligibility is unclear.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Indiana customer buying from a Florida dealership, Indiana says the vehicle must first be titled in Indiana before it can be registered. The Indiana FAQ lists requirements including valid/current title, physical inspection, proof of current insurance, proof of valid SSN or lawful-status/I-94 plus SSA ineligibility letter dated within 60 days, and two proofs of address dated within 60 days. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if physical inspection, address/identity documents, nonresident eligibility, insurance, or tax handling is unclear.
