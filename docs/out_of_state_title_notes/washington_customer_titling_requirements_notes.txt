============================================================
WASHINGTON SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Washington DOL says:

- Customer must get Washington driver license before registering vehicle(s).
- Driver licensing and vehicle licensing are done at separate locations.
- Customer has 30 days after moving to Washington to get driver license and register vehicle.
- If unsure what steps/documents/fees are needed, customer should contact a vehicle licensing office.
- Customer should bring current title to any vehicle licensing office for assistance.
- Customer should write down odometer mileage before going into office.
- To complete transaction by mail, customer should call vehicle licensing office for forms and fees.
- If bank currently holds title, customer should call vehicle licensing office to get fax number so bank can fax copy of title.
- If customer goes to office, plates are issued right away.
- If application is submitted by mail, plates may take up to 3 weeks from date application is received.
- Washington may provide open safety recall information at first registration or renewal.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR WASHINGTON CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Washington customer and the customer wants Washington title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Current title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Current odometer mileage
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Washington-specific items to review:
   - Washington driver license required before vehicle registration
   - 30-day driver license and vehicle registration deadline after moving to Washington
   - Current title for vehicle licensing office
   - Odometer mileage
   - Vehicle licensing office forms and fees
   - Bank-held title fax process
   - Mailing timeline if application processed by mail

3. If financed / bank holds title:
   - Customer should call vehicle licensing office for fax number.
   - Bank needs to fax copy of title.
   - Confirm lender/titleholder instructions before promising completion.

4. If registering by mail:
   - Customer or agency should call vehicle licensing office for exact forms and fees.
   - Warn plates may take up to 3 weeks after application received.

5. If customer recently moved:
   - Confirm Washington driver license status.
   - Confirm 30-day deadline.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Customer does not have Washington driver license before registration.
- 30-day moving deadline is missed.
- Current title is missing.
- Odometer mileage not recorded.
- Bank holds title and fax process is not arranged.
- Vehicle licensing office forms/fees are not confirmed.
- Mail transaction is submitted without correct forms or fees.
- Customer expects immediate plates from mail application.
- Lienholder/title instructions unclear.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Washington customer buying from a Florida dealership, Washington says the customer must get a Washington driver license before registering vehicles and has 30 days after moving to Washington to get their license and register. The customer should bring the current title to a vehicle licensing office and write down the odometer mileage before going. If the bank holds the title, the customer should call the vehicle licensing office for a fax number so the bank can fax a copy of the title. If processing by mail, the customer should call the vehicle licensing office for required forms and fees; plates may take up to 3 weeks after the application is received. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Washington tax handling. Escalate if Washington license, current title, bank-held title fax, mail forms/fees, lienholder, or tax handling is unclear.
