SOURCE LIMITATION
============================================================

The main New Hampshire DMV vehicle registration page could not be fully fetched during note creation.

Official NH DMV search result snippets indicate:
- Proof of VIN is needed.
- Proof of VIN can be original or copy of title, previous or copy of New Hampshire registration, or properly completed VIN verification form.
- Brand-new vehicle registrations require Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin (MSO).
- Proof of residency is required if not already provided.
- New residents have a maximum of 60 days to register vehicles after establishing residency.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NEW HAMPSHIRE CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a New Hampshire customer and the customer wants New Hampshire title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for brand-new vehicle
   - Properly assigned title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Full VIN

2. New Hampshire-specific items to review:
   - Proof of VIN
   - Proof of residency if not already provided
   - MSO for brand-new vehicle
   - 60-day registration timing for new residents
   - New Hampshire DMV/town clerk requirements

3. If proof of VIN is needed:
   - Review whether original/copy of title is enough.
   - Review whether previous/copy of New Hampshire registration applies.
   - Otherwise, obtain properly completed VIN verification form.

4. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title handling.
   - Confirm whether title copy/lienholder information can support VIN/title proof.

5. Because source was limited:
   - Verify directly with NH DMV, town/city clerk, title agency, or lender before final submission.
