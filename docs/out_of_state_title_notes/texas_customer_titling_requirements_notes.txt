TEXAS SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Texas DMV says:

- A vehicle must be titled in the owner’s name within 30 days of the date of sale to avoid delinquent transfer penalties.
- A vehicle must be registered within 30 days of bringing the vehicle to Texas.

Inspection:
- All commercial vehicles must obtain a passing safety inspection before registration, regardless of county of residence.
- Non-commercial vehicles registered in certain emissions counties are not required to obtain a passing safety inspection but are required to obtain a passing emissions inspection before registration.

Texas emissions counties listed by source:
- Brazoria
- Collin
- Dallas
- Denton
- El Paso
- Ellis
- Fort Bend
- Galveston
- Harris
- Johnson
- Kaufman
- Montgomery
- Parker
- Rockwall
- Tarrant
- Travis
- Williamson

Required items after applicable inspection:
- Proof of Inspection, Vehicle Inspection Report, if applicable
- Proof of liability insurance meeting Texas minimum requirements
- Vehicle odometer reading if required
- Original out-of-state title or other valid ownership evidence
- Proof of sales tax payment or current foreign/military ownership document
- Completed Application for Texas Title and/or Registration, Form 130-U
- If the vehicle is not subject to commercial or emissions inspection, self-certify VIN on Form 130-U

Fees listed by Texas source:
- Registration base fee of $50.75 for passenger vehicles and light trucks
- Title application fee of $28 or $33 depending on county
- State portion of vehicle inspection fee up to $30.75
- Local county fees up to $31.50
- 6.25% vehicle sales tax
- $1 insurance verification fee
- $4.75 processing and handling fee

Imported vehicles:
- Vehicle must meet federal and state importation requirements before Texas title/registration.
- Imported vehicles may require FMVSS/DOT/customs compliance, Form HS-7 or other customs/federal safety documentation, bond release if under bond, Texas inspection, VTR-68-A VIN inspection by trained auto theft investigator, and certified English translations for non-English documents.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR TEXAS CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Texas customer and the customer wants Texas title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Original title or MCO/MSO / valid ownership evidence
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Proof of sales tax/payment support if applicable

2. Texas-specific items to review:
   - Form 130-U Application for Texas Title and/or Registration
   - Texas liability insurance
   - Applicable inspection / emissions requirements
   - Vehicle Inspection Report if applicable
   - Odometer reading if required
   - 30-day title deadline from date of sale
   - 30-day registration deadline after bringing vehicle to Texas
   - Texas title/registration fees and 6.25% sales tax

3. If customer’s Texas county is an emissions county:
   - Confirm emissions inspection before registration.
   - Verify proof of insurance for inspection.

4. If commercial vehicle:
   - Confirm passing safety inspection before registration.

5. If vehicle is imported/foreign:
   - Review FMVSS, DOT, customs, HS-7, bond release, VTR-68-A, and English translation requirements.
   - Escalate before submission if import documents are incomplete.

6. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title instructions.
   - Confirm lienholder information is included on Texas title package.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Texas customer buying from a Florida dealership, Texas requires title within 30 days of sale and registration within 30 days of bringing the vehicle to Texas. The customer/title package generally needs Form 130-U, original out-of-state title or valid ownership evidence, proof of Texas liability insurance, odometer reading if required, proof of inspection if applicable, proof of sales tax payment, and Texas fees/taxes. Commercial vehicles require safety inspection before registration, and non-commercial vehicles in listed emissions counties require emissions inspection. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Texas tax handling. Escalate if inspection, emissions, imported vehicle documents, Form 130-U, original title, insurance, or tax handling is unclear.
