============================================================
MARYLAND SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Maryland MVA says:

- Vehicle title and registration must be updated within 60 days of moving to Maryland.
- Failure to meet the deadline disqualifies customer from a tax credit for titling tax paid in another state and may cause citation for out-of-state registration.
- New Maryland residents that already own a vehicle or purchased a vehicle out of state must title and register it in Maryland.
- Valid Maryland Safety Inspection Certificate is required to title and register vehicle in Maryland.
- Safety inspection must be performed by authorized Maryland inspection station.
- If returning to Maryland with a vehicle previously titled in Maryland in customer’s name, safety inspection is not required; complete VR-018 Duplicate Certificate of Title application.
- Emissions/VEIP testing is not done at safety inspection; VEIP notice is sent about three months after registration.

Documents for titling appointment include:
- Valid Maryland driver license or proof of valid Maryland residential address
- Out-of-state title
- If leased or has lien: fax number, account number and mailing information for lienholder, and registration card
- Completed VR-005 if pre-application was not completed
- Maryland insurance information:
  - Insurance company name
  - Policy or binder number
  - Agent name if applicable
- VR-470 Restricted Power of Attorney if required signatures are missing, with proper identification
- Check or money order for payment
- Excise tax is paid when titling/registering; amount depends on vehicle age and tax paid in previous state

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MARYLAND CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Maryland customer and the customer wants Maryland title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Maryland-specific items to review:
   - Maryland safety inspection certificate
   - Out-of-state title
   - Maryland DL or proof of Maryland residential address
   - Maryland insurance information
   - VR-005 if pre-application was not completed
   - VR-470 if required signatures are missing
   - Lienholder fax/account/mailing info and registration card if leased/lien
   - Check or money order for payment

3. If financed or leased:
   - Confirm lienholder or lessor instructions.
   - Provide fax number, account number, mailing information, and registration card if required.
   - Confirm title/lienholder path.

4. If customer has not completed Maryland safety inspection:
   - Do not promise Maryland title/registration can complete.
   - Escalate or advise customer/agency that Maryland inspection is required.

5. If tax credit involved:
   - Maryland deadline matters.
   - Failure to title/register within 60 days may disqualify tax credit for taxes paid to another state.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Maryland customer buying from a Florida dealership, Maryland requires title and registration within 60 days for new Maryland residents or vehicles purchased out of state. Maryland requires a valid Maryland Safety Inspection Certificate from an authorized Maryland inspection station before title/registration. Required documents include Maryland driver license or proof of Maryland residential address, out-of-state title, Maryland insurance information, VR-005 if pre-application was not completed, and VR-470 Restricted POA if required signatures are missing. If the vehicle is leased or has a lien, bring lienholder fax number, account number, mailing information, and registration card. Prepare the dealer ownership package and review Florida DR-123/Maryland excise tax handling. Escalate if inspection, lienholder, insurance, VR forms, 60-day tax credit, or tax handling is unclear.
