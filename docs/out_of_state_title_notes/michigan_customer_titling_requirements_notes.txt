MICHIGAN SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Michigan SOS says vehicles purchased and titled out-of-state can be converted to a Michigan title by presenting the following at a Secretary of State office:

- Out-of-state vehicle title
- Driver’s license or ID
- Michigan No-Fault insurance
- Out-of-state vehicle registration if it was registered in another state
- Lien termination statement or title signed by lienholder

If the title is held by an out-of-state lienholder, Michigan says to also provide one of the following:

- Photocopy or fax of out-of-state title
- Memo title
- Recently validated title application
- Vehicle record printed on the issuing state’s DMV letterhead verifying the vehicle is titled in that state

Michigan notes:
- If title is held by out-of-state lienholder, Michigan will issue a Foreign Ownership-Registration Only registration, also known as memo registration.
- A Michigan title is not issued in that case.
- The out-of-state title remains the ownership document.

Michigan FAQ also says proof of title can include:
- Fax copy or photocopy of title
- Memo title
- Recently validated title application
- Vehicle record on state government letterhead verifying vehicle is titled in that state
- Electronic Lien and Title record issued by the state’s ELT vendor such as DealerTrack, VINTek, Premier eTitleLien, PDP, or DMVDesk containing the title number
- Title number cannot be handwritten

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MICHIGAN CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Michigan customer and the customer wants Michigan title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Michigan-specific items to review:
   - Michigan No-Fault insurance if registration is requested
   - Driver license or ID
   - Out-of-state title
   - Out-of-state registration if vehicle was registered in another state
   - Lien termination statement or title signed by lienholder if lien exists

3. If title is held by out-of-state lienholder:
   - Review Michigan Foreign Ownership-Registration Only workflow.
   - Michigan may register only and not issue Michigan title.
   - Provide acceptable proof of title such as copy/fax title, memo title, validated title application, state DMV record, or ELT vendor record with title number.
   - Confirm title number is not handwritten.

4. If financed:
   - Confirm lender/titleholder instructions.
   - Confirm whether Michigan title will be issued or if foreign ownership registration-only applies.

5. If registering:
   - Michigan No-Fault insurance is required.
   - Do not treat Florida insurance as satisfying Michigan registration requirements unless Michigan/agency confirms.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Michigan No-Fault insurance is missing.
- Out-of-state title is missing.
- Lien termination statement or title signed by lienholder is missing.
- Title held by out-of-state lienholder and proof of title is incomplete.
- ELT record lacks printed/non-handwritten title number.
- Michigan Foreign Ownership-Registration Only is misunderstood as Michigan title issuance.
- Out-of-state registration is missing when required.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Michigan customer buying from a Florida dealership, Michigan requires the out-of-state title, driver license or ID, Michigan No-Fault insurance if registering, out-of-state registration if the vehicle was registered in another state, and lien termination or title signed by lienholder if a lien exists. If the title is held by an out-of-state lienholder, Michigan may issue Foreign Ownership-Registration Only registration instead of a Michigan title, and the out-of-state title remains the ownership document. Acceptable proof may include copy/fax title, memo title, recently validated title application, state DMV vehicle record, or ELT vendor record with printed title number. Prepare the dealer ownership package, lienholder documents, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if Michigan No-Fault, lienholder title, ELT proof, foreign ownership registration, or tax handling is unclear.
