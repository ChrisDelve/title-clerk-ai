============================================================
IOWA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Iowa DOT registration page says vehicle registration requires:
- Current ownership document / certificate of title
- Completed Application for Iowa Certificate of Title and/or Registration
- Application must be signed by all owners
- If lien exists, it should be noted on the application

Iowa DOT title page says for newly purchased used vehicle with out-of-state/non-Iowa title, customer needs:
- Signed title from previous owner/dealer
- Sales tax receipt, bill of sale, or purchase agreement
- Completed Application for Iowa Certificate of Title, Form 411007
- Cancellation of Security Interest, Form 411168, if an uncancelled lien is showing on front of title
- Odometer Statement completed on back of title
- If mileage is not noted on back of title for vehicles model year 2011 or newer, use Form 411077
- Damage Disclosure, Form 411108, for vehicles 8 years old or newer

Iowa DOT also states motor vehicle must be titled and registered with county treasurer within 30 days from date residency is established.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR IOWA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Iowa customer and the customer wants Iowa title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale / purchase agreement
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Iowa-specific items to review:
   - Iowa Form 411007 Application for Certificate of Title and/or Registration
   - All owners sign application
   - Security interest/lien noted on application
   - Form 411168 if uncancelled lien shows on title
   - Form 411077 if 2011-or-newer mileage not on back of title
   - Form 411108 damage disclosure for vehicles 8 years old or newer
   - 30-day title/registration timing if residency established

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/security interest information.
   - Confirm Iowa title/lien notation requirements.

4. If used/out-of-state title:
   - Ensure prior owner/dealer signed title.
   - Include sales tax receipt, bill of sale, or purchase agreement.
   - Confirm odometer and damage disclosure requirements.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Form 411007 is missing or not signed by all owners.
- Signed title from previous owner/dealer is missing.
- Sales tax receipt, bill of sale, or purchase agreement is missing.
- Uncancelled lien shows and Form 411168 is missing.
- Odometer statement is missing for 2011-or-newer vehicle when mileage not on title.
- Damage Disclosure Form 411108 is missing for vehicle 8 years old or newer.
- Iowa 30-day timing issue is ignored.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Iowa customer buying from a Florida dealership, Iowa generally needs the current ownership document, completed Form 411007 signed by all owners, and for a used out-of-state title, the signed title from previous owner/dealer plus sales tax receipt, bill of sale, or purchase agreement. If an uncancelled lien shows, Iowa Form 411168 may be needed. If the vehicle is 2011 or newer and mileage is not on the title, use Form 411077. If the vehicle is 8 years old or newer, Iowa Damage Disclosure Form 411108 may be required. Prepare the dealer package, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if lien/security interest, odometer, damage disclosure, timing, or tax handling is unclear.
