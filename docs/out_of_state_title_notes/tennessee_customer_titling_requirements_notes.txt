============================================================
TENNESSEE SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Tennessee Department of Revenue says:

Current Tennessee residents can obtain titles for new and used vehicles from their county clerk’s office.

For new vehicle titles, applicants must bring:
- Proof of identification and proof of residency
- Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin
- New vehicle’s invoice
- Copy of current registration if transferring license plate

For used vehicle titles, applicants must bring:
- Proof of identification and proof of residency
- Valid certificate of title
- Odometer disclosure statement, if applicable
- Copy of current registration if transferring license plate
- Affidavit of Non-Dealer Transfers for gift, low selling, or lineal relative vehicle transfers

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR TENNESSEE CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Tennessee customer and the customer wants Tennessee title/registration:

1. Determine whether vehicle is:
   - New vehicle
   - Used vehicle
   - Plate transfer involved
   - Gift/low-selling/lineal relative transfer issue
   - Financed/lienholder involved

2. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MSO/MCO for new vehicle
   - Valid certificate of title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - New vehicle invoice if new
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

3. Tennessee-specific items to review:
   - Proof of identification and proof of residency
   - County clerk processing
   - Copy of current registration if transferring Tennessee plate
   - Affidavit of Non-Dealer Transfers only for gift/low-selling/lineal relative transfer scenarios
   - Tennessee title/registration taxes and fees

4. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title handling.
   - Confirm county clerk/lender requirements.
   - Confirm lien notation documentation if applicable.

5. If plate transfer:
   - Obtain copy of current registration.
   - Verify plate transfer eligibility with Tennessee county clerk/agency.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Tennessee customer buying from a Florida dealership, Tennessee titles are handled through the county clerk. For a new vehicle, Tennessee requires proof of identification/residency, manufacturer’s statement of origin, new vehicle invoice, and current registration copy if transferring a plate. For a used vehicle, Tennessee requires proof of identification/residency, valid certificate of title, odometer disclosure statement if applicable, and current registration copy if transferring a plate. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, MSO/MCO or title, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Tennessee tax handling. Escalate if proof of residency, MSO/title, invoice, plate transfer registration, lienholder, county clerk, or tax handling is unclear.
