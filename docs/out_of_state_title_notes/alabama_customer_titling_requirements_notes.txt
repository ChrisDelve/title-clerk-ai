ALABAMA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Alabama guidance from Madison County separates requirements by:

1. Vehicle with a lien
2. Vehicle with a clear title or no lien
3. Leased vehicle

For Alabama registration/title procedures, the source says the vehicle must be brought in at the time of registering so the VIN number and mileage can be certified.

Alabama also requires proof of Alabama insurance for Alabama registration.

============================================================
ALABAMA — VEHICLE WITH LIEN
============================================================

Alabama source says a vehicle with a lien requires:

- Legible copy of original Certificate of Title; OR
- Previous out-of-state registration with title number listed
- Valid driver license for all parties
- Proof of Alabama insurance
- Vehicle brought in at time of registering for VIN number and mileage certification
- Last registration from previous state

Alabama source notes that certain states send the valid original title to the owner and not to the lienholder. Applicants from those states must surrender the title.

States listed:
AZ, KS, KY, ME, MD, MN, NJ, NY, OK, WI

States Alabama says may sometimes give original title to either lienholder or owner:
MI, MT, SD, WY

ALABAMA — CLEAR TITLE / NO LIEN
============================================================

Alabama source says clear title/no lien vehicle requires:

- Original title
- Valid driver license for all parties
- Proof of Alabama insurance
- Vehicle brought in at time of registering for VIN number and mileage certification
- Power of Attorney if one party cannot appear for title procedures
- Last registration from previous state

ALABAMA — LEASED VEHICLE
============================================================

Alabama source says leased vehicles require:

- Original Certificate of Title
- Original Power of Attorney from the lease company
- Valid driver license for all parties
- Proof of Alabama insurance
- Name-change documentation if title is in former name
- Overseas registration if involved
- Last registration from previous state
- Vehicle information including make, model, year, color, body type, cylinders, mileage, and current address

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR ALABAMA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Alabama customer and the customer wants Alabama title/registration:

1. Determine Alabama transaction type:
   - Clear title / no lien
   - Vehicle with lien
   - Leased vehicle

2. Prepare dealership documents:
   - Properly assigned title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Lease company POA if leased

3. Tell customer Alabama may require:
   - Proof of Alabama insurance
   - Valid driver licenses
   - Vehicle brought in for VIN and mileage certification
   - Last registration from previous state
   - Original title or copy/registration support depending on lien status

4. If customer cannot physically bring vehicle to Alabama office for VIN/mileage certification:
   - Do not assume Alabama will waive inspection.
   - Escalate to title lead, agency, or Alabama licensing office.

5. If financed:
   - Verify lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm whether Alabama will accept dealer/lienholder title package.
   - Review whether original title/MSO goes to Alabama, lienholder, or agency.

6. If registering in Alabama:
   - Alabama insurance is required by Alabama source.
   - Florida insurance rules do not replace Alabama insurance requirements.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Customer does not have Alabama insurance.
- Vehicle cannot be brought for Alabama VIN/mileage certification.
- Lien/title possession is unclear.
- Original title is missing when Alabama requires it.
- Leased vehicle lacks original lease company POA.
- Last registration from previous state is missing.
- POA is missing or not notarized when required.
- Name-change documentation is missing.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Alabama customer buying from a Florida dealership, first determine whether the vehicle has a lien, clear title/no lien, or is leased. Alabama guidance says the vehicle must be brought in at registration for VIN and mileage certification and requires proof of Alabama insurance. Clear title/no lien vehicles require the original title; lien vehicles may require a copy of the original title or prior registration with title number; leased vehicles require original title and original lease company POA. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, lienholder information, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if Alabama inspection, lienholder, lease POA, or tax handling is unclear.
