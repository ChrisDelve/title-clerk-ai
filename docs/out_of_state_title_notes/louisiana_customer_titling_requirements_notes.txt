============================================================
LOUISIANA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Louisiana OMV Policy 48.00 covers out-of-state transfers and reciprocity.

For vehicles already titled/registered in the applicant’s name in another state, Louisiana requires:

- Vehicle Application DPSMV1799 completed and signed by owner
- Section F: Out of State Declaration on page 2 of DPSMV1799
- Original out-of-state certificate of title if no lien is recorded
- If original title is held by lienholder, a copy of the title or vehicle registration showing lienholder information
- Current driver license or ID for each vehicle owner
- Louisiana credential: copy of front
- Out-of-state or U.S. territory credential: copy of front and back
- Active duty military ID must be physically verified and not photocopied
- Proof of current liability insurance if requesting a license plate
- Insurance company must be authorized to do business in Louisiana
- Applicable tax, title, and license fees

Louisiana fees listed in Policy 48.00:

- $68.50 title fee
- $8.00 handling fee
- License plate fee, if applicable
- Mortgage/lien recordation fee if lien applies:
  - $10.00 if original out-of-state title indicates outstanding lien
  - $15.00 if UCC-1 is presented to record lien
  - $10.00 for another security agreement

Louisiana road use tax notes:

- Road use tax is required before issuance of Louisiana title or registration.
- Louisiana state and local use taxes may be limited to not more than $90.00 for qualifying new residents if conditions are met.
- If titled/registered in Louisiana after 90 days of bringing the vehicle into Louisiana, road use tax may be assessed on vehicle value.
- Louisiana tax credit may depend on proof of tax paid to another state and Louisiana reciprocity rules.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR LOUISIANA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Louisiana customer and the customer wants Louisiana title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Louisiana-specific items to review:
   - DPSMV1799 Vehicle Application
   - Section F Out of State Declaration
   - Original out-of-state title if no lien
   - Copy of title or registration showing lienholder if title held by lienholder
   - Owner IDs
   - Liability insurance from Louisiana-authorized insurer if plate requested
   - Louisiana title/handling/license/mortgage fees
   - Road use tax and reciprocity/tax credit

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title handling.
   - Confirm whether copy of title or registration showing lienholder is acceptable.
   - Review mortgage/lien recordation fee.

4. If plate requested:
   - Confirm Louisiana-authorized liability insurance.
   - Do not treat Florida insurance as automatically satisfying Louisiana plate requirements.

5. If tax/reciprocity involved:
   - Review Florida DR-123 handling and Louisiana tax credit/road use tax.
   - Escalate to controller/tax/title agency if tax credit or road use tax is unclear.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- DPSMV1799 is missing or unsigned.
- Section F Out of State Declaration is missing.
- Original out-of-state title is missing when no lien exists.
- Title held by lienholder but title copy/registration showing lienholder is missing.
- Owner ID copies are incomplete.
- Active duty military ID is photocopied instead of verified with notation.
- Louisiana-authorized liability insurance is missing when plate requested.
- Mortgage/lien fee is missed.
- Road use tax / reciprocity credit is unclear.
- Florida DR-123 and Louisiana tax treatment are not reconciled.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Louisiana customer buying from a Florida dealership, review Louisiana OMV Policy 48.00. Louisiana requires DPSMV1799 with Section F Out of State Declaration, original out-of-state title if no lien, or title copy/registration showing lienholder if the title is held by a lienholder. Each owner needs current ID, and if a plate is requested, proof of current liability insurance from a company authorized to do business in Louisiana is required. Louisiana title, handling, plate, mortgage/lien, and road use tax rules may apply. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123 plus Louisiana road use tax/reciprocity before submission. Escalate if DPSMV1799, lienholder title, insurance, military ID, or tax credit handling is unclear.
