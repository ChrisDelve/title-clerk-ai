============================================================
GEORGIA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Georgia DOR says titles and license plates for vehicles titled in another state or country can be obtained at the County Tag Office.

Georgia required items include:

- Completed and signed MV-1 Title/Tag Application
- Original valid title issued in applicant’s name or properly assigned
- Out-of-state/country valid registration certificate, if applicable
- Completed and signed Form T-22B Certification of Inspection
- Georgia liability insurance reported by the insurance company and on record with the Department
- Emission Inspection Certificate, if applicable
- English translation of each document not printed in English
- Copy of Georgia driver’s license or ID
- Payment of all fees and/or taxes due:
  - $20.00 license plate fee
  - $18.00 title fee
  - Title Ad Valorem Tax
  - Any penalties due

Georgia notes:
- If title is held by financial institution or leasing company, County Tag Office Agent processing application will contact the financial institution/leasing company requesting out-of-state title before Georgia title application can be processed.
- If a 1986 or newer vehicle and previous state/country does not issue titles, additional requirements apply, including T-22B by Georgia law enforcement officer and current out-of-state registration.
- Vehicles from Massachusetts, Mississippi, or Minnesota require certification from the appropriate motor vehicle agency indicating vehicle was not titled in that state.
- 1963-1985 model vehicle last registered in state/country that does not issue titles will not be issued a Georgia title.
- Imported vehicle exceptions may require CBP, DOT HS-7, and EPA 3520-1 documents.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR GEORGIA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Georgia customer and the customer wants Georgia title/registration:

1. Prepare ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Georgia-specific items to review:
   - MV-1 Title/Tag Application
   - T-22B Certification of Inspection
   - Georgia liability insurance on record
   - Georgia emissions certificate if applicable
   - Georgia DL/ID copy
   - English translations for non-English documents
   - TAVT and Georgia fees

3. If title held by financial institution/leasing company:
   - Georgia County Tag Office may need to contact lienholder/lessor for title.
   - Do not assume title can process until title request/lienholder path is clear.

4. If state/country does not issue titles:
   - Apply Georgia special non-title-state rules.
   - Escalate if Massachusetts, Mississippi, or Minnesota certification is needed.

5. If imported vehicle:
   - Review CBP, DOT HS-7, EPA 3520-1 requirements.
   - Escalate before submission if federal import documents are missing.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- MV-1 is missing or unsigned.
- T-22B inspection is missing.
- Georgia insurance is not on record.
- Georgia emissions certificate missing when applicable.
- Georgia DL/ID copy missing.
- Title held by lienholder/lease company and Georgia has not received title.
- Non-English documents lack translation.
- Non-title-state vehicle lacks required registration/certification.
- Massachusetts/Mississippi/Minnesota certification required but missing.
- Imported vehicle lacks CBP/DOT/EPA documents.
- Tax/TAVT/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Georgia customer buying from a Florida dealership, Georgia requires a completed signed MV-1, original valid title or properly assigned title, out-of-state registration if applicable, completed T-22B Certification of Inspection, Georgia liability insurance on record, emissions certificate if applicable, English translations if needed, Georgia DL/ID copy, and payment of Georgia fees/taxes including title fee, plate fee, and TAVT. If title is held by a financial institution or leasing company, Georgia County Tag Office will request the title before processing. Prepare the dealer package and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if T-22B, insurance, emissions, title possession, lienholder, non-title-state, import, or tax handling is unclear.
