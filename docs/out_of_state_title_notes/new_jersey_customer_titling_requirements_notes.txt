NEW JERSEY SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

New Jersey MVC says:

- If recently moved to New Jersey, customer must transfer out-of-state license and vehicle title/registration within 60 days of move or before current license/registration expires, whichever occurs first.
- Customer may transfer license and registration in one transaction by appointment.
- For vehicle title/registration transfer:
  - Make New Title or Registration appointment
  - Bring proof of identification, usually valid driver license
  - Original title
  - Insurance information
  - Completed Universal Title Application, Form OS/SS-UTA
  - Completed Vehicle Registration Application, Form BA-49
  - If vehicle is owned by business, apply for Entity Identification Number, EIN / formerly CorpCode
  - If vehicle is financed or leased, send completed Application for Release of Title from Lienholder, Form OS/SS-54
  - Once NJMVC receives title, customer will be notified to return to complete process

New Jersey title transfer fees:
- $60 standard vehicle
- $85 financed vehicle with one lien
- $110 financed vehicle with two liens

New Jersey notes:
- All lienholders and leasing companies must have EIN.
- Titles without liens are given to customer with plates, registration card, and temporary inspection card unless mailing address is PO Box; PO Box title held for 10 days then mailed.
- Titles with liens and leased-vehicle titles are mailed to leasing company or lienholder.
- Customer receives title receipt, vehicle registration, license plates, and temporary inspection card.
- If transferring from state that does not issue titles, use non-title-state guidelines.
- Out-of-country vehicles use foreign vehicle instructions.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NEW JERSEY CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a New Jersey customer and the customer wants New Jersey title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned original title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Lease documents if leased

2. New Jersey-specific items to review:
   - Original title
   - Proof of identification
   - Insurance information
   - OS/SS-UTA Universal Title Application
   - BA-49 Vehicle Registration Application
   - EIN if business-owned
   - OS/SS-54 if financed or leased
   - Lienholder/leasing company EIN if financed or leased
   - Title transfer fee based on lien status

3. If financed or leased:
   - Complete/review OS/SS-54 Application for Release of Title from Lienholder.
   - Confirm lienholder or leasing company EIN.
   - Understand NJMVC may need to receive title before customer completes process.
   - Title will be mailed to lienholder/leasing company; customer receives title receipt, registration, plates, and temporary inspection card.

4. If business-owned:
   - Entity Identification Number required.

5. If title from non-title state or out-of-country:
   - Use New Jersey non-title-state or foreign vehicle instructions.
   - Escalate if ownership proof is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a New Jersey customer buying from a Florida dealership, New Jersey requires proof of identification, original title, insurance information, completed Universal Title Application OS/SS-UTA, and Vehicle Registration Application BA-49. If the vehicle is financed or leased, New Jersey requires OS/SS-54 Application for Release of Title from Lienholder, and lienholders/leasing companies must have an EIN. Business-owned vehicles also require an Entity Identification Number. New Jersey title transfer fees are $60 standard, $85 with one lien, and $110 with two liens. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder/lease information, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if original title, OS/SS forms, EIN, lienholder release, non-title-state, foreign vehicle, or tax handling is unclear.
