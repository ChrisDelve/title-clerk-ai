WISCONSIN SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Wisconsin DOT says:

- When a customer purchases a vehicle, receives it as a gift, or becomes a new Wisconsin resident, they must immediately apply for a new Certificate of Title in their name.
- Certificate of Title proves ownership.
- Customer should never buy a vehicle without receiving a title signed by the previous owner.
- If purchased through a licensed Wisconsin dealer, the dealer submits title and registration paperwork.
- Customer must display Wisconsin license plates or a temporary plate within two business days of purchase to legally operate the vehicle.
- Wisconsin and out-of-state titles can be used to apply online through eMV Public.
- Customer can apply online for both Wisconsin title and new plates.
- Customer can apply online for Wisconsin title and transfer existing plates, but a temporary plate cannot be issued in that workflow.
- Customer can apply online for title only if no plates are needed, but title-only does not allow legal operation on Wisconsin roads and customer is not eligible for temporary plate.

For online title/new plate application, Wisconsin source says customer should have:
- Printer with 8.5 x 11 paper to print temporary plate if applicable and eApplication/Receipt
- Valid email address
- Wisconsin Driver License or ID card, last four digits of SSN, and date of birth
- If non-individual customer, full legal business name, zip code, and FEIN
- Vehicle title signed by seller(s)
- For vehicles model year 2011 and newer, odometer reading from date of sale written by seller on back of title
- Lien release if lienholder listed on front of title and loan is paid off
- Payment information

After online transaction, customer must mail:
- Title and License Plate eApplication/Receipt
- Valid original title signed by seller
- Statement of Intent MV2489 if there are alterations on title
- Valid original lien release if vehicle currently has a lien

For in-person title and plates, Wisconsin source says customer should have:
- Certificate of Title, not photocopy, signed by seller(s)
- Odometer reading completed by seller(s) on back of title for vehicles less than 20 model years old
- Lien release if lienholder listed on front of title and loan is paid off
- Driver license/ID as proof of identity
- Payment information
- Optional pre-filled MV1 Application

For mail-in application, Wisconsin source says:
- Certificate of Title, not photocopy, signed by previous owner
- Odometer reading completed by seller(s) on back of title for vehicles less than 20 model years old
- Statement of Intent MV2489 if alterations are on title

Wisconsin restrictions/warnings:
- Junk vehicles are not eligible for Wisconsin title or registration.
- Salvage vehicles are eligible for title only.
- If VIN error occurs online, customer should mail in application.
- Fees are not refundable after online transaction.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR WISCONSIN CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Wisconsin customer and the customer wants Wisconsin title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Valid original title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Original lien release if prior lien is listed and paid off

2. Wisconsin-specific items to review:
   - eMV Public or MV1 process
   - Valid original title signed by seller
   - Odometer requirement based on model year / 20-model-year rule
   - Wisconsin DL/ID, SSN last four, DOB, or business FEIN if applicable
   - Original lien release if lienholder listed and paid off
   - MV2489 if title has alterations
   - Wisconsin plates or temporary plate within two business days if vehicle will be operated
   - Title-only workflow if vehicle will not be driven

3. If customer wants title and new plates:
   - Wisconsin and out-of-state titles can be used online.
   - Temporary plate may be printable if vehicle type is eligible.
   - eApplication/Receipt and original title documents still must be mailed.

4. If customer wants to transfer Wisconsin plates:
   - Wisconsin online plate transfer may be available.
   - Temporary plate cannot be issued in this workflow.

5. If customer wants title-only:
   - Wisconsin title-only workflow does not issue plates or temp plate.
   - Vehicle cannot be legally operated on Wisconsin roads.

6. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder information.
   - Confirm prior lien release if previous lien is listed and paid off.
   - Confirm lienholder/title instructions.

7. If title has alterations:
   - Complete/review Wisconsin MV2489 Statement of Intent.
   - Escalate if title alteration creates ownership or fraud concern.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Valid original title is missing.
- Seller signature is missing.
- Odometer reading is missing when required.
- Original lien release missing when lienholder is listed and loan is paid off.
- Title has alterations and MV2489 is missing.
- Customer tries to operate vehicle without Wisconsin plates or temporary plate within two business days.
- Customer chooses title-only but expects to drive vehicle.
- Junk vehicle submitted for title/registration.
- Salvage vehicle submitted for registration instead of title-only.
- eApplication/Receipt or original title documents not mailed after online application.
- Wisconsin DL/ID or business FEIN information missing.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Wisconsin customer buying from a Florida dealership, Wisconsin says the customer must immediately apply for a new Certificate of Title and must display Wisconsin plates or a temporary plate within two business days of purchase to legally operate the vehicle. Wisconsin and out-of-state titles can be used through eMV Public. Required items generally include the valid original title signed by seller, Wisconsin DL/ID information, odometer reading when required, lien release if a lienholder is listed and the loan is paid off, and payment. After online submission, the customer must mail the eApplication/Receipt, valid original title, and original lien release if applicable. If the title has alterations, Wisconsin requires MV2489 Statement of Intent. Title-only is available but does not allow legal operation and does not provide a temporary plate. Prepare the dealer package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder documents if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Wisconsin tax handling. Escalate if original title, odometer, lien release, MV2489, title-only vs registration, salvage/junk status, or tax handling is unclear.
