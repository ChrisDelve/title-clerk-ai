NEW YORK SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

New York DMV says:

- A person must register vehicle in New York if they move to New York and become a resident.
- Vehicle must be registered in New York within 30 days of becoming a resident.
- New York resident who gets a vehicle from another state and will drive it on New York roadways must register it in New York within 30 days.
- Nonresident registration from state of residence is valid in New York.
- Customer can apply for New York registration and title certificate by mail from outside New York.

Items to mail to local DMV office:
- Completed Vehicle Registration/Title Application, MV-82
- Proof of ownership; must be original, no photocopies
- Proof of NY State automobile liability insurance
- Proof of identity and age; photocopies should be sent, not original documents
- Payment for sales tax

After processing:
- New York sends registration documents, vehicle plates, and 10-day Inspection Extension Sticker VS-1077.
- Customer puts VS-1077 on vehicle when entering New York, not before, marks date of entry.
- Title certificate is sent separately and may take up to 90 days.
- New vehicle title cannot be mailed to a different address than mailing address on registration application.

Proof of ownership if title is not in customer’s name:
- New vehicle from out-of-state dealer:
  - Manufacturer’s Certificate of Origin
  - Dealer’s bill of sale
- Used vehicle from out-of-state dealer:
  - Out-of-state title certificate or transferable registration transferred to dealer
  - Dealer’s bill of sale transferring ownership to customer
- Private owner:
  - Bill of sale
  - Title certificate or transferable registration transferred to customer

Lienholder/title held by lienholder:
- If lien appears on out-of-state title, NY DMV records lien on New York title record and certificate.
- To remove lien, proof of lien satisfaction required.
- If title is held by lienholder, customer must provide:
  - Copy of title certificate with customer’s name from lienholder
  - Certification from lienholder that title certificate is copy of original certificate; copy and certification must be on same piece of paper
  - Statement on lienholder letterhead with owner, year, make, VIN; statement that lienholder holds original title and knows owner will use documents to register in New York State; no request to notify lienholder when vehicle is registered because NY DMV ignores that request

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NEW YORK CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a New York customer and the customer wants New York title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO for new vehicle, or properly assigned/transferable ownership for used vehicle
   - Dealer bill of sale
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. New York-specific items to review:
   - MV-82 Vehicle Registration/Title Application
   - Original proof of ownership; no photocopies
   - NY State automobile liability insurance
   - Proof of identity and age
   - Sales tax payment
   - Dealer bill of sale
   - MCO for new vehicle from out-of-state dealer
   - Used out-of-state title/transferrable registration transferred to dealer for used vehicle

3. If financed/lienholder title:
   - Provide lienholder title copy with customer’s name.
   - Provide lienholder certification on same page as title copy.
   - Provide lienholder letterhead statement with owner, year, make, VIN, and statement that lienholder holds original title and knows customer will use documents to register in New York.
   - Do not ask NY DMV to notify lienholder when registered.

4. If mailing package:
   - Original proof of ownership must be mailed.
   - Proof of identity/age should be photocopies, not originals.
   - Customer should allow time for registration/plates and up to 90 days for title.
   - Title cannot be mailed to a different address.

5. If registering:
   - New York insurance is required.
   - Inspection extension sticker VS-1077 is issued; customer uses it only when entering New York.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a New York customer buying from a Florida dealership, New York requires MV-82, original proof of ownership, NY State automobile liability insurance, proof of identity and age, and sales-tax payment. For a new vehicle from an out-of-state dealer, provide the MCO and dealer bill of sale. For a used vehicle from an out-of-state dealer, provide the out-of-state title certificate or transferable registration transferred to the dealer plus dealer bill of sale transferring ownership to the customer. If the title is held by a lienholder, New York requires a copy of title with customer’s name, lienholder certification on the same page, and lienholder letterhead statement with owner, year, make, VIN, and acknowledgement that lienholder holds the original title. Prepare the dealer package and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if original ownership proof, NY insurance, lienholder documentation, sales tax, or mailing/title address handling is unclear.
