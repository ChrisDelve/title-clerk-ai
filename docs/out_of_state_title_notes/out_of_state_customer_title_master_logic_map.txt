============================================================
DEALERSHIP OUT-OF-STATE CUSTOMER TITLING WORKFLOW
============================================================

For out-of-state customer titling, the dealership does not simply hand documents to the customer and tell the customer to file.

Dealership operational workflow:

- Dealer prepares the out-of-state title package for the customer’s destination state.
- Dealer collects wet signatures on required documents.
- Dealer collects two notarized Powers of Attorney when required by dealership/title-vendor workflow.
- Dealer collects two odometer disclosure statements when required by dealership/title-vendor workflow.
- For new vehicles, dealer reassigns the Manufacturer Statement of Origin / Manufacturer Certificate of Origin to the buyer.
- Dealer completes buyer information, lienholder information, and odometer section on the MSO/MCO when applicable.
- Dealer may sign title/MSO reassignment documents by Power of Attorney when authorized.
- The person signing by POA must provide their driver license copy when required by dealership/title-vendor workflow.
- Dealer prepares the buyer’s order / bill of sale, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and state-specific title/registration documents.
- Dealer submits the completed package to the vendor/title agency in the customer’s destination state.
- The vendor/title agency handles submission to the customer’s state DMV/county/tag/title office.
- Dealer must not describe the process as only “give the customer paperwork and customer files it” unless that is the actual deal-specific instruction from management/vendor.

============================================================
ANSWER WORDING RULE
============================================================

For out-of-state customer titling answers, do NOT say:

- “The customer is responsible for submitting everything themselves.”
- “The dealership only provides documents to the customer.”
- “The dealership processes a Florida title first.”

Instead say:

- “The dealership prepares the out-of-state title package and submits it to the vendor/title agency for the customer’s destination state.”
- “For a new vehicle, the dealership reassigns the MSO/MCO to the buyer and completes lienholder and odometer information when applicable.”
- “The dealership collects wet signatures, including two notarized POAs and two odometer disclosure statements when required by dealership/vendor workflow.”
- “If signing by POA, the authorized signer signs by POA and provides driver license copy when required.”

Do not overstate state-specific forms as universally required unless the state-specific note clearly confirms them.

For destination-state paperwork, say:
- “state-specific forms required by the vendor/title agency”
- “Georgia/state-specific title and registration forms”
- “forms such as MV-1 or inspection forms if required by the Georgia vendor/county”

Do not say:
- “Georgia requires MV-1 and T-22B in every case”
unless the source note clearly supports that exact requirement.
