============================================================
DELAWARE SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Delaware DMV says for vehicles purchased out of state:

Step 1:
- All seven-model-year or newer vehicles purchased out of state must have VIN verified.
- Present vehicle at any DMV recheck inspection lane.
- New car dealers are authorized to perform this verification on new vehicles and must provide Application for Title MV212.
- All other vehicles are required to be inspected at any DMV Inspection Lane before Delaware title can be issued.

Step 2:
- At the Inspection Lane, customer receives application for Delaware title.
- Required documents include:
  - Certificate of Origin or existing title with odometer disclosure
  - Bill of sale if purchased from dealer
  - Proof of valid Delaware liability insurance
  - Delaware driver license or other accepted proof of residency
  - Signatures of all owners on Delaware application and existing title/certificate of origin
  - Check whether state of purchase requires title-related documents to be notarized

Step 3:
- Delaware title/document fees, document fee, registration fees, and other fees may apply.
- Delaware references a 5.25% document fee based on sale price or current NADA average trade-in value, whichever is higher.
- If out-of-state registration expired, Delaware may issue a 5-day temporary tag with required proof and fee.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR DELAWARE CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Delaware customer and the customer wants Delaware title/registration:

1. Prepare ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Delaware-specific items to review:
   - VIN verification or DMV inspection lane requirement
   - MV212 if new car dealer verification applies
   - Delaware liability insurance if registration requested
   - Delaware driver license or accepted proof of residency
   - All owner signatures
   - Notary requirement if Florida/dealer purchase documents require notarization for Delaware acceptance

3. If vehicle is seven model years or newer:
   - Delaware VIN verification required.
   - Confirm whether dealer verification/MV212 is acceptable or DMV recheck lane is needed.

4. If vehicle is older or not eligible for dealer verification:
   - Delaware may require DMV Inspection Lane before title issuance.

5. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm title/lienholder information required by Delaware.
   - Confirm whether lienholder receives title.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Delaware VIN verification/inspection is missing.
- Vehicle is seven-model-year or newer and VIN verification was not completed.
- Dealer bill of sale is missing.
- Delaware insurance is missing when registration is requested.
- Delaware driver license or residency proof is missing.
- All owners did not sign required documents.
- Notary requirement from purchase state is ignored.
- Tax/document fee handling is unclear.
- Lienholder instructions are unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Delaware customer buying from a Florida dealership, Delaware requires VIN verification for seven-model-year-or-newer out-of-state vehicles and inspection at a Delaware DMV Inspection Lane for other vehicles before Delaware title issuance. Required documents include certificate of origin or existing title with odometer disclosure, dealer bill of sale, Delaware liability insurance if registering, Delaware driver license or accepted residency proof, and signatures of all owners. Check whether title-related documents need notarization. Prepare the dealer package and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if Delaware inspection/VIN verification, insurance, residency proof, signatures, lienholder instructions, or tax handling is unclear.
