============================================================
KANSAS SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Kansas DOR says:

- Application for certificate of title and registration must be made through the county treasurer’s office in the county where the vehicle is garaged.
- To avoid penalty, vehicle registration must be made within 60 days from date of purchase.

Required vehicle information includes:
- Title assigned to new owner
- Back of title completed with purchase price, date of sale, odometer reading, printed names and signatures of seller(s) and buyer(s)
- If more than one owner:
  - AND = all persons listed must sign
  - OR = either person may sign
  - No connection listed defaults to AND/all parties must sign
- Lien release if lienholder recorded on title
- Vehicle inspection required if title was issued from another state or bill of sale is from out-of-state owner
- Vehicle and title must be taken to Kansas motor vehicle inspection station
- MVE-1 issued before application for title/registration
- Pink copy of MVE-1 submitted to treasurer
- Current proof of insurance
- Sales tax receipt if dealer purchase; if tax paid to another state and there is a difference, customer is responsible for difference
- Property tax paid at registration unless applying for temporary plate

Kansas E-title notes:
- Required documents as above
- For 60-day permit with e-title, need TR-39A Electronic Title Sales Agreement, copy of seller’s current registration receipt, and proof of insurance
- Lien release required to get clear paper title
- Seller must provide lien release or clear paper title before registration can be completed
- TR-40 may allow buyer to complete assignment after lien release and clear title received
- TR-128 required if lien not being paid off and lienholder consents to transfer ownership

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR KANSAS CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Kansas customer and the customer wants Kansas title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Kansas-specific items to review:
   - Assigned title with purchase price, date of sale, odometer, printed names and signatures
   - Ownership conjunction AND/OR signature rules
   - Lien release if lienholder recorded
   - Vehicle inspection/MVE-1 if out-of-state title or bill of sale from out-of-state owner
   - Current proof of insurance
   - Sales tax receipt
   - Property tax at registration unless temporary plate
   - 60-day registration timing

3. If title/e-title/lien issue:
   - Review TR-39A for e-title 60-day permit.
   - Review TR-40 for POA/purchase price/odometer disclosure after lien release.
   - Review TR-128 if lienholder consents without payoff.
   - Do not promise registration if lien release or clear paper title is missing.

4. If inspection not completed:
   - Kansas requires vehicle/title inspection and MVE-1 before title/registration.
   - Escalate to agency or Kansas county treasurer.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Kansas customer buying from a Florida dealership, Kansas requires county treasurer title/registration processing in the county where the vehicle is garaged and generally requires registration within 60 days of purchase to avoid penalty. The title assignment must include purchase price, sale date, odometer, printed names, and seller/buyer signatures. If the title is out of state or bill of sale is from an out-of-state owner, Kansas requires vehicle inspection and MVE-1. Current proof of insurance, sales tax receipt, lien release if applicable, and property tax review are also needed. For Kansas e-title issues, review TR-39A, TR-40, or TR-128 as applicable. Escalate if MVE-1, lien release, e-title, tax, insurance, or timing issues are unclear.
