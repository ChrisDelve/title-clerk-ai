============================================================
IDAHO SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Idaho ITD says:

If bought from an Idaho dealer:
- Dealer prepares title application and files it with ITD or county motor vehicle office.
- Dealer must file within 30 days of delivery.

If bought from an out-of-state dealer:
- Dealer may prepare the application for title and file it with ITD.
- If dealer does not, buyer must file required documents with county motor vehicle office within 30 days of transfer to avoid $20 late-filing penalty.

Required titling documents include:
- Existing title with proper signatures releasing interest from previous owner or lienholder, if bought used from private party
- Odometer Disclosure for vehicles newer than 2010, weighing under 16,000 lbs.
- VIN inspection by law enforcement officer, DMV employee, military police officer, or Idaho licensed vehicle dealer if vehicle is coming from another state or has never been titled in Idaho
- Applicable fees
- Optional Title Application — out-of-state dealers, lienholders, and leasing companies may prepare this
- Power of Attorney if applying for title by mail through county assessor’s office
- Bill of Sale may be used when properly released title cannot be obtained, but requires additional steps

Idaho fees listed:
- Duplicate title: $14
- Penalty for late title filing more than 30 days after transfer: $20
- Rush processing optional additional fee: $26
- Release of Liability: $3.50
- Title Stop: $21
- Transitional Ownership Document: $26
- County title/admin fees may vary by county

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR IDAHO CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Idaho customer and the customer wants Idaho title/registration:

1. Determine whether dealership will file Idaho title application:
   - Idaho allows out-of-state dealer to prepare and file application.
   - If not, customer must file with Idaho county motor vehicle office within 30 days.

2. Prepare ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure if vehicle newer than 2010 and under 16,000 lbs.
   - Lienholder information if financed

3. Idaho-specific items:
   - VIN inspection if vehicle is coming from another state or never titled in Idaho
   - Authorized VIN inspector
   - Idaho title application if dealership/lender/lease company prepares it
   - POA if applying by mail through county assessor
   - Conditional title steps if properly released title cannot be obtained

4. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm whether lienholder or out-of-state dealer is preparing title application.
   - Confirm title/lien filing within 30 days.

5. If customer handles filing:
   - Warn customer about Idaho 30-day filing requirement and possible $20 late penalty.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Idaho title application is not filed within 30 days.
- VIN inspection is missing.
- VIN inspection completed by unauthorized party.
- Odometer disclosure is missing for newer-than-2010 / under-16,000-lb vehicle.
- POA is missing for title-by-mail through county assessor.
- Properly released title cannot be obtained and conditional title steps are not followed.
- Lienholder/dealer/customer filing responsibility is unclear.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Idaho customer buying from a Florida dealership, Idaho allows an out-of-state dealer to prepare and file the title application with ITD. If the dealer does not file it, the customer must file required documents with the county motor vehicle office within 30 days of transfer to avoid a $20 late-filing penalty. Idaho requires VIN inspection for vehicles coming from another state or never titled in Idaho, and odometer disclosure for vehicles newer than 2010 weighing under 16,000 lbs. Prepare the dealer package, title application if filing, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if VIN inspection, 30-day filing responsibility, conditional title, lienholder instructions, or tax handling is unclear.
