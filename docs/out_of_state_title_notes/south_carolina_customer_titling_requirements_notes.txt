SOUTH CAROLINA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

South Carolina SCDMV says:

Before title and registration transfer:
- Customer should get an SC driver license.
- Customer must pay vehicle property taxes with county treasurer before registration.
- Customer cannot receive new SC title by mail until out-of-state title clears through NMVTIS.
- Registration may be received before title clearance is complete.

Steps for first-time SC title/registration:
- Update vehicle insurance company with new SC address.
- Insurance company must be licensed to do business in South Carolina.
- Contact county auditor with VIN and most recent registration card.
- County auditor generates property tax bill.
- Property tax bill must be paid to county treasurer before registration.
- Original paid property tax receipt must be brought to SCDMV.

Documents/items listed by SCDMV:
- Completed Title Application, SCDMV Form 400
- Out-of-state vehicle title and/or registration
- If lien exists: lienholder company name and address; valid current registration
- Acceptable identification
- Liability insurance company information
- Power of Attorney if leasing and Form 400 is signed by lessee on behalf of lessor
- Original paid vehicle property tax receipt from county treasurer
- $250 Infrastructure Maintenance Fee (IMF)
- Title and registration fees

Fee note:
- Most regular passenger vehicles titled and registered in SC pay $305 to SCDMV:
  - $250 IMF
  - $15 title fee
  - $40 registration/license plate fee
- This does not include county property tax.
- Registration/plate fee is paid every two years.

No SC license:
- If customer does not have valid SC beginner’s permit, driver license, or ID, complete Statement of Vehicle Operation, Form TI-006.
- TI-006 applies if active duty military in SC, enrolled in SC school, or vehicle kept in SC at least six months a year.

Vehicles with lien:
- Need Form 400
- Form TI-006
- Out-of-state registration
- Original paid property tax receipt
- SC beginner’s permit, driver license, or ID
- Title and registration fees
- If registration is expired, information must be sent to lienholder and lienholder mails it to SCDMV with title.

Vehicles without lien:
- Need Form 400
- Form TI-006
- Out-of-state title
- Original paid property tax receipt
- SC beginner’s permit, driver license, or ID
- Title and registration fees

Registering without title:
- SCDMV may register out-of-state vehicle without title depending on vehicle year and year other state started titling vehicles.
- Original bill of sale may be required.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR SOUTH CAROLINA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a South Carolina customer and the customer wants South Carolina title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. South Carolina-specific items to review:
   - Form 400 Title Application
   - Out-of-state title and/or registration
   - County property tax paid before registration
   - Original paid property tax receipt
   - SC-address insurance with company licensed in SC
   - Liability insurance company information
   - Acceptable ID
   - IMF and title/registration fees
   - TI-006 if customer does not have SC license/permit/ID
   - POA if leased and lessee signs for lessor

3. If financed/lien exists:
   - Provide lienholder name and address.
   - Confirm valid current registration.
   - If registration is expired, review SCDMV lienholder/title-mailing requirement.
   - Do not promise registration completion until lienholder/title route is clear.

4. If no lien:
   - Out-of-state title is required.

5. If customer does not have SC license/permit/ID:
   - Complete/review TI-006 and applicable qualifying reason.

6. If vehicle may be registered without title:
   - Review vehicle year and other state’s title-start year.
   - Original bill of sale may be required.
   - Escalate before submission.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a South Carolina customer buying from a Florida dealership, South Carolina requires Form 400, out-of-state title and/or registration, acceptable ID, liability insurance company information, original paid county property tax receipt, IMF, and title/registration fees. The customer must pay county property tax before registration. Insurance must be updated to the SC address and the insurer must be licensed to do business in South Carolina. If there is a lien, provide lienholder name/address and current registration; if registration is expired, the lienholder/title mailing path must be reviewed. If the customer lacks an SC license, permit, or ID, Form TI-006 may be required. Prepare the dealer package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/SC IMF/property tax handling. Escalate if property tax, insurance, lienholder title, TI-006, lease POA, or tax handling is unclear.
