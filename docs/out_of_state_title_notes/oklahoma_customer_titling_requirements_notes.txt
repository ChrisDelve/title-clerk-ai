============================================================
OKLAHOMA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Service Oklahoma says:

After moving to Oklahoma or purchasing a vehicle from out of state, customer must visit Service Oklahoma on N Classen in Oklahoma City or a Licensed Operator location to process tag, title, and tax to receive vehicle registration.

For out-of-state purchase from private seller or dealer, required documents include:

- Appropriately assigned out-of-state title
- 701-6 Form — Application for Oklahoma Certificate of Title
- Valid driver license
- Proof of Oklahoma insurance
- The vehicle being registered
- Purchase Agreement or 722-1 Form — Declaration of Vehicle Purchase Price

Registration process:
- Gather documents.
- Visit Service Oklahoma or Licensed Operator location.
- Fees are finalized at visit.
- Once payment is made and documents are processed, customer receives standard license plate and decal.
- In case of lien, title is sent to lienholder.
- If no lien, title is mailed to customer within 2 business days as part of registration.
- Specialty plate may be ordered and mailed later.
- Oklahoma recommends making copies because originals may not be returned.

Taxes and fees:
- Fees vary by vehicle type, year, and other variables.
- Taxes owed can be estimated using Sales & Excise Tax Calculator.
- Estimate is based on NADA listed pricing and may vary.
- Estimate does not include other fees due at registration.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR OKLAHOMA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Oklahoma customer and the customer wants Oklahoma title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Appropriately assigned out-of-state title or MCO/MSO as applicable
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Purchase Agreement / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Oklahoma-specific items to review:
   - 701-6 Application for Oklahoma Certificate of Title
   - Valid driver license
   - Proof of Oklahoma insurance
   - Vehicle must be presented/registered
   - Purchase Agreement or 722-1 Declaration of Vehicle Purchase Price
   - Visit Service Oklahoma or Licensed Operator
   - Copies of originals because originals may not be returned

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder information.
   - Oklahoma sends title to lienholder if lien exists.
   - Confirm lender/title mailing instructions.

4. If no lien:
   - Oklahoma mails title to customer within 2 business days after registration.

5. If tax/fee question:
   - Fees finalized at visit.
   - Oklahoma Sales & Excise Tax Calculator is estimate only.
   - Review Florida DR-123/tax handling and Oklahoma tax/excise exposure.

============================================================
TAX / RECIPROCITY NOTES
============================================================

For Florida dealer sale to Oklahoma resident:

- Review Florida DOR out-of-state resident sales tax / DR-123 workflow.
- Service Oklahoma says taxes may be estimated with Sales & Excise Tax Calculator, but final amount may vary and does not include all fees due at registration.
- Florida DOR TIP has special Oklahoma language that should be reviewed because Oklahoma may require tax/excise tax at title/registration and may allow refund up to Florida tax paid in certain situations.
- Verify tax handling with controller, agency, lender, or Service Oklahoma before finalizing.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Out-of-state title is not appropriately assigned.
- Form 701-6 is missing.
- Valid driver license is missing.
- Oklahoma insurance is missing.
- Vehicle being registered is not available when required.
- Purchase Agreement or 722-1 is missing.
- Originals are sent without copies and are not returned.
- Lienholder information is missing.
- Customer expects title but lien exists and title is sent to lienholder.
- Tax/excise estimate is treated as final.
- Florida DR-123 and Oklahoma tax/refund handling unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Oklahoma customer buying from a Florida dealership, Service Oklahoma requires an appropriately assigned out-of-state title, Form 701-6 Application for Oklahoma Certificate of Title, valid driver license, proof of Oklahoma insurance, the vehicle being registered, and either a purchase agreement or Form 722-1 Declaration of Vehicle Purchase Price. The customer must visit Service Oklahoma or a Licensed Operator location, where fees are finalized and plates/decals are issued after processing. If there is a lien, Oklahoma sends the title to the lienholder; if there is no lien, title is mailed to the customer within 2 business days as part of registration. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123 plus Oklahoma tax/excise handling. Escalate if title assignment, Oklahoma insurance, 701-6, 722-1, lienholder, vehicle presentation, or tax handling is unclear.
