============================================================
COLORADO SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Colorado DMV search result states:
- If a vehicle was titled in another state, Colorado requires Verification of Vehicle Identification Number form DR 2698.

Colorado DR 2698 form includes:
- Reason for inspection: Out of State
- Vehicle Identification Number verification
- Colorado Dealer Statement Concerning an Out of State Vehicle
- Dealer certification that vehicle was free and clear of liens/encumbrances, not stolen, dealership has sure and adequate title, and dealership has authority to sell/transfer
- Verification may be completed by authorized parties listed on the form, including:
  - Licensed Colorado dealer
  - Colorado law enforcement
  - Licensed Colorado emission station
  - County clerk or assessor at their discretion
  - Other authorized personnel as listed on form

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR COLORADO CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Colorado customer and the customer wants Colorado title/registration:

1. Prepare ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Colorado-specific requirements to review:
   - Colorado DR 2698 VIN Verification for out-of-state title
   - Authorized VIN verifier requirements
   - Colorado dealer statement if applicable
   - Colorado insurance/registration requirements if registering

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lender/lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm lienholder/title mailing requirements.
   - Confirm whether Colorado county office or agency needs lien documentation.

4. If customer cannot complete Colorado VIN verification:
   - Do not assume Colorado will waive DR 2698.
   - Escalate to title lead, agency, or Colorado county motor vehicle office.

5. If registering in Colorado:
   - Verify Colorado insurance requirements.
   - Out-of-state insurance may not be enough unless accepted under Colorado law.
   - Confirm with Colorado county office if unclear.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- VIN verification completed by unauthorized party.
- Colorado dealer statement is required but incomplete.
- Lienholder/title mailing instructions are unclear.
- Colorado insurance/registration requirements are unclear.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.
- Ownership package lacks proper assignment or odometer disclosure/statement if applicable.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Colorado customer buying from a Florida dealership, Colorado generally requires VIN verification on DR 2698 when the vehicle was titled in another state. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder documents if financed, and review whether DR 2698 must be completed by an authorized verifier. If registration is involved, verify Colorado insurance/registration requirements. Escalate if DR 2698, lienholder/title mailing, Colorado county requirements, or tax handling is unclear.
