WEST VIRGINIA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

West Virginia DMV says:

Used vehicle purchases:
- If vehicle purchased is used, previous owner's title must be properly reassigned to buyer, whether West Virginia title or out-of-state title.
- For West Virginia title, both seller and buyer must sign the back of the title.
- For out-of-state title, previous owner must sign the title and name/address of new owner should appear on title.
- New owner submits out-of-state title with Application for Certificate of Title for a Motor Vehicle, DMV-1-TR.
- See Odometer Statement section for mileage disclosure requirements based on vehicle class.

Used vehicle fees:
- $15 title fee
- 6% titling sales tax on vehicles with net purchase price over $500
- $30 tax on vehicles with net purchase price under $500
- $10 lien fee if applicable
- Registration fee and/or registration transfer fee

Tax:
- If trade-in is involved, 6% titling sales tax is based on purchase price minus trade-in.
- Trade-in must be titled in West Virginia and in applicant's name to receive credit.
- Rebates are taxable.
- West Virginia does not charge nonresidents the 6% tax since the vehicle is not being titled in West Virginia.
- Some states collect taxes on nonresident vehicle purchases.
- West Virginia says customer does not get credit for taxes paid to another state.

Bill of sale / purchase price:
- If purchased from individual and purchase price is below 50% of current NADA Clean Loan Book value, notarized bill of sale must accompany title application or tax assessed on NADA value.
- If purchased from individual and purchase price is above 50%, tax assessed on that price.
- Vehicle not purchased from registered dealer and two years old or less taxed on NADA Clean Loan Book value despite purchase price; notarized bill of sale not accepted.
- Titles submitted listing purchase price and accompanied by bill of sale showing different price will not be accepted.
- If price conflict, original owner must apply for duplicate title and begin process over; if duplicate unavailable, tax assessed on book value.
- All notarized bills of sale or DMV-7-TR must be accompanied by copies of both buyer and seller driver licenses.

Titles and liens:
- Vehicles without liens may be titled and registered by mail or in person at DMV regional office or privately-owned license service.
- If title has lien and final payment is made, lienholder sends title to vehicle owner with lien released.
- DMV is not notified when lien is released; lien remains on file unless vehicle owner applies for clear title.
- If title lost and lien still on record, lien release by original lienholder required before clear duplicate title can be issued.

Odometer:
- When motor vehicle is sold/transferred, odometer reading must be recorded in assignment section of title by seller.
- If title lacks odometer section, seller may record mileage on DMV-TM-1.
- Odometer disclosure is mandatory at time of ownership transfer for listed classes.
- WV notes federal odometer disclosure required for 2011 and newer vehicles for 20 years from manufacture.
- WV says it does not recognize age as an exemption; exemption applies only to appropriate vehicle class.
- Required odometer statuses include Actual Mileage, Not Actual Mileage, Mileage Exceeds Mechanical Limits, or Exempt only if applicable category.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR WEST VIRGINIA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a West Virginia customer and the customer wants West Virginia title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Properly assigned title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Purchase price documentation

2. West Virginia-specific items to review:
   - DMV-1-TR Application for Certificate of Title
   - Out-of-state title properly reassigned
   - Previous owner signature and new owner name/address on out-of-state title
   - Odometer disclosure based on WV class rules
   - $15 title fee
   - 6% titling sales tax or $30 minimum tax rule
   - $10 lien fee if applicable
   - Registration/registration transfer fees
   - No credit for tax paid to another state
   - Trade credit only if trade titled in WV and applicant’s name
   - Purchase price/title/bill-of-sale consistency

3. If financed/lien exists:
   - Confirm lienholder information.
   - Confirm lien fee.
   - Confirm lien release if prior lien is shown.
   - If lien released but WV record not updated, clear title application may be needed.

4. If bill of sale used:
   - Ensure purchase price does not conflict with title.
   - If notarized bill of sale or DMV-7-TR is used, include buyer and seller driver license copies.
   - Escalate if price is below 50% NADA or title/bill of sale conflict.

5. If tax question:
   - West Virginia does not credit taxes paid to another state.
   - Review Florida DR-123/nonresident tax workflow carefully before finalizing.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a West Virginia customer buying from a Florida dealership, West Virginia requires the out-of-state title to be properly reassigned, with the previous owner signing the title and the new owner’s name/address appearing on the title. The customer submits the out-of-state title with DMV-1-TR. Fees include a title fee, 6% titling sales tax on vehicles over $500 net purchase price or a $30 tax under $500, lien fee if applicable, and registration/transfer fees. West Virginia does not credit taxes paid to another state, so Florida DR-123/WV tax handling must be reviewed carefully. Odometer disclosure is required based on West Virginia class rules, and WV says age alone is not recognized as an odometer exemption. If title price and bill of sale price conflict, WV says the title application will not be accepted. Prepare the dealer package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and escalate if title assignment, price conflict, lien release, odometer, or tax handling is unclear.
