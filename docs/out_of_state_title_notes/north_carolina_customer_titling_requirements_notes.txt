NORTH CAROLINA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

North Carolina NCDMV title requirements page says:

General requirements:
- The individual titling the vehicle must have a valid North Carolina driver license or ID.
- Some exceptions for non-North Carolina residents apply.
- For new North Carolina residents, a valid out-of-state driver license and North Carolina Temporary Driving Certificate are required.
- For vehicles owned by more than one individual, all signatures on required documents must be notarized and accompanied by a black-and-white copy of alternate owner’s valid driver license or ID.
- Alternatively, Power of Attorney can sign for co-owners; if paperwork is signed by POA, black-and-white copy of owner’s valid driver license is required.
- If mailing paperwork, individual titling the vehicle must provide black-and-white copy of valid North Carolina driver license.
- Property taxes for vehicle being registered must be paid.

Special requirements:
- Additional requirements may apply for other vehicle types such as antiques, replicas, mobile homes, mopeds, motorcycles, trailers.
- Special cases include leased, salvaged, abandoned vehicles, owner without proof of ownership, or vehicle in a trust.

New and non-North Carolina residents:
- New residents or nonresidents who recently acquired a vehicle must get the vehicle titled in North Carolina before registering it.
- In addition to driver license, non-North Carolina residents must provide one of:
  - Military ID for active-duty military stationed in North Carolina
  - Student ID showing enrollment in North Carolina college or university
  - Form MVR-614 documenting vehicle is garaged in North Carolina for at least six months each year
  - Court-ordered sale of vehicle
  - Registration for a motor home

Proof of liability insurance:
- North Carolina law requires continuous liability insurance provided by a North Carolina-licensed provider on all vehicles titled and registered in North Carolina.
- Insurance is required prior to titling a vehicle.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NORTH CAROLINA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a North Carolina customer and the customer wants North Carolina title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. North Carolina-specific items to review:
   - Valid North Carolina driver license or ID, unless new/nonresident exception applies
   - For new resident: valid out-of-state driver license and North Carolina Temporary Driving Certificate
   - North Carolina-licensed provider liability insurance before titling
   - Property taxes for registration
   - Notarized signatures for multiple owners
   - Black-and-white ID copies when required
   - MVR-614 or other qualifying nonresident proof if nonresident
   - Title-before-registration rule

3. If multiple owners:
   - Ensure required signatures are notarized.
   - Include black-and-white copy of alternate owner’s valid DL/ID.
   - If POA signs, include black-and-white copy of owner’s valid DL.

4. If customer is nonresident:
   - Confirm qualifying exception: military, student, MVR-614, court-ordered sale, or motor home registration.
   - Escalate if no qualifying proof.

5. If registering:
   - Verify NC-licensed provider liability insurance.
   - Verify property tax requirement.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a North Carolina customer buying from a Florida dealership, North Carolina generally requires the person titling the vehicle to have a valid North Carolina driver license or ID. New North Carolina residents need a valid out-of-state driver license and a North Carolina Temporary Driving Certificate. Nonresidents must qualify under a listed exception such as active-duty military ID, student ID, MVR-614 showing the vehicle is garaged in North Carolina at least six months each year, court-ordered sale, or motor home registration. North Carolina requires liability insurance from a North Carolina-licensed provider before titling. Multiple-owner documents require notarized signatures and black-and-white ID copies. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if NC ID, insurance, nonresident proof, notarization, property tax, or tax handling is unclear.
