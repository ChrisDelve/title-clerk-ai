============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This file helps a Florida dealership determine Florida sales tax treatment when selling a new or used motor vehicle in Florida to a purchaser who is a resident of another state.

Use this file for questions like:

- How much Florida sales tax do we collect from an out-of-state customer?
- Does this out-of-state customer need DR-123?
- What tax rate do we charge for a customer from Alabama?
- Does Florida cap nonresident vehicle sales tax at 6%?
- Which states do not allow credit for Florida tax?
- Does Arkansas get taxed twice?
- Does Mississippi get taxed twice?
- Does West Virginia get taxed twice?
- How does Oklahoma work?
- Does the customer’s state allow trade-in credit?
- Does the customer’s state have no sales tax?
- What happens if the customer titles the vehicle in their home state within 45 days?

============================================================
SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Florida law allows a partial sales tax exemption on the sale of a new or used motor vehicle in Florida to a resident of another state.

The amount of Florida sales tax collected from the nonresident purchaser is the amount of sales tax that would be imposed by the purchaser’s home state if the vehicle were purchased in that state.

If the purchaser’s home-state sales tax rate is greater than or equal to Florida’s 6% state sales tax rate, Florida collects 6%.

If the purchaser’s home-state sales tax rate is less than Florida’s 6% state sales tax rate, the purchaser may claim a partial exemption from Florida sales tax.

For the partial exemption to apply, the nonresident purchaser must complete Form DR-123, Affidavit for Partial Exemption of Motor Vehicle Sold to a Resident of Another State.

Form DR-123 declares the purchaser’s intent to license the vehicle in the purchaser’s home state within 45 days of the date of purchase.

Form DR-123 must be completed at the time of sale and provided to the selling dealer.

If the nonresident purchaser licenses the vehicle in their home state within 45 days from the date of purchase, there is no requirement that the motor vehicle be removed from Florida.

The tax collected is Florida sales tax and must be paid to the Florida Department of Revenue.

============================================================
NONRESIDENT ENTITY / CORPORATION / PARTNERSHIP RULE
============================================================

The partial exemption for a motor vehicle sold in Florida to a nonresident purchaser does not apply to a nonresident corporation or partnership when:

- An officer of the corporation is a Florida resident
- A stockholder who owns at least 10% of the corporation is a Florida resident
- A partner who has at least 10% ownership in the partnership is a Florida resident

However, the partial exemption may be allowed for corporations or partnerships if the vehicle is removed from Florida within 45 days after purchase and remains outside Florida for a minimum of 180 days, regardless of residency of owners or stockholders.

IF purchaser_is_nonresident_corporation_or_partnership = TRUE
AND officer_stockholder_10_percent_or_partner_10_percent_is_Florida_resident = TRUE
THEN DR_123_partial_exemption_not_allowed_by_default = TRUE

IF purchaser_is_nonresident_corporation_or_partnership = TRUE
AND Florida_resident_owner_or_officer_issue_exists = TRUE
AND vehicle_removed_from_Florida_within_45_days = TRUE
AND vehicle_remains_outside_Florida_for_180_days = TRUE
THEN partial_exemption_may_be_allowed_review_required = TRUE

============================================================
SPECIAL DOUBLE-TAX / NO-CREDIT WARNING STATES
============================================================

Florida DOR specifically warns:

Arkansas, Mississippi, and West Virginia impose a sales tax on motor vehicles but do not allow a credit for taxes paid to Florida.

Oklahoma requires residents to pay sales tax and excise tax on a motor vehicle purchased in Florida when titling or registering the vehicle in Oklahoma, but Oklahoma will allow a refund in the amount of sales tax paid to Florida, up to the amount of sales tax paid in Oklahoma.

Residents of Arkansas, Mississippi, West Virginia, and Oklahoma should be informed that they must pay sales tax to Florida at the rate imposed by their home state when they purchase a vehicle in Florida and will also be required to pay tax to their home state when the vehicle is licensed in their home state.

============================================================
FLORIDA USE TAX FOR VEHICLES PURCHASED OUTSIDE FLORIDA
============================================================

Florida’s use tax rate of 6% generally applies to motor vehicles purchased outside Florida and later titled, registered, or licensed in Florida.

Florida allows credit to reduce Florida use tax when a like tax was lawfully imposed and paid in another state, the District of Columbia, or a U.S. territory.

Credit can apply to tax paid to another state, county, or city.

If the amount paid to the other jurisdiction is equal to or greater than the Florida tax due, no additional Florida sales tax is due.

If the amount paid to the other jurisdiction is less than the Florida tax due, only the difference is due.

A vehicle used in another state, District of Columbia, or U.S. territory for six months or longer before being brought into Florida is presumed to have been purchased for use outside Florida. If the owner provides documentation showing use outside Florida for six months or longer before Florida registration, no Florida use tax is due.

GENERAL ANSWER TEMPLATE
============================================================

For a Florida dealer sale to an out-of-state resident, Florida collects the amount of sales tax that would be imposed by the purchaser’s home state, capped at Florida’s 6% state sales tax rate. If the purchaser’s home-state rate is below 6%, Form DR-123 is required at the time of sale for the partial exemption. The customer must declare intent to license the vehicle in their home state within 45 days. If licensed in the home state within 45 days, the vehicle does not have to be removed from Florida. Always verify special state rules, trade-in treatment, no-credit states, and tax/refund exposure before finalizing.

============================================================
NO-CREDIT / DOUBLE-TAX ANSWER TEMPLATE
============================================================

Florida DOR specifically warns that Arkansas, Mississippi, and West Virginia impose motor vehicle tax but do not allow credit for taxes paid to Florida. Customers from those states should be warned that they may pay Florida tax at the home-state rate when purchasing in Florida and may also owe tax again when licensing the vehicle in their home state. Oklahoma is also special: Oklahoma requires sales tax and excise tax at title/registration but may allow a refund up to the Florida sales tax paid.
