CALIFORNIA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

California DMV says buying a vehicle from a dealership often means at least some registration process may be handled by the dealer.

Many dealers are authorized to:
- Transfer titles
- Issue temporary registration to use until permanent registration and stickers arrive by mail

If the dealer does not provide registration services:
- Customer must register the vehicle themselves.

California DMV says to register a vehicle/vessel purchased from a dealer, customer will always need:

- If vehicle is new: completed Application for Title or Registration, REG 343
- If vehicle is used: California Certificate of Title, or out-of-state title if currently registered in another state
- If title is lost or damaged: Application for Replacement or Transfer of Title, REG 227, to request a copy
- Signature(s) of seller(s) and/or lienholder on Certificate of Title
- Buyer/customer signature on Certificate of Title
- Applicable title transfer fees or use tax

California DMV page also lists items under “You may also need.” For dealership workflow purposes, review these as required review items, except REG 4008 commercial GVW/CGW, which is conditional for commercial vehicles only:

Required review items:
- Vehicle/Vessel Transfer and Reassignment, REG 262
  - REG 262 cannot be downloaded because it contains security features
  - Customer must visit DMV field office in person to get the form
- Statement of Facts, REG 256
- Smog certification
- Vehicle Emission System Statement, REG 139
- Use tax and/or other fees

Conditional commercial-only item:
- Declaration of Gross Vehicle Weight / Combined Gross Vehicle Weight, REG 4008, only if purchasing a commercial vehicle

California DMV says to complete registration, customer should visit a DMV field office in person.

Smog FAQ:
- If gas-powered vehicle is less than 4 years old, smog certification is not needed, but smog abatement fee is due.
- If gas-powered vehicle is more than 4 years old, dealer must get smog certification before selling, unless vehicle was smog certified within last 90 days.
- Smog inspections are not required for:
  - Diesel-powered vehicles weighing more than 14,000 pounds
  - Electric-powered vehicles
  - Natural gas-powered vehicles
  - Motorcycles
  - Trailers
  - Special equipment, such as construction or farm vehicles
  - Off-highway vehicles

Fees FAQ:
- DMV calculates fees when application is submitted.
- Fees may include title transfer fees.
- Use tax is 7.25% to 10.25% based on new registered owner’s city and county of residence.
- Transfer fees must be paid within 10 days of sale.
- If missed, late penalty is charged after 30 days.
- If registering more than one vehicle/vessel, separate transfer fee required for each vehicle/vessel.

California dealer/consumer notes:
- California dealers must show customer a National Motor Vehicle Title Information System, NMVTIS, report.
- Customer should review purchase contract, warranty, and safety recalls.
- If co-owners are joined by “or,” one owner can sell without other owner’s consent.
- If co-owners are joined by slash “/” or “and,” both owner signatures are required.
- California DMV notes there are restrictions on buying a vehicle/vessel from another state.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR CALIFORNIA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a California customer and the customer wants California title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for new vehicle
   - California Certificate of Title or out-of-state title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Seller/lienholder signatures on title
   - Buyer signature on title

2. California-specific items to review:
   - REG 343 for new vehicle
   - REG 227 if title is lost or damaged
   - REG 262 Vehicle/Vessel Transfer and Reassignment
   - REG 256 Statement of Facts
   - Smog certification
   - REG 139 Vehicle Emission System Statement
   - Use tax and other fees
   - REG 4008 only if commercial vehicle
   - DMV field office visit if customer must complete registration themselves

3. If used vehicle:
   - Confirm out-of-state title if vehicle is currently registered in another state.
   - Confirm all seller/lienholder and buyer signatures.
   - Confirm whether REG 262 is needed.
   - Confirm odometer disclosure/statement if applicable.

4. If new vehicle:
   - Complete/review REG 343.
   - Include MCO/MSO and dealer sales documents.
   - Confirm use tax and fees.

5. If smog applies:
   - For gas-powered vehicles more than 4 years old, confirm dealer smog certification before sale unless certified within last 90 days.
   - For gas-powered vehicles less than 4 years old, review smog abatement fee.
   - Confirm exemptions for electric, natural gas, motorcycles, trailers, special equipment, off-highway vehicles, and qualifying diesel vehicles over 14,000 pounds.

6. If commercial vehicle:
   - Review REG 4008 Declaration of Gross Vehicle Weight / Combined Gross Vehicle Weight.
   - Do not request REG 4008 as default for non-commercial vehicles.

7. If California registration is not handled by dealer:
   - Customer must register vehicle themselves.
   - California DMV source says to complete registration, customer should visit a DMV field office in person.

8. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder signature and lienholder/title instructions.
   - Confirm whether lienholder information is required on California title/registration forms.

TAX / RECIPROCITY NOTES
============================================================

For Florida dealer sale to California resident:

- Review Florida DOR out-of-state resident sales tax / DR-123 workflow.
- California DMV says use tax may range from 7.25% to 10.25% based on the new registered owner’s city and county of residence.
- Transfer fees must be paid within 10 days of sale; late penalty applies after 30 days if missed.
- Verify tax handling with controller, agency, lender, California DMV, or California registration service before finalizing.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a California customer buying from a Florida dealership, California requires REG 343 if the vehicle is new, or the California Certificate of Title/out-of-state title if the vehicle is used and currently registered in another state. The title must have required seller/lienholder signatures and buyer signature. If the title is lost or damaged, REG 227 is needed. For dealership workflow, also review REG 262, REG 256, smog certification, REG 139, use tax, and other fees. REG 4008 is only required for commercial vehicles, not as a default for non-commercial vehicles. California DMV says use tax can range from 7.25% to 10.25% based on the new registered owner’s city/county, and transfer fees must be paid within 10 days of sale, with late penalty after 30 days. If the dealer does not handle California registration, the customer must complete registration themselves and should visit a DMV field office. Prepare the dealer package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/California tax handling. Escalate if title signatures, REG forms, smog, use tax, field office completion, commercial GVW, lienholder, or tax handling is unclear.
