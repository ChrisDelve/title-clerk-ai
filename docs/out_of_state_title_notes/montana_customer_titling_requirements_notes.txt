============================================================
MONTANA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Gallatin County, Montana says:

For all title and registration transactions, customer should bring:

- Full VIN of vehicle being titled/registered
- Montana driver license or ID
- If multiple people are on the title, picture of their driver license is needed
- Physical original title
- Montana source says it does not accept copies, memorandum titles, electronic titles, or a copy of a registration to transfer ownership

If customer does not have Montana driver license:
- Valid government-issued ID is required
  - Examples include out-of-state driver license, passport, passport card, foreign driver license
- Proof of residency is required

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MONTANA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Montana customer and the customer wants Montana title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Physical original MCO/MSO or properly assigned physical original title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Full VIN

2. Montana-specific items to review:
   - Physical original title is required for ownership transfer.
   - Montana source says copies, memorandum titles, electronic titles, and registration copies are not accepted to transfer ownership.
   - Montana driver license/ID is needed.
   - If customer lacks Montana driver license, valid government-issued ID plus proof of residency is required.
   - If multiple owners, picture of each driver license may be needed.

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title handling.
   - Because Montana source says physical original title is required, do not assume electronic title/copy/memo title will work.
   - Escalate if lender, ELT, or title possession conflicts with Montana original-title requirement.

4. If customer only has copy/electronic title/memo title:
   - Hold and escalate before promising Montana title/registration.
   - Obtain physical original title path or agency confirmation.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Physical original title is missing.
- Dealer only has copy, memo title, electronic title, or registration copy.
- Full VIN is missing.
- Montana driver license/ID missing.
- Multiple owners and ID copies/pictures missing.
- Customer lacks Montana driver license and proof of residency is missing.
- Lienholder/title possession conflicts with original title requirement.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Montana customer buying from a Florida dealership, Montana/Gallatin County guidance says all title and registration transactions need the full VIN, Montana driver license or ID, and the physical original title. The source specifically says copies, memorandum titles, electronic titles, or registration copies are not accepted to transfer ownership. If the customer does not have a Montana driver license, they need valid government-issued ID and proof of residency. Prepare the dealer ownership package, physical original title or MCO/MSO, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if the title is electronic, held by lienholder, only a copy/memo title is available, or residency/ID/tax handling is unclear.
