============================================================
ALASKA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Alaska DMV says that when a person recently moved to Alaska or is bringing a vehicle from another state, they need to transfer the out-of-state title and register the vehicle in Alaska.

Alaska requires:

- Most current out-of-state title
- Form V1 — Vehicle Transaction Application
- Payment of fees

Alaska notes:
- If the vehicle is already titled in the customer’s name, the customer does not need to sign the title before transfer.
- If the vehicle was recently purchased, the title must be properly assigned and signed over by the previous owner.
- If there is an unreleased lien, the customer may only be eligible for registration only.
- If the vehicle has a lien, the title will be mailed directly to the lienholder after processing.
- Lien release is required once the loan is paid off.
- Alaska title fee listed by source: $15
- Alaska lienholder recording fee if recording a lien for the first time: $15
- Registration and plate fees vary by vehicle type.

ALASKA FORM V1 NOTES
============================================================

Alaska Form V1 is the Vehicle Transaction Application.

Alaska source states:
- Form V1 is used for both titling and registration.
- All applicable sections should be filled out.
- It must be signed in ink.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR ALASKA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Alaska customer and the customer wants Alaska title/registration:

1. Prepare ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Determine whether:
   - Vehicle is being titled to Alaska customer for first time.
   - Vehicle is financed with lienholder.
   - Alaska will issue title to lienholder.
   - Customer may only be eligible for registration only due to unreleased lien.

3. Include Alaska-specific form:
   - Alaska Form V1 Vehicle Transaction Application
   - Ensure applicable sections are completed and signed in ink.

4. Fees:
   - Alaska source lists title fee as $15.
   - Alaska source lists first-time lienholder recording fee as $15.
   - Registration and plate fees vary by vehicle type.

5. If financed:
   - Confirm lender/lienholder instructions.
   - Confirm where Alaska title should be mailed.
   - Review if lienholder recording fee applies.

6. If unreleased lien exists:
   - Do not promise clean Alaska title without lienholder/lien review.
   - Escalate to title lead, lender, agency, or Alaska DMV.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Most current out-of-state title is missing.
- Title is not properly assigned for a recently purchased vehicle.
- Alaska Form V1 is missing, incomplete, or not signed in ink.
- Unreleased lien exists and title eligibility is unclear.
- Lienholder recording information is missing.
- Lender instructions conflict with Alaska DMV requirements.
- Registration-only issue is misunderstood as clean title eligibility.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Alaska customer buying from a Florida dealership, Alaska requires the most current out-of-state title, Form V1 Vehicle Transaction Application, and applicable fees. If the vehicle was recently purchased, the title must be properly assigned and signed over by the previous owner. If there is an unreleased lien, Alaska says the customer may only be eligible for registration only. If there is a lien, the Alaska title may be mailed directly to the lienholder after processing. Prepare the dealer title package, completed Alaska Form V1, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, and lienholder information if financed. Escalate if lien status, registration-only eligibility, Form V1, or tax handling is unclear.
