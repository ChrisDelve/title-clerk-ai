HAWAIʻI COUNTY SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Hawaiʻi County Vehicle Registration & Licensing states that the following are required to switch from out-of-state license plates to Hawaiʻi license plates and obtain Hawaiʻi County registration:

- Submit the last issued out-of-state certificate of registration.
  - It cannot be laminated.
  - It cannot be a photocopy.

- Submit the last issued out-of-state certificate of title if customer is listed as the lienholder.
  - It cannot be laminated.
  - It cannot be a photocopy.

- Submit a bill of lading or shipping receipt showing the date the vehicle arrived in Hawaiʻi County.

- Submit a current Hawaiʻi certificate of safety inspection.
  - A temporary safety inspection failed for “Registration” is acceptable to register the vehicle.

- If vehicle’s model year is previous, current, or subsequent year, submit State of Hawaiʻi Form G-27 with any required attachments.
  - Form G-27 may be obtained from the State of Hawaiʻi Department of Taxation.
  - Questions regarding Form G-27 should be directed to the Hawaiʻi District Tax Office at 808-974-6321.

- If the vehicle weight as established by the manufacturer is not available, vehicle may be required to be weighed at a state-certified scale.
  - A weight slip is required for vehicle models 2016 and newer.

- Registration personnel determine required fees.

- All vehicles applying for registration in the County of Hawaiʻi must comply with all applicable Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards, FMVSS, as of the date of manufacture as required by Hawaiʻi Revised Statutes 286-42.

- Non-compliant vehicles may not be registered in Hawaiʻi County, including:
  - Off-road motorcycles
  - Off-road motor vehicles, including ATVs
  - Military vehicles
  - Imported vehicles
  - Kit cars
  - Modified vehicles

- Motor Vehicle Control Inspectors may be contacted at 808-640-7339 or 808-640-7342 for more information or assistance.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR HAWAIʻI CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Hawaiʻi customer and the customer wants Hawaiʻi title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for new vehicle or properly assigned title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed
   - Original registration/title documents if applicable

2. Hawaiʻi County-specific items to review:
   - Last issued out-of-state certificate of registration
   - Last issued out-of-state certificate of title if customer is listed as lienholder
   - Bill of lading or shipping receipt showing arrival date in Hawaiʻi County
   - Current Hawaiʻi certificate of safety inspection
   - Temporary failed safety inspection for “Registration” if applicable
   - Form G-27 if vehicle model year is previous, current, or subsequent year
   - Weight slip if model year 2016 or newer, or if manufacturer weight is unavailable
   - FMVSS compliance

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title instructions.
   - Confirm whether lienholder documents are needed.
   - Confirm title mailing/lien recording path.

4. If vehicle is being shipped:
   - Make sure the customer gets a bill of lading or shipping receipt.
   - Confirm it shows the date the vehicle arrived in Hawaiʻi County.

5. If registration is being processed:
   - Do not use laminated or photocopied out-of-state registration/title documents where originals are required.
   - Confirm Hawaiʻi safety inspection.
   - Confirm fees will be determined by registration personnel.

6. If vehicle is imported, modified, kit, off-road, ATV, military, or potentially non-FMVSS compliant:
   - Hold and escalate.
   - Contact Hawaiʻi County Motor Vehicle Control Inspectors before promising registration.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Last issued out-of-state certificate of registration is missing.
- Out-of-state registration is laminated or a photocopy.
- Last issued out-of-state title is required but missing.
- Out-of-state title is laminated or a photocopy.
- Bill of lading or shipping receipt is missing.
- Bill of lading or shipping receipt does not show arrival date in Hawaiʻi County.
- Current Hawaiʻi safety inspection is missing.
- G-27 is required but missing.
- Required G-27 attachments are missing.
- Weight slip is required but missing.
- Vehicle is 2016 or newer and weight slip is missing.
- FMVSS compliance is unclear.
- Vehicle is off-road, ATV, military, imported, kit, modified, or otherwise non-compliant.
- Fees are assumed instead of determined by registration personnel.
- Tax/DR-123/G-27 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Hawaiʻi customer buying from a Florida dealership, Hawaiʻi County requires the last issued out-of-state certificate of registration, not laminated and not a photocopy, plus the last issued out-of-state certificate of title if the customer is listed as the lienholder. The customer also needs a bill of lading or shipping receipt showing the date the vehicle arrived in Hawaiʻi County and a current Hawaiʻi certificate of safety inspection. A temporary safety inspection failed for “Registration” is acceptable for registration. If the vehicle model year is the previous, current, or subsequent year, Hawaiʻi Form G-27 with required attachments may be required. A weight slip is required for 2016-and-newer vehicles, or if manufacturer weight is unavailable. Hawaiʻi County also requires FMVSS compliance and may not register non-compliant vehicles such as off-road motorcycles, ATVs, military vehicles, imported vehicles, kit cars, or modified vehicles. Prepare the dealer package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Hawaiʻi tax handling. Escalate if shipping receipt, safety inspection, G-27, weight slip, FMVSS compliance, lienholder/title, or tax handling is unclear.
