MINNESOTA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Minnesota DPS says:

- If customer recently moved to Minnesota, they have 60 days to register a passenger vehicle, motorcycle, utility trailer, or house trailer.
- Vehicle must be registered immediately if registration is expired or if it is a commercial trailer or truck.
- Customer may register at a deputy registrar office by mailing in completed Application to Title/Register a Vehicle with fees.

To register in Minnesota, customer must provide:

- Name and address of any lienholders
- Current odometer reading
- Driver’s license or acceptable identification
- Name of insurance company, policy number, and policy expiration date

For leased vehicles, provide:
- Registration card or copy of title showing leasing company as owner
- Lessee designation form signed by authorized agent of leasing company
- If leasing company provides POA, customer may sign lessee designation form
- Application to title/register a vehicle completed and signed by leasing company
- If leasing company provides POA, customer may sign application to title/register

For U.S. state or jurisdiction vehicles:
- Customer must also provide current vehicle title, Manufacturer’s Certificate of Origin, or Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin.
- Minnesota title will only be issued when the most current out-of-state title is submitted.
- The following can also be used to register a motor vehicle:
  - Most recent registration card issued from U.S. state/jurisdiction, if not expired more than one year
  - Copy of out-of-state title clearly marked “COPY”
  - Confirmation of Ownership issued when bank is holding physical title
  - Title Application Receipt issued by titling state
  - Web Dealer Title issued by titling state
  - Title Summary issued by titling state
  - Electronic Title Copy

For out-of-country vehicles, federal forms may be required:
- CBP Form 7501 or CBP Form 3461
- EPA Form 3520
- NHTSA Form HS-7
- Additional compliance documentation may be needed

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MINNESOTA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Minnesota customer and the customer wants Minnesota title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder name and address if financed

2. Minnesota-specific items to review:
   - Application to title/register vehicle
   - Current title, MCO, or MSO if title issuance requested
   - Most current out-of-state title required for Minnesota title
   - Driver license or acceptable ID
   - Insurance company, policy number, and expiration date
   - Current odometer reading
   - Lienholder name and address
   - 60-day registration timing / immediate registration if expired or commercial

3. If title is held by bank/lienholder:
   - Review alternate proof for registration only.
   - Understand that Minnesota title will only issue when most current out-of-state title is submitted.
   - Escalate if customer expects title but bank holds physical title.

4. If leased:
   - Obtain registration card or copy of title showing leasing company as owner.
   - Obtain lessee designation form signed by lessor/authorized agent or signed under POA.
   - Obtain title/register application completed and signed by leasing company or POA.

5. If imported/out-of-country:
   - Review CBP, EPA, NHTSA forms and additional compliance documents.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Minnesota customer buying from a Florida dealership, Minnesota requires lienholder name/address if applicable, current odometer reading, driver license or acceptable ID, and insurance company/policy/expiration information. For a U.S. state vehicle, Minnesota requires current title, MCO, or MSO for title issuance, and Minnesota title will only be issued when the most current out-of-state title is submitted. If the bank holds the title, certain alternate documents may support registration, but not title issuance. Leased vehicles require lessor ownership proof, lessee designation form, and title/register application signed by the leasing company or under POA. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if title possession, leased documents, insurance, odometer, registration timing, or tax handling is unclear.
