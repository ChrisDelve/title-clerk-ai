============================================================
ILLINOIS SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Illinois Secretary of State title/registration checklist for out-of-state title shows:

Next steps:
- Start Electronic Registration and Title transaction online.
- Gather documents and payment method.
- Submit application/payment by mail or bring to a DMV facility.

Documents listed:
- Out-of-state title
- If vehicle is 2011 or newer, verify odometer reading on vehicle with the assignment on the title and application
- Proof of insurance
- Printed document from Electronic Title and Registration transaction
- Loan information for vehicle, if applicable
- Tax form

Payment listed:
- Check or money order to Illinois Secretary of State for title and registration fees
- Check or money order to Illinois Department of Revenue for taxes
- Cash is not accepted for mailed transactions

Illinois notes:
- All presented documents must be valid and correct.
- Photocopies are not accepted.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR ILLINOIS CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Illinois customer and the customer wants Illinois title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder/loan information if financed

2. Illinois-specific items to review:
   - Illinois Electronic Registration and Title transaction
   - Printed ERT transaction document
   - Illinois proof of insurance
   - Illinois tax form
   - Loan information if financed
   - Odometer verification for 2011-or-newer vehicle
   - Originals/valid documents, not photocopies

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/loan information required for Illinois package.
   - Confirm lender/title mailing instructions.

4. If mailed:
   - Confirm checks/money orders are correct for Illinois SOS and Illinois Department of Revenue.
   - Do not tell customer cash can be mailed.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Out-of-state title is missing.
- ERT transaction document is missing.
- Proof of Illinois insurance is missing.
- Tax form is missing.
- Loan/lien information is missing.
- Odometer verification for 2011-or-newer vehicle is not reviewed.
- Documents are photocopies rather than valid originals/correct documents.
- Payment to Illinois SOS or Illinois Department of Revenue is wrong.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Illinois customer buying from a Florida dealership, Illinois may require the customer to start an Electronic Registration and Title transaction, then submit the out-of-state title, proof of insurance, printed ERT document, tax form, loan information if financed, and payment for title/registration fees and taxes. For 2011-or-newer vehicles, Illinois says to verify the odometer reading on the vehicle with the title assignment and application. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if the ERT document, tax form, insurance, loan information, odometer verification, or payment handling is unclear.
