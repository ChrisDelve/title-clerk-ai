NEBRASKA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Nebraska DMV says:

Motor vehicles, motorboats, ATVs, minibikes, and UTVs coming into Nebraska from another state are required to obtain a Nebraska Certificate of Title unless they fall into exempt categories.

To obtain Nebraska title, the following documentation, properly assigned, must be surrendered to a County Treasurer office:

Proof of ownership, such as:
- Manufacturer's Statement of Origin (MSO) or Importer's Certificate for new vehicles
- Certificate of Title from vehicle's previous location for used vehicles
- Vehicle's last registration and Bill of Sale for used vehicles if previous location was not a title-issuing state
- U.S. Military Registration Document for vehicles registered by U.S. Military
- U.S. Department of State Certificate of Title for vehicles sold by U.S. Government
- For vehicles registered in foreign country: current registration certificate and/or bill of sale, foreign country ownership documents, and import papers
- Court Order

Additional Nebraska requirements:
- Application for Certificate of Title
- $10.00 title fee
- Sheriff's Inspection
- If transfer of ownership is occurring at same time, seller must provide buyer with Bill of Sale or Nebraska Department of Revenue Form 6, Nebraska Sales/Use Tax and Tire Fee Statement for Motor Vehicle and Trailer Sales.
- County Treasurer retains all documentation presented for title issuance.
- Once documentation is verified, Nebraska Certificate of Title will be issued.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NEBRASKA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Nebraska customer and the customer wants Nebraska title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for new vehicle, or properly assigned title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Nebraska-specific items to review:
   - Nebraska Application for Certificate of Title
   - Properly assigned ownership documents surrendered to County Treasurer
   - Sheriff’s Inspection
   - $10 title fee
   - Bill of Sale or Nebraska DOR Form 6 if transfer of ownership occurs at same time
   - Last registration and bill of sale if prior location was not title-issuing
   - Import papers if foreign country registration/ownership history exists

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title handling.
   - Confirm County Treasurer title/lien recording requirements.

4. If prior location was non-title-issuing:
   - Nebraska requires last registration and Bill of Sale.
   - Escalate if customer lacks either.

5. If foreign/imported vehicle:
   - Review foreign registration, bill of sale, ownership documents, and import papers.
   - Escalate before submission if import chain is incomplete.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Nebraska customer buying from a Florida dealership, Nebraska generally requires vehicles coming from another state to obtain a Nebraska Certificate of Title unless exempt. The customer must surrender properly assigned ownership documents to the County Treasurer, such as MSO/importer certificate for new vehicles or prior title for used vehicles. Nebraska also requires an Application for Certificate of Title, $10 title fee, and Sheriff’s Inspection. If transfer of ownership is occurring at the same time, the seller must provide a Bill of Sale or Nebraska Department of Revenue Form 6. If the prior location was not title-issuing, Nebraska requires last registration and bill of sale. Prepare the dealer ownership package, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123 plus Nebraska Form 6/tax handling. Escalate if sheriff inspection, ownership proof, non-title-state proof, import documents, lienholder, or tax handling is unclear.
