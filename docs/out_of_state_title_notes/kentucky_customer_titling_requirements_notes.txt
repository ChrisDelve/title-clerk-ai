============================================================
KENTUCKY SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Kentucky DRIVE says for out-of-state vehicles, take the following to the local County Clerk to apply for title:

- Proof of Kentucky Insurance, issue date within 45 days
- Kentucky Driver's License, Kentucky ID Card, or Social Security card
- Completed TC96-182 form and signed-over title
- If prior state only issues registration or bill of sale, bring those in place of signed-over title
- Signatures must be notarized
- If name on title differs from name on ID, provide affidavit showing they are same person; marriage license or divorce decree is an example
- Business vehicles licensed by Secretary of State titled using FEIN

Kentucky also says:
- Title Lien Statements must be submitted to County Clerk.
- County Clerk processes title and registration.
- County Clerk may request title from lienholder.
- Do not use liquid paper/white-out, scratch through, mark out, erase, or mutilate title.
- Provide affidavit with new information signed and notarized.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR KENTUCKY CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Kentucky customer and the customer wants Kentucky title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Kentucky-specific items to review:
   - TC96-182 completed
   - Signed-over title, or registration/bill of sale if prior state only issues those
   - Notarized signatures
   - Proof of Kentucky insurance issued within 45 days
   - Kentucky DL/ID or SSN card
   - Name affidavit if title name differs from ID
   - FEIN if business vehicle
   - Title lien statement if financed/lien involved

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder instructions.
   - County Clerk may request title from lienholder.
   - Confirm title/lienholder path before promising completion.

4. If corrections/alterations:
   - Do not use white-out or scratch-outs.
   - Obtain notarized affidavit for new information.
   - Escalate if title condition is questionable.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Kentucky insurance missing or issue date older than 45 days.
- TC96-182 missing.
- Signatures not notarized.
- Signed-over title missing when prior state issues titles.
- Registration/bill of sale substitution used without confirming prior state only issues those.
- Name on title differs from ID without affidavit/name document.
- Title has white-out, scratch-out, mark-out, erasure, or mutilation.
- Title/lienholder held title path is unclear.
- Business title lacks FEIN review.
- Tax/DR-123 handling is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Kentucky customer buying from a Florida dealership, Kentucky requires proof of Kentucky insurance issued within 45 days, Kentucky DL/ID or SSN card, completed TC96-182, and a signed-over title. If the prior state only issues registration or bill of sale, Kentucky says to bring those in place of the signed title. Signatures must be notarized. If the title name differs from the customer’s ID, an affidavit or name-change document is needed. Title lien statements go through the County Clerk, and the clerk may request the title from the lienholder. Do not use white-out or scratch-outs on the title; use a signed/notarized affidavit for new information. Escalate if insurance timing, notarization, lienholder title, name mismatch, title alteration, or tax handling is unclear.
