============================================================
OREGON SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Oregon DMV Form 735-6603 says to apply for title and registration for vehicles new to Oregon:

- Complete and sign Application for Title and Registration, Form 735-226.
- Include odometer reading and all security interest holders.
- Submit the out-of-state title, Manufacturer's Certificate of Origin, or other original ownership document.
- Do not mail the application to DMV without the title or it will be returned unprocessed.
- Get a VIN inspection if the vehicle has an out-of-state or out-of-country title, salvage title, or ownership document.
- VIN inspection can be done at DMV; fee is $9 when done in Oregon.
- If vehicle is out of state, inspection can be done by the state’s DMV or law enforcement officer.
- Provide proof of Low Emissions Vehicle compliance if applicable.
- LEV applies to passenger vehicles model year 2009 or newer with 7,500 miles or less unless exempt.
- Electric, hybrid, and government-owned vehicles are exempt from LEV requirement.
- Submit DEQ emissions certificate if living in Portland Metro Area or Medford and required.
- Pay title, registration, and plate fees.

For out-of-country title or ownership documents, Oregon also requires:
- Proof vehicle meets U.S. Federal Vehicle Standards.
- Certification of Liens on an Imported Vehicle, Form 735-6436.

Security interest holder/title held by lender:
- If security interest holder is holding the title, send completed documents, fees, and Security Interest Holder letter Form 735-6603L to the holder.
- The letter asks the security interest holder to send the title, documents, and fees to DMV.
- Do not mail application without title.

Additional Oregon notes:
- Purchased from out-of-state dealer and vehicle is brand new or has 7,500 miles or less: Use Tax Certificate may be needed.
- Damaged vehicles may carry damage brand.
- DMV will not issue title for junk title, junk certificate, similar ownership document, or NMVTIS history showing crushed, junk, parts-only, or scrapped.
- Certain older diesel-powered trucks in Clackamas, Multnomah, or Washington County may require diesel retrofit compliance.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR OREGON CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to an Oregon customer and the customer wants Oregon title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Original MCO/MSO or properly assigned original title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Security interest holder/lienholder information if financed

2. Oregon-specific items to review:
   - Oregon Form 735-226 Application for Title and Registration
   - Original title/MCO/original ownership document
   - VIN inspection for out-of-state/out-of-country/salvage ownership document
   - LEV compliance if model year 2009 or newer passenger vehicle with 7,500 miles or less
   - DEQ emissions certificate if customer is in Portland Metro Area or Medford and emissions applies
   - Oregon Use Tax Certificate if brand new or 7,500 miles or less from out-of-state dealer
   - Security Interest Holder letter 735-6603L if lender/titleholder has title

3. If financed:
   - Confirm whether security interest holder is holding title.
   - Send completed documents, fees, and 735-6603L to security interest holder if required.
   - Do not submit package without title unless Oregon process specifically directs lender/titleholder handling.

4. If imported/out-of-country:
   - Review proof of U.S. Federal Vehicle Standards.
   - Review Oregon Form 735-6436 Certification of Liens on Imported Vehicle.
   - Escalate if federal import/ownership documents are incomplete.

5. If customer is in emissions/LEV area:
   - Confirm LEV compliance or exemption.
   - Confirm DEQ certificate if applicable.
   - Escalate if emissions/LEV proof is missing.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Original out-of-state title/MCO/original ownership document is missing.
- Oregon application is mailed without title.
- VIN inspection missing.
- VIN inspection completed by unauthorized party.
- LEV compliance/exemption missing for 2009-or-newer passenger vehicle with 7,500 miles or less.
- DEQ emissions certificate missing when required.
- Security interest holder/title path unclear.
- 735-6603L missing when security interest holder holds title.
- Use Tax Certificate issue missed.
- Imported/out-of-country Federal Standards or 735-6436 missing.
- Damaged/junk/NMVTIS brand issue not reviewed.
- Oregon diesel retrofit issue not reviewed.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For an Oregon customer buying from a Florida dealership, Oregon requires Form 735-226, the original out-of-state title/MCO/original ownership document, VIN inspection for out-of-state or out-of-country title/ownership documents, LEV compliance if applicable, DEQ emissions certificate if required, and title/registration/plate fees. Oregon warns not to mail the application without the title or it will be returned unprocessed. If the security interest holder has the title, use Oregon Form 735-6603L and send completed documents/fees to the holder so the title package can be sent to DMV. If the vehicle is brand new or has 7,500 miles or less from an out-of-state dealer, review Oregon Use Tax Certificate requirements. Prepare the dealer ownership package, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if title possession, VIN inspection, LEV/DEQ, use tax, imported vehicle, damaged/junk brand, or tax handling is unclear.
