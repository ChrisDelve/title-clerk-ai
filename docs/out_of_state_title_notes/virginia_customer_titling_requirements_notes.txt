VIRGINIA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Virginia DMV says:

For out-of-state vehicles or trailers, customer must present:

- Completed online or paper Application for Title and Registration
- Proof of address
- One of the following:
  - Title or registration card from previous state of residence
  - Previous owner's out-of-state title signed over to customer
  - Ownership document issued by U.S. Armed Forces showing customer as owner

If purchased within the last 12 months, provide one of the following:

- Proof of purchase price, such as buyer's order, bill of sale, or seller's declared sale price on title
- Vehicle Price Certification signed by seller or purchaser

Virginia title fees/taxes:

- $15 title fee
- Sales and Use Tax unless exempt
- If exempt, bring proof of exemption or complete Purchaser's Statement of Tax Exemption

Odometer:

- When vehicle is sold or transferred, seller must record odometer reading in assignment section of title.
- Buyer must record odometer reading on Application for Title and Registration.
- If out-of-state title does not have an assignment section, seller may record mileage on Odometer Disclosure Statement.
- If out-of-state title does not have assignment section and odometer reading is not actual/current, seller must disclose the correct status on Odometer Disclosure Statement.
- If out-of-state title shows vehicle was exempt from odometer disclosure, no odometer disclosure is required for Virginia title; present the out-of-state title showing exemption.

Electronic / paper title:

- Customer can choose paper title or have DMV keep electronic record of title.
- If no lien ever existed and no paper title was previously issued, one-time paper title may be issued at no fee.
- If lien existed or paper title was previously printed, substitute title fee applies.

Dealer processing:

- Virginia DMV says if customer recently purchased from dealer and dealer is processing work, it may take up to 30 days for transaction to process and vehicle to appear in DMV record.
- Leased vehicles will not show in customer's DMV record.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR VIRGINIA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Virginia customer and the customer wants Virginia title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for new vehicle or properly assigned title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Purchase price proof
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Virginia-specific items to review:
   - Virginia Application for Title and Registration
   - Proof of Virginia address
   - Prior title/registration/ownership document
   - Purchase price proof if purchased within last 12 months
   - Sales and Use Tax or exemption proof
   - Odometer disclosure on title/application or separate Odometer Disclosure Statement if title lacks assignment section
   - Paper vs electronic title selection

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title instructions.
   - Confirm whether Virginia electronic title or paper title is selected.
   - Confirm lienholder information is included.

4. If odometer issue:
   - If out-of-state title lacks assignment section, use Odometer Disclosure Statement.
   - If mileage is non-actual, ensure proper non-actual status is marked.
   - If title shows odometer exemption, retain proof of exemption.

5. If tax issue:
   - Review Virginia sales/use tax and exemption rules.
   - Review Florida DR-123/nonresident sale workflow before finalizing.

============================================================
TAX / RECIPROCITY NOTES
============================================================

For Florida dealer sale to Virginia resident:

- Review Florida DOR out-of-state resident sales tax / DR-123 workflow.
- Virginia requires Sales and Use Tax unless exempt.
- If exempt, Virginia requires exemption proof or Purchaser's Statement of Tax Exemption.
- Verify tax handling with controller, agency, lender, or Virginia DMV before finalizing.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS

- Purchase price proof missing for vehicle purchased within last 12 months.
- Sales/use tax or exemption proof missing.
- Odometer reading missing on title/application.

- Non-actual mileage status not disclosed.
- Lienholder/title instructions unclear.
- Paper/electronic title selection unclear.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Virginia customer buying from a Florida dealership, Virginia requires a completed Application for Title and Registration, proof of address, and ownership proof such as prior title/registration, assigned out-of-state title, or eligible U.S. Armed Forces ownership document. If the vehicle was purchased within the last 12 months, Virginia also requires proof of purchase price such as buyer’s order, bill of sale, declared sale price on title, or Vehicle Price Certification. Virginia charges a title fee and Sales and Use Tax unless exempt. Odometer must be recorded by the seller on the title assignment and by the buyer on the application; if the out-of-state title has no assignment section, use Virginia’s Odometer Disclosure Statement. Prepare the dealer title package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/Virginia tax handling. Escalate if proof of address, purchase price, odometer, lienholder, electronic/paper title, or tax handling is unclear.
