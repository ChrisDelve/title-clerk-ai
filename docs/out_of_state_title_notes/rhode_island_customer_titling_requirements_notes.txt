RHODE ISLAND SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Rhode Island DMV says Rhode Island title law changed effective January 1, 2024:

- All used vehicles coming into Rhode Island from out of state, regardless of model year, require VIN Inspection from a local municipal police department.
- If an out-of-state used vehicle is being registered in Rhode Island and the state from which the vehicle is coming required a title for that vehicle:
  - That title must be in the name of the seller.
  - The title must be properly assigned to the buyer.
  - The title must be presented with VIN Inspection at time of sales tax payment or registration at RI DMV.
- If an out-of-state used vehicle is being registered in Rhode Island and the state from which the vehicle is coming did not require a title:
  - Valid proof of ownership, such as old registration or paid sales tax form, in the name of seller must be presented.
  - VIN Inspection must also be presented at time of sales tax payment or registration at RI DMV.
- All used vehicles, regardless of model year, will now be issued a Rhode Island Certificate of Title upon payment of Sales Tax and/or Registration.

Taxes and titles:
- When purchasing a vehicle in Rhode Island, customer is required to pay sales tax and obtain title in their own name even if they do not register the vehicle.
- Customer must complete both:
  - Sales Tax form
  - Application for Registration TR-1
- For out-of-state vehicles, VIN check from police department is required.
- Transaction may be processed by mail to Rhode Island DMV in Cranston.
- Costs are listed in DMV Title Fee Table.

Previously non-titled vehicles:
- Prior to January 1, 2024, Rhode Island did not title model year 2000 and older vehicles.
- Effective January 1, 2024, all vehicles are now eligible for title, including model year 2000 and older previously registered under old law.
- To obtain title for a previously untitled vehicle registered in customer’s name, present valid registration, expired registration, or stamped paid sales tax form and completed TR-2/TR-9 with payment.

Lien notes:
- Rhode Island does not require a person to apply for a new title once lien is satisfied.
- RI requires lien to be released by lienholder signing off on title or providing lien release letter to attach to title.
- Duplicate title can only be issued to owner listed on original title.
- If there is still a lien, lienholder can apply.
- If original title was issued with lienholder listed, original lien release is needed to receive lien-free title.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR RHODE ISLAND CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Rhode Island customer and the customer wants Rhode Island title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO for new vehicle or properly assigned title for used vehicle
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Rhode Island-specific items to review:
   - Local municipal police VIN inspection for all used vehicles coming into RI from out of state
   - Title in seller’s name and properly assigned to buyer if prior state required title
   - Valid proof of ownership in seller’s name if prior state did not require title
   - Sales Tax form
   - TR-1 Application for Registration
   - VIN check from police department for out-of-state vehicle
   - Title required even if customer does not register
   - RI DMV mail-in option if applicable

3. If used vehicle:
   - Do not ignore VIN inspection due to model year.
   - Since January 1, 2024, all used out-of-state vehicles require VIN inspection from local municipal police department.

4. If non-title state history:
   - Verify old registration, paid sales tax form, or other valid proof of ownership in seller’s name.
   - Escalate if ownership proof does not match seller.

5. If financed/lienholder:
   - Confirm lienholder information.
   - Confirm lien release handling if prior title has lienholder.
   - Escalate if active lien or lien release is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Rhode Island customer buying from a Florida dealership, Rhode Island’s title law changed effective January 1, 2024. All used vehicles coming into Rhode Island from out of state, regardless of model year, require VIN inspection from a local municipal police department. If the prior state required a title, the title must be in the seller’s name, properly assigned to the buyer, and presented with VIN inspection at the time of sales tax payment or registration. If the prior state did not require a title, valid proof of ownership such as old registration or paid sales tax form in the seller’s name must be presented with VIN inspection. Rhode Island requires both a Sales Tax form and TR-1 Application for Registration, and requires sales tax/title in the customer’s name even if the vehicle is not registered. Prepare the dealer package, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information if financed, and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if VIN inspection, ownership proof, TR-1, sales tax form, lien release, or tax handling is unclear.
