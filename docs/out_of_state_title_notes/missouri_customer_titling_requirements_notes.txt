MISSOURI SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Missouri DOR says:

For Missouri resident titling:
- Customer has 30 days from date of purchase to title and pay sales tax.
- Late titling penalty begins on the 31st day after purchase.
- Required documents include:
  - Certificate of Title properly signed over, or Manufacturer's Statement of Origin (MSO)
  - Signed Application for Missouri Title and License, Form 108
  - Identification number and odometer inspection (ID/OD), if ownership transferred on title issued by another state or country
  - Safety inspection not more than 60 days old may satisfy ID/OD inspection
  - Odometer Disclosure Statement, Form 3019, if applicable
  - Notarized Lien Release, Form 4809, if applicable
  - Additional documentation may be requested at titling

For new resident/out-of-state title registration:
- Customer has 30 days from becoming Missouri resident to title vehicle.
- If lienholder has out-of-state title, customer may use Owner Out-of-State Title Request, Form 5834.
- If lienholder refuses/unable to release title, Missouri may allow one-year registration with acceptable proof, but Missouri title is not issued and one-year registration is not renewable until out-of-state title is received.
- Required documents include:
  - Original title or proof of ownership according to prior state law
  - Form 108
  - ID/OD inspection
  - Current insurance card or other proof of financial responsibility
  - Statement of non-assessment from Missouri county/city assessor, or military LES if applicable
  - Missouri safety inspection not more than 60 days old
  - Emissions inspection not more than 60 days old if resident of St. Louis City, Jefferson, St. Charles, or St. Louis County, if applicable

Missouri fees/taxes listed:
- $8.50 title fee
- $9.00 processing fee
- $2.00 electronic transmission fee may be collected, if applicable
- Registration/plate fees based on taxable horsepower or weight
- State tax 4.225% plus local sales tax, less trade-in allowance, if any
- If vehicle owned/operated in another state for at least 90 days before titling/registering in Missouri, no additional sales tax is due
- If owned less than 90 days and tax paid to another state is less than Missouri state/local tax, proof of tax paid is required and difference is due

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MISSOURI CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Missouri customer and the customer wants Missouri title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Missouri-specific items to review:
   - Missouri Form 108
   - ID/OD inspection
   - Safety inspection not more than 60 days old
   - Emissions inspection if customer resides in applicable Missouri emissions area
   - Proof of financial responsibility/insurance
   - Statement of non-assessment or military LES if applicable
   - Form 3019 if odometer disclosure applies
   - Form 4809 lien release if lien release applies

3. If financed or title held by lienholder:
   - Review Form 5834 title request.
   - Confirm whether lienholder will release out-of-state title.
   - If lienholder will not release title, Missouri may issue one-year registration only; Missouri title is not issued until title is received.
   - Escalate if customer expects full title but lienholder/title possession blocks issuance.

4. If registering:
   - Verify Missouri insurance/financial responsibility.
   - Verify inspection and emissions requirements.

5. If tax question:
   - Review 30-day timing and Missouri state/local tax rules.
   - Review Florida DR-123 / out-of-state resident sales tax workflow.

============================================================
TAX / RECIPROCITY NOTES
============================================================

For Florida dealer sale to Missouri resident:

- Review Florida DOR out-of-state resident sales tax / DR-123 workflow.
- Missouri source says state tax is 4.225% plus local tax, less trade-in allowance, if any.
- If vehicle was owned/operated in another state for at least 90 days before Missouri titling/registration, no additional sales tax is due.
- If owned less than 90 days and sales tax paid to another state is less than Missouri state/local tax, customer must show proof of payment and pay the difference.
- Verify tax handling with controller, agency, lender, or Missouri DOR before finalizing.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Form 108 missing or unsigned.
- ID/OD inspection missing.
- Missouri safety inspection missing or older than 60 days.
- Emissions inspection missing for applicable area.
- Insurance/financial responsibility missing.
- Statement of non-assessment missing.
- Lien release missing when applicable.
- Form 5834 not used when lienholder holds out-of-state title.
- Lienholder refuses title and customer expects Missouri title instead of one-year registration only.
- Odometer disclosure missing when applicable.
- 30-day titling/tax deadline missed.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Missouri customer buying from a Florida dealership, Missouri generally requires Form 108, title/MSO or acceptable ownership proof, ID/OD inspection for out-of-state titles, odometer disclosure if applicable, lien release if applicable, insurance/financial responsibility, statement of non-assessment, and safety inspection not more than 60 days old. Emissions inspection may be required for St. Louis City, Jefferson, St. Charles, or St. Louis County residents. If the lienholder holds the out-of-state title, review Form 5834; if the lienholder will not release title, Missouri may issue one-year registration only and will not issue Missouri title until the title is received. Prepare the dealer ownership package and review Florida DR-123/Missouri tax handling. Escalate if inspections, lienholder title, emissions, insurance, non-assessment, or tax handling is unclear.
