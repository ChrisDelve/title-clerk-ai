PENNSYLVANIA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

PennDOT Fact Sheet says:

General title rule:
- Out-of-state certificate of title must be surrendered to PennDOT when applicant submits Form MV-1.
- Pennsylvania Certificate of Title will only be issued when the out-of-state title is submitted with completed MV-1 and processed by PennDOT.
- Temporary Pennsylvania registration plates may be issued only when the out-of-state title is submitted with completed MV-1.

If out-of-state title is attached:
- Temporary registration plate may be issued by issuing agent.
- Form MV-1 must be completed.
- Check or money order payable to Commonwealth of Pennsylvania attached for all title, lien, registration fees, and sales tax if applicable.
- Lienholder listed on out-of-state title must be listed on MV-1 unless lien was satisfied on out-of-state title.
- If refinanced, new lienholder must be listed on MV-1.
- Fee required to record lien.

If out-of-state lienholder will not release title to owner but will release to PennDOT:
- Vehicle owner must contact PennDOT Call Center to request letter to lienholder asking title be sent to PennDOT.
- Authorized agent may not complete MV-1 until PennDOT receives out-of-state certificate of title.
- Once PennDOT receives title, customer receives letter and provides copy to authorized agent.
- Agent contacts PennDOT representative listed in letter.
- PennDOT representative faxes copy of out-of-state title to agent so MV-1 can be completed.
- This is the only time an authorized agent may accept photocopy of certificate of title to complete MV-1.
- Temporary registration plate may be issued.
- MV-1 completed with check/money order for title, lien, registration fees, and sales tax if applicable.
- Out-of-state lienholder name, address, and FIN if lienholder participates in ELT must be listed on MV-1.
- Agent submits all documents to PennDOT representative so original out-of-state title can be attached.
- Agent retains customer letter and faxed title copy.

If lienholder refuses to release title to owner or PennDOT:
- No temporary registration plate may be issued by issuing agent.
- Vehicle owner must comply with out-of-state lienholder requirements to obtain title.
- Out-of-state registration plate must be valid for vehicle to continue operating on Pennsylvania roadways.

Special VIN instructions:
- Section A of MV-1 must verify VIN matches out-of-state certificate of title and vehicle VIN plate.
- VIN plate tracing must be attached to reverse side of white copy of all MV-1 applications for PA Certificates of Title for:
  - All trucks or truck tractors
  - All motor homes
  - All vehicles previously titled in another state
- If VIN tracing is not possible, certified inspection mechanic or notary public employed by motor vehicle dealer/issuing agent may visually verify.
- If vehicle is physically outside Pennsylvania, VIN must be visually verified by police officer, mechanic employed by motor vehicle dealer, Armed Forces installation provost marshal, or motor transportation officer using Form MV-41.

Sales tax:
- If vehicle titled in applicant's name for at least six months, proof of sales tax paid is not required.
- If less than six months, applicant must provide proof of sales tax paid or remit Pennsylvania sales tax.
- Some exceptions apply for construction contractors and military personnel whose home of record/residency was Pennsylvania when purchased.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR PENNSYLVANIA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Pennsylvania customer and the customer wants Pennsylvania title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Original title/MCO or properly assigned out-of-state title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Buyer’s order / bill of sale
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Pennsylvania-specific items to review:
   - Form MV-1
   - Out-of-state title surrender to PennDOT
   - Temporary plate availability only if out-of-state title submitted with MV-1
   - Check/money order for title, lien, registration fees, and sales tax if applicable
   - Lienholder listed on MV-1
   - FIN if lienholder participates in ELT
   - VIN tracing or MV-41 verification if vehicle outside Pennsylvania
   - Proof of sales tax paid if required

3. If lienholder holds title:
   - Determine whether lienholder will release title to owner, PennDOT, or neither.
   - If lienholder releases only to PennDOT, owner must contact PennDOT Call Center and wait for PennDOT title receipt before agent completes MV-1.
   - If lienholder refuses to release title to owner or PennDOT, no temporary registration plate may be issued.
   - Do not promise PA title or temp plate until lienholder/title release path is clear.

4. If vehicle is outside Pennsylvania:
   - Use MV-41 VIN verification by authorized verifier if tracing cannot be completed or vehicle outside PA.
   - Do not assume Florida VIN/photo is enough unless Pennsylvania source allows.

5. If tax issue:
   - Review whether vehicle has been titled in applicant’s name for at least six months.
   - If less than six months, proof of tax paid or PA tax may be required.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Out-of-state title is not surrendered.
- MV-1 is completed before PennDOT receives lienholder-held title.
- Temporary plate issued without out-of-state title submitted with MV-1.
- Lienholder refuses to release title to owner or PennDOT.
- Lienholder/FIN not listed correctly on MV-1.
- VIN tracing missing.
- MV-41 missing when vehicle is outside Pennsylvania and visual verification is required.
- Sales tax proof missing when vehicle titled in applicant’s name less than six months.
- Agent uses photocopy of title outside the one PennDOT-authorized lienholder scenario.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Pennsylvania customer buying from a Florida dealership, Pennsylvania requires Form MV-1 and surrender of the out-of-state title to PennDOT. A Pennsylvania title is only issued when the out-of-state title is submitted with completed MV-1 and processed. Temporary Pennsylvania registration plates may be issued only when the out-of-state title is submitted with MV-1. If the out-of-state lienholder will release the title only to PennDOT, the owner must contact PennDOT and the authorized agent cannot complete MV-1 until PennDOT receives the title. If the lienholder refuses to release the title to the owner or PennDOT, no temporary plate may be issued. Pennsylvania also requires VIN tracing for vehicles previously titled in another state, with MV-41 visual verification if the vehicle is physically outside Pennsylvania. Prepare the dealer package, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder information, and review Florida DR-123/PA tax handling. Escalate if title surrender, lienholder release, MV-1, VIN tracing/MV-41, temp plate, or tax handling is unclear.
