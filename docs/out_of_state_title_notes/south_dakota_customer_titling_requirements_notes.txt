SOUTH DAKOTA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

South Dakota DOR says:

General:
- Motor vehicle titling and registration is handled through local county treasurer’s office.
- Motor Vehicle or Boat Title & Registration Application must be signed by record owner(s) or authorized agent.
- In addition to title, seller should provide Bill of Sale to purchaser.

Timing:
- Vehicle owners have 45 days to apply for title and registration of an acquired vehicle.
- There is no extension on the 45-day requirement.
- Interest and penalties accrue on motor vehicle excise tax.
- Fees are assessed from purchase date regardless of application date.
- Interest is 1% or $5, whichever is greater, for first month and 1% per month thereafter.
- One-time penalty equal to 10% of tax or $10, whichever is greater, applies against applications made after 60 days from purchase date.
- Title late fees accrue at $1 per week up to 24 weeks, then max at $50.

Out-of-state resident registering in South Dakota:
- Customer should know their state may prohibit registering vehicle in another state.
- Required items listed:
  - Original out-of-state titles
  - Copy of state driver license
  - Social Security number
  - Motor Vehicle and Boat Title & Registration Application
- Take paperwork to local county treasurer’s office.
- County treasurer gives appropriate fees for that county.

Moving to South Dakota:
- Customer has 90 days from date of arrival to title and license vehicle in South Dakota.
- If vehicle titled/licensed in another state, reciprocity applies.
- If prior state tax rate is above South Dakota’s tax rate, no additional tax.
- If prior state has no tax or lower tax rate than South Dakota, customer pays additional tax to match.

Plate rule:
- South Dakota is a plate-with-owner state.
- Plates are retained by owner.
- When purchasing new vehicle, owner transfers old plates to newly acquired vehicle through local county treasurer.

ELT:
- South Dakota has been an ELT state since October 2012.
- If lienholder is on vehicle loan, title remains electronic until lienholder releases interest.
- When paid off and lienholder releases, paper title is printed and mailed to location indicated by lienholder.
- If lender does not use provider and there is current title document, title plus loan contract and $10 lien fee is submitted to county treasurer.
- If title is already electronic, loan contract and $10 fee is remitted to county treasurer and lien is noted.

Damage disclosure:
- State law requires seller to provide completed Damage Disclosure Statement upon sale/transfer/trade.
- If motor vehicle or large boat titled in another jurisdiction is to be titled in South Dakota, importer must also complete Damage Disclosure Statement.
- Effective July 1, 2015, damage disclosure is no longer mandated, but damage disclosure remains noted on titles issued before July 1, 2015.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR SOUTH DAKOTA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a South Dakota customer and the customer wants South Dakota title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Original out-of-state title or MCO/MSO
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Bill of Sale / buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information and loan contract if financed

2. South Dakota-specific items to review:
   - Motor Vehicle or Boat Title & Registration Application
   - Application signed by record owner(s) or authorized agent
   - Local county treasurer processing
   - 45-day title/registration deadline from purchase date
   - Original out-of-state title
   - Copy of state driver license and SSN if out-of-state resident registration scenario
   - Plate-with-owner transfer if customer has existing SD plate
   - ELT/lien process if financed
   - Damage disclosure history if applicable

3. If financed:
   - Confirm whether lienholder participates in ELT.
   - Confirm whether title will remain electronic.
   - Confirm loan contract and lien fee handling if lender does not use provider.

4. If customer is moving to South Dakota:
   - Review 90-day title/license requirement.
   - Review tax reciprocity.

5. If customer is out-of-state resident trying to register in South Dakota:
   - Warn that their home state may prohibit registering in another state.
   - Confirm original titles, DL copy, SSN, and application.
   - Escalate if residency/eligibility is unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a South Dakota customer buying from a Florida dealership, South Dakota title/registration is handled through the local county treasurer. The Motor Vehicle or Boat Title & Registration Application must be signed by the record owner(s) or authorized agent, and the seller should provide a bill of sale. South Dakota gives buyers 45 days to apply for title and registration; late applications accrue interest, penalties, and title late fees. If the customer is registering in South Dakota as an out-of-state resident, the source lists original out-of-state title, copy of state driver license, SSN, and application, but warns the customer’s home state may prohibit registration elsewhere. South Dakota is a plate-with-owner state and an ELT state. Prepare the dealer package, original title/MCO, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder documents if financed, and review Florida DR-123/SD tax handling. Escalate if original title, county treasurer fees, ELT/lien, residency eligibility, timing, or tax handling is unclear.
