NORTH DAKOTA SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

North Dakota DOT out-of-state dealer resources state:

Requirements to apply for North Dakota title with an out-of-state title or MCO:

- Application for certificate of title must be made by owner using legal name evidenced by valid state-issued driver license, nondriver ID card, or other document confirming true identity.
- Nonresidents and military personnel must provide paper copy of current driver license card or nondriver ID card with certificate of title application.
- A business, trust, partnership, or corporation must provide paper copy document confirming legal name.
- Original out-of-state title completed by seller and buyer is required.
- If new vehicle, MCO completed by seller and buyer is required.
- If purchased from dealership, copy of Dealer Bill of Sale is required.
- Dealer Bill of Sale must include:
  - Seller information
  - Buyer information
  - Purchase price
  - Date of sale
  - Vehicle information including make, model, and VIN
- If vehicle is 2011 or newer, odometer information must be completed within reassignment on title or Odometer Disclosure for Retail Sale on MCO, with seller and buyer signatures.
- If vehicle is less than 9 years old, SFN 18609 Damage Disclosure Statement must be completed by seller.
- SFN 2872 Application for Certificate of Title and Registration of a Vehicle must be completed in full and signed by buyer.
- Tax is due on 5% of purchase price.
- Certificate of title fee is $5.00.
- Abandoned Vehicle Disposal fee is $1.50.
- Registration fees are based on first year registered and vehicle weight.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR NORTH DAKOTA CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a North Dakota customer and the customer wants North Dakota title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - Original out-of-state title completed by seller and buyer, or MCO completed by seller and buyer
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Dealer Bill of Sale with seller, buyer, purchase price, date of sale, make, model, and VIN
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if 2011 or newer
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. North Dakota-specific items to review:
   - Owner legal-name identity document
   - Paper copy of current DL/ID for nonresident or military
   - Business/trust/entity legal-name document if applicable
   - SFN 2872 completed and signed by buyer
   - SFN 18609 damage disclosure if vehicle is less than 9 years old
   - 5% tax on purchase price
   - $5 title fee
   - $1.50 abandoned vehicle disposal fee
   - Registration fees based on weight/year

3. If financed:
   - Confirm lienholder/title instructions.
   - Confirm lienholder information is included where required.
   - Verify whether title will be sent to lienholder.

4. If vehicle is 2011 or newer:
   - Confirm odometer information is completed in reassignment/title or MCO odometer retail sale section with seller and buyer signatures.

5. If vehicle is less than 9 years old:
   - Confirm SFN 18609 damage disclosure is completed by seller.

REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- Original out-of-state title or MCO is not completed by seller and buyer.
- Dealer Bill of Sale is missing or incomplete.
- SFN 2872 is missing or unsigned.
- Odometer information missing for 2011-or-newer vehicle.
- SFN 18609 damage disclosure missing for vehicle less than 9 years old.
- Nonresident/military paper ID copy missing.
- Business/trust/entity legal-name proof missing.
- Tax/DR-123 handling unclear.
- Registration weight/year fee calculation unclear.
- Lienholder/title instructions unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a North Dakota customer buying from a Florida dealership, North Dakota requires the original out-of-state title or MCO completed by seller and buyer, SFN 2872 completed and signed by the buyer, and a dealer bill of sale if purchased from a dealership. The dealer bill of sale must include seller, buyer, purchase price, sale date, and vehicle information including make, model, and VIN. If the vehicle is 2011 or newer, odometer information must be completed with seller and buyer signatures. If the vehicle is less than 9 years old, SFN 18609 Damage Disclosure Statement is required. North Dakota tax is due on 5% of purchase price, plus title and other applicable fees. Prepare the dealer package and review Florida DR-123/tax handling. Escalate if SFN forms, odometer, damage disclosure, identity copy, lienholder, or tax handling is unclear.
