============================================================
MAINE SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

Maine BMV passenger vehicle registration page states:

When purchased from a dealer:
- Customer must pay excise/municipal tax at town office.
- If vehicle is new, tax collector requires manufacturer’s suggested retail price sticker / Monroney label or copy.
- If local municipal office does not issue registrations, customer can register at BMV branch after paying tax at municipal office.
- Required items include:
  - Bill of sale or sales agreement from dealer
  - Blue title application for vehicle models 25 years old and newer
  - Proof of insurance
  - White and yellow copies of registration application and excise tax receipt
- Registration fee for plate if needed

When purchased from a private sale or out-of-state dealer:
- Customer must pay excise/municipal tax at town office.
- Required items include:
  - Signed bill of sale
  - Title signed from previous owner to current owner for vehicle models 25 years old and newer
  - Proof of insurance
  - White and yellow copies of registration application and excise tax receipt
- Maine sales tax is 5.5% on purchase price.
- Title application fee is $33 on vehicle models 25 years old and newer.
- Registration fees apply for plate if needed.

New Maine residents:
- Have 30 days after establishing residency to convert registrations and titles.
- Need out-of-state title for vehicle models 25 years old and newer.
- Need name/address of lienholders if applicable.
- Need out-of-state registration certificate.
- Need proof of insurance.
- Need white/yellow registration application and excise tax receipt.
- Maine use tax form may prove exemption from sales tax when showing out-of-state registration.

FLORIDA DEALER WORKFLOW FOR MAINE CUSTOMER
============================================================

When a Florida dealership sells a vehicle to a Maine customer and the customer wants Maine title/registration:

1. Prepare dealer ownership package:
   - MCO/MSO or properly assigned title
   - Dealer reassignment if applicable
   - Dealer bill of sale / sales agreement
   - Buyer’s order
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder information if financed

2. Maine-specific items to review:
   - Customer must pay excise/municipal tax at town office.
   - Blue title application if vehicle is 25 years old or newer.
   - Proof of insurance.
   - White/yellow registration application and excise tax receipt copies.
   - Monroney label/copy if vehicle is new.
   - Signed title from prior owner to current owner for out-of-state dealer/private sale and 25-years-or-newer vehicle.
   - Maine title fee and sales tax.

3. If financed:
   - Provide lienholder name and address.
   - Confirm lender/title mailing instructions.
   - Confirm whether Maine title application needs lienholder details.

4. If customer is new Maine resident:
   - 30-day conversion rule may apply.
   - Use tax exemption form may apply if showing out-of-state registration.

5. If registering:
   - Maine proof of insurance required.
   - Do not assume Florida insurance satisfies Maine registration requirements.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a Maine customer buying from a Florida dealership, Maine requires the customer to pay excise/municipal tax at the town office. For a dealer purchase, Maine lists a bill of sale or sales agreement from the dealer, blue title application for vehicles 25 years old and newer, proof of insurance, and white/yellow registration application plus excise-tax receipt copies. If purchased from an out-of-state dealer, Maine also references signed bill of sale and signed title for 25-years-or-newer vehicles. Prepare the dealer ownership package, buyer’s order, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder name/address if financed, and review Florida DR-123 plus Maine sales/excise tax handling. Escalate if title age, excise tax, insurance, bill of sale, lienholder, or tax handling is unclear.
