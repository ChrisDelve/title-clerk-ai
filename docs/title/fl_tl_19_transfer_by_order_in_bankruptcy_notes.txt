FLORIDA TITLE PROCEDURE — TRANSFER OF CERTIFICATE OF TITLE BY ORDER IN BANKRUPTCY
PROCEDURE: TL-19
CATEGORY: TITLE TRANSFERS / COURT ORDER / BANKRUPTCY
RELATED STATUTES:
- F.S. 319.28
- F.S. 328.01

==================================================
PROCEDURE SUMMARY
==================================================

This procedure governs transfers of ownership involving motor vehicles, mobile homes, or vessels when ownership is transferred through bankruptcy proceedings.

The bankruptcy trustee is the legally authorized party responsible for transferring ownership, applying for duplicate titles, assigning titles, or completing reassignment documentation.

Transactions may involve:
- Florida titles
- Out-of-state titles
- Lost titles
- Dealer reassignments
- Trustee-issued bills of sale
- Court-authorized ownership transfers

The procedure applies to:
- Motor vehicles
- Mobile homes
- Vessels

==================================================
FLORIDA TITLE AVAILABLE
==================================================

IF FLORIDA TITLE IS AVAILABLE:

REQUIRED:
- Original Florida title
- Trustee signs as transferor/seller
- Purchaser signs if required
- Dealer reassignment if dealer involved
- Bankruptcy documentation
- HSMV application
- Lien satisfactions unless court preserves liens
- Taxes or exemption
- Registration/decal information
- Title fees

==================================================
ACCEPTABLE BANKRUPTCY DOCUMENTS
==================================================

ANY ONE OF THE FOLLOWING IS ACCEPTABLE:

- Trustee appointment from bankruptcy court
- Interim trustee appointment
- Order approving trustee bond
- Notice of Commencement of Case

==================================================
TITLE APPLICATION FORMS
==================================================

MOTOR VEHICLES:
- HSMV 82040 MV

VESSELS:
- HSMV 82040 VS

MOBILE HOMES:
- HSMV 82040 MH

==================================================
IF FLORIDA TITLE IS NOT AVAILABLE
==================================================

REQUIRED:
- Bill of sale OR HSMV 82050
- Trustee as seller
- Purchaser information
- Bankruptcy documentation
- HSMV application
- Lien satisfactions unless preserved/released by court
- Tax or exemption information
- Registration/decal information
- Title fees

==================================================
DUPLICATE TITLE RULES
==================================================

IMPORTANT RULE:

Once bankruptcy judgment is issued:
ONLY THE TRUSTEE MAY APPLY FOR OR RECEIVE DUPLICATE TITLE.

REQUIRED:
- HSMV 82101
- Trustee signature
- Trustee address on application
- Bankruptcy documentation
- Proof of identification
- Lien satisfactions if applicable
- Title fees

IMPORTANT:
Duplicate title should be mailed to trustee address — not owner.

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE TITLE PROCEDURES
==================================================

REQUIRED:
- HSMV title application
- VIN verification if applicable
- Bankruptcy documentation
- Out-of-state ownership document assigned by trustee
- Lien satisfactions
- Tax or exemption information
- Florida registration/decal assignment
- Title fees

==================================================
VIN VERIFICATION RULES
==================================================

VIN VERIFICATION NOT REQUIRED FOR:
- Vessels
- Mobile homes
- Trailers under 2,000 pounds
- Travel trailers
- Camping trailers
- Truck campers
- Fifth-wheel recreation trailers

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE TITLE MISSING
==================================================

IF OUT-OF-STATE TITLE CANNOT BE LOCATED:

REQUIRED:
- Verification of title/lien status
- Bill of sale from trustee
- Follow TL-07 verification guidelines

==================================================
LIEN RULES
==================================================

LIEN SATISFACTIONS REQUIRED FOR:
- Any recorded liens
- Florida liens
- Out-of-state liens

UNLESS:
- Court order preserves liens
- Court order releases liens

==================================================
BANKRUPT LIENHOLDER PROCEDURES
==================================================

IF LIENHOLDER FILED BANKRUPTCY:

The trustee must:
- Complete lien reassignment
- Assign loans to new lienholder
- Apply for duplicate title if title lost

==================================================
DEALER RULES
==================================================

IMPORTANT:

If dealer purchases vehicle obtained through bankruptcy:
- Dealer MUST title vehicle in dealer name first
BEFORE resale.

Applies to:
- Motor vehicle dealers
- Vessel dealers
- Individual purchasers

==================================================
UNTITLED / UNREGISTERED VEHICLES
==================================================

IF VEHICLE NEVER TITLED OR REGISTERED:

Court order may serve as proof of ownership IF:
- Court order grants trustee authority to sell

==================================================
REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

MOTOR VEHICLES:
- Florida plate transfer/purchase
OR
- Non-use affidavit

VESSELS:
- Florida registration assignment
OR
- Non-use affidavit

MOBILE HOMES:
- Decal or RP decal required
- NON-USE AFFIDAVIT NOT ACCEPTABLE

==================================================
COMMON DEALERSHIP ERRORS
==================================================

- Accepting owner signature instead of trustee
- Missing bankruptcy documentation
- Missing lien satisfactions
- Duplicate title issued to owner instead of trustee
- Selling bankruptcy vehicle before titling into dealer name
- Missing VIN verification on out-of-state vehicle
- Using incorrect HSMV application version
- Assuming court order automatically clears liens

IMPORTANT OPERATIONAL NOTES
==================================================

- Trustee is the controlling legal authority after bankruptcy judgment
- Court orders do NOT automatically clear all liens
- Dealer must obtain title before resale
- Duplicate titles go only to trustee
- Out-of-state ownership verification may be required
- Bankruptcy documents must clearly identify trustee authority
