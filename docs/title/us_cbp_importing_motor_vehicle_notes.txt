PURPOSE
============================================================

This file helps distinguish a vehicle imported into Florida from another country from a vehicle brought into Florida from another U.S. state.

Use this file for questions like:

- Customer is importing a vehicle into Florida from another country.
- Customer has a foreign title.
- Vehicle is coming from outside the United States.
- What customs documents are needed to title an imported vehicle?
- What is EPA Form 3520-1?
- What is DOT Form HS-7?
- What do we need for an out-of-country vehicle?
- Can we title a vehicle imported from another country in Florida?

============================================================
CORE DISTINCTION
============================================================

There are two different workflows:

1. OUT-OF-STATE VEHICLE INTO FLORIDA

This means the vehicle was already titled/registered in another U.S. state.

Typical Florida title workflow may involve:
- Original out-of-state title
- HSMV 82040
- VIN verification / HSMV 82042
- Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
- Lien release or lienholder information if applicable
- Florida insurance if registration is requested
- Florida fees/taxes

2. OUT-OF-COUNTRY VEHICLE IMPORTED INTO THE UNITED STATES / FLORIDA

This means the vehicle entered the United States from another country.

This requires federal import review before Florida title/registration can safely proceed.

Federal import review may involve:
- CBP entry/import documents
- EPA Form 3520-1
- DOT Form HS-7
- Proof vehicle complies with U.S. EPA emissions requirements
- Proof vehicle complies with U.S. DOT / FMVSS safety requirements
- Foreign ownership/title documents
- Customs release/entry paperwork
- English translations if documents are not in English
- Registered Importer / Independent Commercial Importer review if nonconforming
- Escalation before Florida title submission

============================================================
CBP SOURCE SUMMARY
============================================================

U.S. Customs and Border Protection guidance for importing a motor vehicle states that parties responsible for imported vehicles must submit proper documents to CBP at the time entry is made.

The key federal forms include:

- EPA Form 3520-1
- DOT Form HS-7

These forms relate to federal emissions and safety compliance.

A vehicle imported from outside the United States should not be treated like a normal out-of-state U.S. title transaction until import/customs/EPA/DOT compliance documents are reviewed.

============================================================
FLORIDA DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW — OUT-OF-COUNTRY IMPORT
============================================================

When a customer is importing a vehicle into Florida from another country:

1. Determine origin:
   - Ask whether the vehicle came from another U.S. state or another country.
   - If another country, use CBP/federal import workflow before normal Florida title workflow.

2. Review federal import documents:
   - CBP entry/import documents
   - EPA Form 3520-1
   - DOT Form HS-7
   - Foreign title/registration/ownership documents
   - Customs release documents
   - English translations if applicable
   - Proof of federal safety/emissions compliance if required

3. Review Florida title documents:
   - HSMV 82040 title application
   - VIN verification / HSMV 82042 if required
   - Odometer disclosure/statement if applicable
   - Lienholder documents if financed
   - Florida insurance if registration/plate issuance is requested
   - Florida fees/taxes

4. Do not submit as a normal out-of-state title transaction until:
   - Federal import documents are reviewed
   - EPA/DOT compliance is clear
   - Ownership chain is clear
   - VIN and document chain match
   - Any foreign-language documents are translated if required
   - Title lead/controller/tax collector/vendor confirms path

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- User says “importing” but origin country/state is not clarified.
- Vehicle came from another country and app treats it like a normal U.S. out-of-state title.
- EPA Form 3520-1 is missing.
- DOT Form HS-7 is missing.
- CBP/customs entry documents are missing.
- Foreign ownership/title documents are incomplete.
- Foreign documents are not in English and no translation is provided.
- VIN on foreign/customs documents does not match vehicle.
- Vehicle is nonconforming or not manufactured for U.S. market.
- EPA/DOT/FMVSS compliance is unclear.
- Customer expects Florida title/registration before federal import compliance is resolved.
- Lienholder/financing documents are unclear.
- Tax/customs/title handling is unclear.

============================================================
ANSWER WORDING RULE
============================================================

If a user asks:

“Customer is importing a vehicle into Florida”

Do not assume it is only an out-of-state U.S. title.

First distinguish:

- Is the vehicle coming from another U.S. state?
- Or was it imported from another country?

If imported from another country, answer should include:

- Federal import/customs review is required.
- CBP import documents must be reviewed.
- EPA Form 3520-1 and DOT Form HS-7 may be required.
- Foreign title/ownership documents must be reviewed.
- Florida title/registration cannot be treated like a normal out-of-state title until federal import compliance is clear.
- Escalate before submission if customs, EPA, DOT, VIN, ownership, or foreign-document issues are unclear.

Do not say only:

- “Use HSMV 82040 and VIN verification.”

That is incomplete for an out-of-country import.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

If a customer is importing a vehicle into Florida, first determine whether the vehicle is coming from another U.S. state or from another country. If it is from another U.S. state, use the normal Florida out-of-state title workflow: properly assigned out-of-state title or MCO, HSMV 82040, VIN verification/HSMV 82042 if required, odometer disclosure/statement if applicable, lienholder documents if financed, and Florida insurance if registration is requested.

If the vehicle is imported from another country, do not treat it like a normal out-of-state U.S. title. Review CBP/customs entry documents, EPA Form 3520-1, DOT Form HS-7, foreign title/ownership documents, federal emissions/safety compliance, VIN consistency, and English translation needs before Florida title submission. Hold and escalate to title lead, controller, tax collector, CBP/import specialist, or FLHSMV if customs, EPA, DOT, ownership, VIN, or compliance documents are missing or unclear.
