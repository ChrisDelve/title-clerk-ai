FLHSMV TL-18
APPLICATION FOR CERTIFICATE OF TITLE AND SATISFACTION OF LIENS INVOLVING REGISTERED OWNERS OR LIENHOLDERS WHO ARE DECEASED

PRIMARY RULE:
Deceased owner transfers MUST first be classified into the correct TL-18 scenario before determining required documents or processing steps.

INITIAL INTAKE QUESTIONS:
1. Is the title available?
2. Is the title Florida or out-of-state?
3. Is ownership single or joint?
4. If joint, are names connected by:
   - AND
   - OR
   - AND with Rights of Survivorship
5. Were the owners married?
6. Is there a will?
7. Has probate been opened?
8. Are Letters of Administration available?
9. Is there a court order, summary administration, or exempt property order?
10. Is the title electronic?
11. Are there active liens?
12. Is there a surviving spouse?
13. Is trust ownership involved?
14. Is a successor trustee involved?

TL-18 SCENARIO ROUTING:

SCENARIO 1:
Single ownership.
Estate probated.
Letters of Administration issued.

IF TITLE AVAILABLE:
- Personal representative signs title as seller.
- Transfer may go to purchaser or Estate of Deceased.
- HSMV 82040 MV / VS / MH required.
- Letters of Administration required.
- Lien satisfactions required if applicable.
- Sales tax or exemption required.
- Tag or non-use affidavit required.
- Title fees required.

IF TITLE NOT AVAILABLE:
- HSMV 82101 completed by personal representative.
- Duplicate title issued in deceased owner's name first.
- Then follow title available process.

SCENARIO 2:
Joint ownership connected by AND.
Not married couple.
Estate administered.

- Personal representative AND surviving co-owner must sign.
- Letters of Administration required.

SCENARIO 3:
Joint ownership connected by OR.

- Surviving co-owner receives full rights to vehicle.
- Probate may not be required.
- Death certificate required.
- Surviving owner may transfer directly.

SCENARIO 4:
AND with Rights of Survivorship.

- Surviving co-owner automatically receives ownership rights.
- Death certificate required.
- Surviving co-owner may transfer title directly.

SCENARIO 5:
Married couple.
Tenancy by the Entirety.

- Surviving spouse receives ownership rights.
- Death certificate required.
- Surviving spouse may transfer title.

SCENARIO 6:
Estate not probated.
Single ownership.

IF SURVIVING SPOUSE EXISTS:
- Surviving spouse may transfer title without taking title first in some situations.

IF NO SURVIVING SPOUSE:
- Heirs must apply for title.
- Release of Heirs section required.

SCENARIO 7:
Out-of-state title.

- Follow deceased-owner procedure PLUS out-of-state title requirements.
- VIN verification required.
- Ownership verification required.

SCENARIO 8:
Trustee deceased.

- Successor trustee authority must be verified.
- Trust agreement required.
- Death certificate for trustee required.

HIGH RISK ESCALATION:
- Ownership disputes
- Missing probate authority
- Missing heirs
- Fraud concerns
- VIN mismatch
- Active lien complications
- Missing trust authority
- Conflicting wills
- Missing death certificate
- Multiple deceased owners

CRITICAL RULES:
- Powers of attorney become void upon death.
- Electronic titles may require printing before transfer.
- Lien status must always be verified.
- Exact ownership conjunction controls transfer authority.
- Probate status changes required documents.
- Surviving spouse transfers may qualify for no-fee transfer.
