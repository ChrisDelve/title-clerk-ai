FLORIDA TITLE PROCEDURE — TRANSFER OF CERTIFICATE OF TITLE INVOLVING FORECLOSURE PROCEEDINGS
PROCEDURE: TL-51
CATEGORY: FORECLOSURE / COURT ORDER TITLES / MOBILE HOME TITLING

==================================================
PROCEDURE SUMMARY
==================================================

This procedure governs:
- Foreclosure title transfers
- Court ordered title transfers
- Mobile home foreclosure titling
- Judicial foreclosure processing
- Clerk of Court Certificate of Title processing
- Foreclosure ownership transfers
- Court-issued title documents

Applies primarily to:
- Mobile homes
- Judicial foreclosure proceedings
- Court ordered ownership transfers

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FORECLOSURE RULE
==================================================

Foreclosure:
Is:
- Judicial procedure.

Meaning:
- Court ordered.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT REPOSSESSION RULE
==================================================

Repossession:
Is:
- Non-judicial procedure.

Meaning:
- NOT court ordered.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CERTIFICATE OF TITLE RULE
==================================================

After foreclosure sale:
Clerk of Court issues:
- "Certificate of Title"

This document:
Functions as:
- Court ownership transfer document.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT SALE TIMELINE RULE
==================================================

Court directs:
Public sale:
- Minimum 20 days after judgment
- Maximum 35 days after judgment.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PUBLICATION RULE
==================================================

Notice of sale:
Must be published:
- Once per week
- For 2 consecutive weeks.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT SECOND PUBLICATION RULE
==================================================

Second publication:
Must occur:
- At least 5 days before sale.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CERTIFICATE OF SALE RULE
==================================================

After sale:
Clerk files:
- Certificate of Sale.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 10-DAY OBJECTION RULE
==================================================

If no objections filed:
Within:
- 10 days

Clerk then files:
- Certificate of Title.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FORECLOSURE TITLE DOCUMENT RULE
==================================================

Court-issued Certificate of Title:
Looks similar to:
- Court order.

Contains:
- Property description
- Mobile home description
- Buyer information
- Filing/recording information

==================================================
DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
==================================================

Need:
- HSMV 82040 or applicable version
- Court-issued Certificate of Title copy
- Sales tax documentation
- Mobile home decal or RP decal
- Title fees

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PHOTOCOPY RULE
==================================================

Need:
- Photocopy of court-issued Certificate of Title.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CERTIFICATE CONTENT RULE
==================================================

Certificate of Title copy must show:
- Year
- Make
- Identification number
- Filing/recording information

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT LIEN SATISFACTION RULE
==================================================

Lien satisfaction:
NOT required:
For:
- Recorded liens in foreclosure proceeding.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TAX RULE
==================================================

Need:
- Florida sales tax
OR
- Sales tax exemption information.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MOBILE HOME DECAL RULE
==================================================

Need:
- Mobile home decal
OR
- RP decal.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NON-USE AFFIDAVIT RULE
==================================================

Non-use affidavit:
NOT acceptable:
For:
- Mobile homes.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT COURT ORDER HEADING RULE
==================================================

Foreclosure court order should contain heading:
- "Certificate of Title"

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FORECLOSURE VS REPO RULE
==================================================

Foreclosure:
- Court process

Repossession:
- Contract/lender process.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CLERK CERTIFICATION RULE
==================================================

Certificate confirms:
- Certificate of sale filed
AND
- No objections filed within required period.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FORECLOSURE BUYER RULE
==================================================

Highest bidder:
Receives:
- Court-issued ownership rights.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TITLE SOURCE RULE
==================================================

Ownership transfer authority comes from:
- Court foreclosure process

NOT:
- Traditional reassignment title.

AI DECISION LOGIC
==================================================

AI should determine:
1. Is transaction foreclosure related?
2. Is it judicial foreclosure?
3. Is mobile home involved?
4. Is Certificate of Title from court included?
5. Is document from Clerk of Court?
6. Is lien satisfaction required?
7. Are sales taxes documented?
8. Is proper HSMV form used?
9. Is mobile home decal/RP decal included?
10. Is title fee included?
11. Is foreclosure complete?
12. Is there Florida title record?
13. Is there out-of-state lien?
14. Was foreclosure processed through court?
15. Is non-use affidavit improperly submitted?

==================================================
COMMON TITLE CLERK ERRORS
==================================================

- Treating foreclosure like repossession
- Asking for lien satisfaction unnecessarily
- Accepting non-use affidavit for mobile home
- Missing Clerk of Court Certificate of Title
- Using wrong HSMV form
- Missing RP/mobile home decal
- Failing to verify court-issued title
- Confusing Certificate of Sale with Certificate of Title
- Missing recording/book/page information
- Incorrectly processing mobile home foreclosure

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FORECLOSURE DOCUMENT FLOW
==================================================

Typical flow:
1. Court foreclosure judgment
2. Public sale
3. Certificate of Sale issued
4. 10-day objection period
5. Certificate of Title issued
6. Applicant submits to tax collector
7. Florida title issued
