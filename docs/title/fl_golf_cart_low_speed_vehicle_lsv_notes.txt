PURPOSE
============================================================

This file helps a Florida dealership answer questions about whether a golf cart can be titled and registered in Florida.

Use this file for questions like:

- Customer wants to title/register a golf cart.

============================================================
CORE DISTINCTION
============================================================

Do not treat every golf cart as title/register eligible.

First determine whether the vehicle is:

1. A golf cart
2. A low-speed vehicle, LSV
3. A golf cart modified/converted to a low-speed vehicle

A standard golf cart and a low-speed vehicle are not the same for title/registration purposes.

============================================================
DEFINITIONS
============================================================

GOLF CART:

Florida Statute 320.01 defines golf cart as a motor vehicle designed and manufactured for operation on a golf course for sporting or recreational purposes and not capable of exceeding 20 miles per hour.

LOW-SPEED VEHICLE / LSV:

Florida Statute 320.01 defines low-speed vehicle as a four-wheeled vehicle whose top speed is greater than 20 miles per hour but not greater than 25 miles per hour, including neighborhood electric vehicles.

Low-speed vehicles must comply with federal safety standards in 49 C.F.R. 571.500 and Florida LSV requirements.

============================================================
CONVERTED GOLF CART TO LSV WORKFLOW
============================================================

FLHSMV guidance says the converted golf cart must be street-legal before applying for title and registration.

Before titling/registering a converted golf cart:
- The converted golf cart must be inspected.
- The converted golf cart must be assigned a VIN at a Motorist Services Regional Office.
- The applicant must present photographs of the front, back, and both sides of the converted vehicle.
- The converted golf cart must be trailered to a Motorist Services Regional Office.
- Documents and fees must be presented for inspection, VIN assignment, title, and registration.

Required/review items listed by FLHSMV low-speed vehicle guidance include:
- Manufacturer’s Certificate of Origin or bill of sale for the golf cart
- HSMV 84490 Statement of Builder, completed by customer and Compliance Examiner/Inspector
- HSMV 86064 Affidavit for Golf Cart Modified to a Low Speed Vehicle
- Original bills of sale or receipts for all parts used to convert the golf cart to a low-speed vehicle
- Certified weight slip for the converted golf cart
- HSMV 82040 Application for Certificate of Title With/Without Registration
- Inspection / VIN assignment by Motorist Services Regional Office
- Applicable title, registration, and fees

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

When a customer asks to title/register a golf cart:

1. Classify the vehicle first:
   - Is it a standard golf cart?
   - Is it already titled as a low-speed vehicle?
   - Was it converted from golf cart to LSV?
   - What is its top speed?
   - Does it have a VIN?
   - Does it meet federal/FMVSS and Florida LSV requirements?

2. If standard golf cart:
   - Do not default to title/registration.
   - Explain that standard golf carts are not handled the same as LSVs.
   - Review local roadway/ordinance rules if customer asks about road use.
   - Escalate if customer insists it should be titled/tagged.

3. If already an LSV:
   - Verify existing title or MCO/MSO.
   - Verify VIN.
   - Complete/review HSMV 82040.
   - Verify Florida insurance if registering.
   - Process as title/registration if all LSV requirements are met.

4. If converted golf cart:
   - Do not submit as a normal title transaction.
   - Confirm street-legal conversion.
   - Send/refer for Motorist Services Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment.
   - Require photos, HSMV 84490, HSMV 86064, conversion part receipts, certified weight slip, proof of ownership, HSMV 82040, and applicable fees.
   - Escalate before submission if classification or conversion documents are unclear.

============================================================
REJECTION / DELAY RISKS
============================================================

High risk if:

- App treats standard golf cart as automatically title/register eligible.
- App fails to distinguish golf cart from LSV.
- Top speed is unknown.
- Vehicle has no VIN.
- Vehicle is not street-legal.
- Converted golf cart has not been inspected by Motorist Services Regional Office.
- VIN assignment has not occurred.
- Required photos are missing.
- HSMV 86064 is missing.
- HSMV 84490 is missing.
- Conversion part receipts are missing.
- Certified weight slip is missing.
- HSMV 82040 is missing for LSV title application.
- Florida insurance is missing when registration/plate issuance is requested.
- Customer believes local golf cart road authorization equals state title/registration eligibility.
- Dealership tries to process converted golf cart as normal motor vehicle without Regional Office inspection/VIN assignment.

============================================================
HARD WORDING RULE — CONVERTED GOLF CART
============================================================

For converted golf cart questions, use this exact wording:

For a converted golf cart, Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment are required before title/registration.

Do not soften this to:
- “if VIN assignment is needed”
- “VIN verification or assignment if applicable”
- “coordinate with Regional Office if needed”

Converted golf cart workflow requires Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment before title/registration.

For golf cart questions, do not answer as if all golf carts are title/register eligible.

Answer should start by saying:

“First determine whether this is a standard golf cart, an existing low-speed vehicle, or a golf cart converted to a low-speed vehicle.”

Do not say:
- “Golf carts typically require registration if operated on public roads.”
- “If exempt from titling, only registration may be required.”
- “Issue a golf cart decal or plate as required by Florida law.”

Instead say:
- A standard golf cart is not the same as an LSV.
- A standard golf cart is not processed like a normal titled/registered motor vehicle.
- An LSV or converted golf cart may require title/registration if it meets LSV requirements.
- Converted golf carts must be street-legal before applying for title/registration.
- Converted golf carts require Motorist Services Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment before title/registration.
- For a converted golf cart, Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment are required before title/registration.
- Escalate if classification, VIN, top speed, street-legal status, or conversion documents are unclear.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For a customer wanting to title/register a golf cart in Florida, first determine whether it is a standard golf cart, an existing low-speed vehicle, or a golf cart converted to a low-speed vehicle. A standard golf cart is not the same as an LSV and should not be treated as automatically title/register eligible. If it is an LSV, verify title/MCO/MSO, VIN, HSMV 82040, Florida insurance if registering, and that it meets LSV requirements. If it is a converted golf cart, it must be street-legal before applying for title/registration. For a converted golf cart, Regional Office inspection and VIN assignment are required before title/registration. Review photos of all four sides, MCO or bill of sale, HSMV 84490 Statement of Builder, HSMV 86064 Affidavit for Golf Cart Modified to a Low Speed Vehicle, original receipts for conversion parts, certified weight slip, HSMV 82040, and applicable fees. Hold and escalate if classification, top speed, VIN, street-legal status, inspection, or conversion documents are unclear.
