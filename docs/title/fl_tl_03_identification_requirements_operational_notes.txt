FLORIDA TITLE & LIEN PROCEDURE
CERTIFIED OR PLAIN COPIES OF SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS
SOURCE: TL-03
REVISION DATE: 03/28/2024

PURPOSE:
This procedure governs when photocopies, certified copies, faxed copies, translations, and supporting legal documents may be accepted with Florida title applications.

==================================================
CORE OPERATIONAL RULE
==================================================

ALWAYS DETERMINE:
1. Is the document:
   - Original
   - Photocopy
   - Certified copy
   - Faxed copy
   - Foreign document

2. Does certification matter?
3. Is translation required?
4. Is clerk required to physically inspect original?

==================================================
POA COPY ACCEPTANCE RULES
==================================================

A photocopy of:
- HSMV 82053
- General POA
- Durable POA

MAY BE ACCEPTED IF:
The clerk views the original or certified copy during processing.

CUSTOMER MAY:
- Retain original
- Submit photocopy

CLERK MAY:
- Make photocopy for submission

==================================================
WHO MAY CERTIFY POA COPIES
==================================================

ACCEPTABLE CERTIFIERS:
1. Notary Public
2. Clerk of Court
3. Active military commissioned officer
4. LNC / Legalman Chief Petty Officer (E-7)

IMPORTANT:
Notary cannot certify public record copies prohibited by statute.

==================================================
FOREIGN DOCUMENT RULES
==================================================

IF DOCUMENTS OR TITLES ARE NOT IN ENGLISH:

REQUIRED:
- Full English translation
- Translator signature
- Notarized translation
- Certification translation is accurate

TRANSLATOR MUST:
- State fluency/knowledge
- Translate professionally/regularly
- Have NO ownership interest in vehicle

==================================================
FOREIGN CERTIFICATION ACCEPTANCE
==================================================

ACCEPTABLE FOREIGN CERTIFIERS:
- Judge
- Justice
- Minister
- Consul General
- Charge d'affaires
- US Consul

==================================================
COURT ORDER REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

COURT ORDER MUST INCLUDE:
- Court name
- Case number
- Parties involved
- Order language/body
- Judge identification
- Date entered
- Vehicle identification information

ACCEPTABLE:
Photocopy of court order.

==================================================
DOCUMENTS NOT REQUIRING CERTIFICATION
==================================================

Photocopies/faxes acceptable WITHOUT certification for:
- Court Orders
- Replevin
- Bankruptcy
- Letters of Administration
- Death Certificates
- Wills
- Vessel Security Agreements
- Trust Agreements

==================================================
FAX RULES
==================================================

NOT ACCEPTABLE:
Completed HSMV forms faxed with copied signatures.

ACCEPTABLE:
Blank HSMV forms faxed first, then completed with original signatures.

==================================================
MULTI-VEHICLE POA RULE
==================================================

IF ONE POA COVERS MULTIPLE VEHICLES:
- Photocopy may accompany each transaction set.
- Clerk must reference first title number on subsequent files.

BEST PRACTICE:
Document linkage in issuance system comments.

==================================================
DEALERSHIP OPERATIONAL GUIDANCE
==================================================

ALWAYS VERIFY:
- Clerk viewed original when required
- Certification language present
- Translation notarized if foreign
- VIN/title data complete
- Judge/order information complete
- Faxed forms contain original signatures

==================================================
COMMON PROCESSING SCENARIOS
==================================================

SCENARIO:
Customer keeps original POA
→ Clerk views original and submits photocopy.

SCENARIO:
Foreign probate documents
→ Require notarized English translation.

SCENARIO:
Faxed signed HSMV form
→ Reject.

SCENARIO:
Faxed blank HSMV form later signed originally
→ Acceptable.

SCENARIO:
Estate processing
→ Letters of Administration photocopy acceptable.

SCENARIO:
Court ordered title transfer
→ Court order photocopy acceptable if required data included.

==================================================
RED FLAGS
==================================================

- Missing VIN information
- Missing translator certification
- Faxed copied signatures
- No proof original viewed
- Foreign document without notarized translation
- Court order missing judge identification
- Multi-vehicle POA without cross-reference title numbers

==================================================
BEST PRACTICE
==================================================

Before accepting supporting documents:
1. Confirm certification requirements.
2. Verify original inspection requirements.
3. Verify translation compliance.
4. Verify signatures and notarization.
5. Verify supporting legal authority.
6. Verify complete vehicle identification.
