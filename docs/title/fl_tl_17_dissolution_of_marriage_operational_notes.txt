FLORIDA TITLE & LIEN PROCEDURE
DISSOLUTION OF MARRIAGE / DIVORCE TITLE TRANSFERS
SOURCE: TL-17
REVISION DATE: 06/26/2023

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

This procedure governs:
- Divorce-related title transfers
- Court ordered ownership transfers
- Motor vehicle title transfers after dissolution
- Mobile home title transfers after dissolution
- Vessel title transfers after dissolution

==================================================
LEGAL AUTHORITY
==================================================

Section 319.28(1)(a) and (2)(a), F.S.
Transfer by operation of law.

Section 328.01(3)(b), F.S.
Application for certificate of title.

Florida Rule of Civil Procedure 1.570(d)
Court judgment acts as conveyance document.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

A divorce decree MAY act as:
- Transfer document
- Conveyance document
- Ownership assignment

WITHOUT spouse signature
IF wording properly conveys ownership.

==================================================
GENERAL RULE
==================================================

Court order must specifically identify:
- Vehicle
- Mobile home
- Vessel

==================================================
IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Court order should reference:
- VIN/HIN
- Title number
- Year
- Make
- Model

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

If VIN/HIN missing:
Other identifying characteristics
must clearly identify property.

==================================================
CONVEYANCE LANGUAGE RULE
==================================================

Acceptable ownership wording includes:
- "Shall have sole and separate property"
- "Exclusive ownership and possession"
- "Vehicle shall belong to..."
- "Awarded to..."

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

If decree properly conveys ownership:
Other spouse signature NOT required.

==================================================
DEALERSHIP INTAKE QUESTIONS
==================================================

ALWAYS ASK:
1. Do you have title?
2. Florida title or out-of-state?
3. Has spouse signed title?
4. Will spouse sign title?
5. Did decree award vehicle?
6. Do you have decree/court order?

==================================================
FLORIDA TITLE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

When previously titled in Florida:

Required:
- Florida title
OR
- Lost title certification on HSMV 82040

- Divorce decree/court order
- HSMV 82040
- Lien satisfactions if applicable
- Sales tax information
- Registration/non-use affidavit
- Title fees

==================================================
COURT ORDER PAGE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Only these pages required:
- First page
- Conveyance page
- Judge signature page

Entire decree NOT required.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Customer applying:
Must be person awarded ownership.

==================================================
LIEN RULE
==================================================

All liens must be:
- Satisfied
OR
- Properly transferred

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT LIEN RULE
==================================================

If lienholder refuses to release title:
Transfer CANNOT occur.

==================================================
TRANSFER OF EQUITY RULE
==================================================

Transfer of Equity allowed:
Florida title transactions ONLY.

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE TITLE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

When previously titled out-of-state:

Required:
- Out-of-state title
OR
- Ownership/lien verification

==================================================
ACCEPTABLE OWNERSHIP VERIFICATION
==================================================

Acceptable proof:
- State printout
- State letterhead verification
- Photocopy of title
- Law enforcement teletype verification

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE VIN RULE
==================================================

Out-of-state vehicles:
Require VIN verification.

==================================================
ACCEPTABLE VIN VERIFICATION
==================================================

Use:
- HSMV 82042
OR
- Section 8 of HSMV 82040

==================================================
VIN EXEMPTIONS
==================================================

VIN verification NOT required for:
- Mobile homes
- Vessels
- Travel trailers
- Camping trailers
- Truck campers
- Fifth-wheel recreation trailers
- Trailers under 2,000 lbs
- New dealer vehicles

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE LIEN RULE
==================================================

Transfer of Equity:
NOT allowed on out-of-state titles.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Out-of-state title with lien:
Must satisfy prior lien first.

==================================================
REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Must provide:
- Florida plate transfer
OR
- New registration
OR
- Non-use affidavit

==================================================
MOBILE HOME RULE
==================================================

Non-use affidavit:
NOT acceptable for mobile homes.

==================================================
VESSEL RULE
==================================================

Vessel transfers require:
- Florida registration transfer
OR
- New registration
OR
- Non-use affidavit

==================================================
TITLE APPLICATION RULE
==================================================

Use updated forms:

Motor Vehicles:
HSMV 82040 MV

Vessels:
HSMV 82040 VS

Mobile Homes:
HSMV 82040 MH

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Effective 2024:
Old HSMV 82040 no longer accepted.

==================================================
COURT ORDER COMPONENTS
==================================================

Court order must contain:
- Court name
- Parties involved
- Case number
- Order type
- Vehicle identification
- Conveyance wording
- Judge signature
- Entry date

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT ERROR AREA
==================================================

BIGGEST DEALERSHIP MISTAKE:
Court order does NOT properly
award ownership.

==================================================
DEALERSHIP HIGH-RISK ERROR AREAS
==================================================

MOST COMMON ERRORS:
- Missing conveyance wording
- Wrong spouse applying
- Missing judge signature page
- VIN/HIN mismatch
- Unsatisfied lien
- Missing VIN verification
- Transfer of Equity attempted on out-of-state title
- Missing registration transfer
- Missing sales tax information
- Incomplete court order pages
- No proof of ownership
- Wrong HSMV 82040 version
- Mobile home non-use affidavit submitted
- Lienholder refusing release

==================================================
WORKFLOW BEST PRACTICE
==================================================

Before processing:

1. Verify ownership awarded
2. Verify decree wording
3. Verify VIN/HIN match
4. Verify title status
5. Verify lien status
6. Verify customer identity
7. Verify Florida vs out-of-state
8. Verify VIN inspection if required
9. Verify correct HSMV form
10. Verify sales tax section
11. Verify registration transfer
12. Verify title fees
13. Verify judge signature page
14. Verify conveyance page included

==================================================
DEALERSHIP DECISION LOGIC
==================================================

IF:
Florida title +
Proper decree wording +
No lien issue

THEN:
Process without spouse signature.

--------------------------------------------------

IF:
Out-of-state title +
Outstanding lien

THEN:
Prior lien must be satisfied first.

--------------------------------------------------

IF:
Court order vague or unclear

THEN:
Reject until clarified.

==================================================

DIVORCE DECREE:
Court order granting divorce.

PETITIONER:
Spouse initiating divorce.

RESPONDENT:
Other spouse.
