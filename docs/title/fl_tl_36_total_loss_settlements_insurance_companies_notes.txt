==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT DEFINITIONS
==================================================

CERTIFICATE OF DESTRUCTION:
Authorizes:
- Dismantling
- Destruction
- Parts/scrap use only

INDEPENDENT ENTITY:
Business temporarily storing damaged vehicles under agreement with insurance company and engaged in resale/sale of damaged vehicles.

LATE MODEL VEHICLE:
- 7 model years old or newer

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TOTAL LOSS RULES
==================================================

INSURED VEHICLE:
Total loss exists when insurance company pays to replace vehicle.

UNINSURED/SELF-INSURED VEHICLE:
Total loss exists when repair cost:
- 80% or more
of replacement value.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 90% RULE
==================================================

Certificate of destruction REQUIRED when:
- Vehicle is 7 years old or newer
AND
- Retail value at least $7,500
AND
- Repair cost equals or exceeds 90% of retail value.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MOBILE HOME RULE
==================================================

Certificate of destruction REQUIRED when:
- Mobile home repair cost is 80% or more of retail value.

EXCEPTION:
Not applicable if mobile home worth less than:
- $1,500 retail before damage.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT OWNER RETAINED RULE
==================================================

If owner keeps vehicle after insurance payout:
Owner may directly apply for:
- Salvage rebuildable
OR
- Certificate of destruction.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 72-HOUR RULE
==================================================

Insurance company must submit:
- Application and title

Within:
- 72 hours
after receiving title from owner.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TITLE BRAND RULE
==================================================

Once salvage issued:
Brand follows vehicle:
- For entire lifecycle.

==================================================
SALVAGE REBUILDABLE VS COD
==================================================

REBUILDABLE:
Vehicle may eventually return to road after rebuild process.

CERTIFICATE OF DESTRUCTION:
Vehicle:
- Cannot be rebuilt for road use.
- Parts/scrap only.

==================================================
WHEN COD REQUIRED
==================================================

Certificate of destruction required when:
1. 90%+ damage rule applies
2. Vehicle only useful for parts/scrap
3. Out-of-state title says:
   - junk
   - nonrepairable
   - dismantling only
4. Insurance company chooses COD voluntarily

==================================================
WHEN REBUILDABLE ALLOWED
==================================================

Rebuildable allowed when:
- Vehicle under $7,500 OR
- Older than 7 model years
AND
- Not damaged to parts/scrap-only condition.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT OWNER/SELF-INSURED RULE
==================================================

Owner/self-insured may apply for rebuildable when:
- Vehicle is 7 years or newer
- Worth at least $7,500
- Damage is 80% to 89%

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT WHEELCHAIR VAN RULE
==================================================

Insurance company may request:
- Insurance-declared total loss
instead of COD when:
- Vehicle has wheelchair modifications
AND
- Repairable safely.

==================================================
TITLE BRANDS
==================================================

Possible brands:
- Salvage Rebuildable
- Salvage Rebuildable Flood
- Salvage Theft
- Insurance Declared Total Loss
- Total Loss
- Certificate of Destruction

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TOTAL LOSS BRAND RULE
==================================================

If insurance repair cost exceeds:
- 100% of vehicle value

Owner may request:
- "Total Loss" brand

This:
- DOES NOT create salvage title.
- Creates regular title with Total Loss brand.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT SALVAGE THEFT RULE
==================================================

Salvage Theft issued when:
- Vehicle stolen
AND
- Insurance company pays total loss.

==================================================
SALVAGE THEFT RECOVERY RULE
==================================================

If recovered substantially intact:
Vehicle may return to normal title flow.

If NOT substantially intact:
Must process:
- Rebuildable
OR
- Certificate of destruction.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT VIN VERIFICATION RULES
==================================================

VIN verification commonly required using:
- HSMV 82042

Verification may be completed by:
- Florida notary
- Licensed dealer
- Law enforcement
- Military police
- Compliance examiner
- Tax collector employee

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT COD VIN RULE
==================================================

If applying for COD using out-of-state title:
VIN verification required.

==================================================
VIN VERIFICATION EXEMPTIONS
==================================================

VIN verification NOT required for:
- Mobile homes
- Trailers under 2,000 lbs
- Travel trailers
- Camping trailers
- Truck campers
- Fifth-wheel recreation trailers

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT LOST TITLE RULE
==================================================

If Florida title unavailable:
Use:
- HSMV 82101

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT ELECTRONIC TITLE RULE
==================================================

If Florida electronic title:
Use:
- HSMV 82994
to transfer interest to insurance company.

==================================================
MAIN FORMS USED
==================================================

PRIMARY FORMS:
- HSMV 82363
- HSMV 82042
- HSMV 82101
- HSMV 82040 MV
- HSMV 82040 MH
- HSMV 82040 VS

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT ELECTRONIC SIGNATURE RULE
==================================================

Electronic signature process allowed for:
- Certificate of destruction only
when title:
- Lost
OR
- Not available.

==================================================
E-SIGN PROCESS
==================================================

Insurance company:
1. Sends HSMV 82053 to owner
2. Owner electronically signs
3. Insurance company completes:
   - HSMV 82101
   - HSMV 82363
   - HSMV 82052
4. Tax collector processes COD.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT INDEPENDENT ENTITY RULE
==================================================

Independent entity:
- NOT towing company
- NOT repair facility

Must notify owner vehicle available for pickup.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 30-DAY NOTICE RULE
==================================================

Independent entity must:
- Send certified notice to owner

Owner has:
- 30 days
to retrieve vehicle.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NMVTIS RULE
==================================================

Insurance companies:
Must notify NMVTIS.

Independent entities:
Must provide proof of NMVTIS reporting.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT STORAGE FEE RULE
==================================================

Independent entity:
CANNOT:
- Charge storage fees
- Apply for title under towing/storage statutes.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RECORD RETENTION RULE
==================================================

Independent entity must keep:
- Notice records
- NMVTIS records
- Delivery attempts

For:
- 3 years minimum.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT ATTEMPTED DELIVERY RULE
==================================================

Attempted delivery:
- Counts as valid notice attempt.

Must have:
- Tracking proof
- Delivery timeline.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT REASSIGNMENT RULE
==================================================

Rebuildable salvage titles:
May be reassigned:
- Dealer to dealer multiple times.

COD:
Maximum:
- 2 reassignments.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NON-USE RULE
==================================================

Non-use affidavit:
Allowed for motor vehicles.

NOT allowed for:
- Mobile homes.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PENALTY RULE
==================================================

Title transfer required:
Within:
- 30 days
from transfer date.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FEMA RULE
==================================================

FEMA:
Does not need title issued in its name.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CALIFORNIA RULE
==================================================

California salvage TITLE:
Already rebuilt.

California salvage CERTIFICATE:
NOT rebuilt.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MISSING VIN RULE
==================================================

Missing VIN plate:
Vehicle may be:
- Contraband
- Subject to forfeiture.

If COD being issued:
Replacement VIN decal:
- NOT required.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT REBUILD RULE
==================================================

If dealer rebuilds rebuildable vehicle:
Dealer MUST:
- Obtain rebuilt title
before sale.

==================================================
COMMON TITLE CLERK ERRORS
==================================================

- Wrong salvage classification
- Missing VIN verification
- Missing lien satisfactions
- Incorrect 90% determination
- Processing rebuildable instead of COD
- Missing owner retained designation
- Missing odometer statement
- Incorrect salvage theft handling
- Missing NMVTIS proof
- Accepting invalid reassignment
- Forgetting title brand carry-forward

==================================================
AI DECISION LOGIC
==================================================

AI should determine:
1. Is vehicle insured or uninsured?
2. Is total loss threshold met?
3. Is vehicle late model?
4. Is retail value above $7,500?
5. Is repair estimate 80%-89% or 90%+?
6. Is rebuildable allowed?
7. Is COD mandatory?
8. Is owner retaining salvage?
9. Is salvage theft involved?
10. Is VIN verification required?
11. Is title available?
12. Is NMVTIS reporting required?
13. Is independent entity involved?
14. Is wheelchair exemption applicable?
15. Is electronic title involved?
