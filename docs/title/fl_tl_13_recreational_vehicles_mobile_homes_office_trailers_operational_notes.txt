FLORIDA TITLE & LIEN PROCEDURE
RECREATIONAL VEHICLES, MOBILE HOMES, AND OFFICE TRAILERS
SOURCE: TL-13
REVISION DATE: 06/26/2023

==================================================
PURPOSE
==================================================

This procedure governs:
- Recreational vehicle title requirements
- Mobile home title requirements
- Office trailer title requirements
- RV conversions
- Van conversions
- Tiny home trailer handling
- Park trailer classifications
- Truck conversion RV rules

==================================================
IMPORTANT RV CLASSIFICATIONS
==================================================

Travel Trailer = TV
Camping Trailer = CA
Motor Home = MH
Private Motor Coach = PC
Van Conversion = VC
Mobile Home = HS
Office Trailer = OT
Park Trailer = PT
Pickup Truck Mounted Camper = PM

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Slide-in campers:
NOT titled.

Add-a-rooms:
NOT titled.

Port-a-rooms:
NOT titled.

==================================================
TRAILER WEIGHT RULE
==================================================

Trailers under 2,000 lbs:
No Florida title required.

Trailers 2,000 lbs or more:
Must be titled.

==================================================
TINY HOME RULE
==================================================

Tiny homes built on trailers:
Usually NOT HUD or ANSI compliant.

If trailer weighs 2,000 lbs or more:
Must title as trailer.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Tiny home itself:
May NOT qualify as mobile home or RV.

Often only trailer gets titled.

==================================================
RV BODY TYPE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

TV = rigid travel trailer
CA = folding camper
MH = self-propelled RV
PC = private motor coach
VC = van RV conversion
HS = mobile home
OT = office trailer
PT = park trailer
PM = pickup with mounted camper

==================================================
OFFICE TRAILER RULE
==================================================

Office trailer weight:
Length × Width × 20 lbs.

==================================================
MOTOR HOME CONVERSION RULE
==================================================

Converted van/motor home:
Requires second-stage MCO.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Second-stage VIN:
Must match first-stage VIN.

==================================================
RV CONVERSION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Converted recreational vehicle must contain:
- 110v wiring
OR
- LP gas piping
OR
- Plumbing system

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Second-stage manufacturer:
Must be licensed and bonded RV manufacturer.

==================================================
MODEL YEAR RULE
==================================================

1981 and newer motor homes:
Use second-stage MCO year.

Even if NMVTIS differs.

==================================================
TITLE ONLY RULE
==================================================

Title-only RV applications:
Require dead storage affidavit
if not registered.

==================================================
CONVERSION AFFIDAVIT RULE
==================================================

Owner-built RV conversion:
Requires affidavit stating installed:
- 110v electrical
OR
- LP gas piping
OR
- Plumbing system

==================================================
BROKER RULE
==================================================

RV brokers:
Do NOT appear in title chain.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

RV brokers:
Still considered dealers
for title processing purposes.

==================================================
HORSE TRAILER RULE
==================================================

Horse trailer with living quarters:
Classified as travel trailer.

==================================================
HEAVY TRUCK CONVERSION RULE
==================================================

Heavy truck may become RV IF:
- Used only for personal effects
AND
- Meets RV definition
AND
- NO fifth wheel connection

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

If truck conversion has fifth wheel:
Cannot title as RV.

==================================================
TRUCK TRACTOR RULE
==================================================

Truck tractors:
CANNOT convert into RVs.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Truck tractor with fifth wheel:
Must remain truck tractor.

==================================================
AUCTION RULE
==================================================

Motor vehicle dealers:
May dispose of RV trade-ins at auction.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

Motor vehicle dealer:
Cannot buy RV for resale
unless properly licensed as RV dealer.

==================================================
NEW 82040 FORM RULE
==================================================

New forms:
- HSMV 82040 MV
- HSMV 82040 VS
- HSMV 82040 MH

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RULE
==================================================

As of 2024:
Motor vehicles and mobile homes
must use updated 82040 forms.

==================================================
DEALERSHIP HIGH-RISK ERROR AREAS
==================================================

MOST COMMON ERRORS:
- Titling slide-in campers
- Wrong RV body type
- Wrong trailer classification
- Missing second-stage MCO
- Wrong VIN usage
- RV conversion without affidavit
- Titling truck tractors as RVs
- Missing dead storage affidavit
- Wrong weight calculations
- Tiny home misclassification
- Fifth-wheel conversion mistakes
