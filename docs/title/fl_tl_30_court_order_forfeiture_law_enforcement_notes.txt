FLORIDA TITLE PROCEDURE — COURT ORDER OF FORFEITURE TO LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY
PROCEDURE: TL-30
CATEGORY: LAW ENFORCEMENT / OPERATION OF LAW TITLE
RELATED STATUTES:
- Sections 932.701–7055 F.S.
- Section 319.33(7)(a) F.S.

==================================================
PROCEDURE SUMMARY
==================================================

This procedure explains title requirements for:
- Confiscated vehicles
- Forfeited vehicles
- Contraband vehicles
- Law enforcement seizure cases
- Court ordered forfeiture
- Public auction forfeiture sales

Applies when:
- Vehicle/mobile home confiscated by law enforcement
- Court enters forfeiture order
- Agency retains vehicle
OR
- Vehicle sold at auction

==================================================
CORE LEGAL CONCEPTS
==================================================

CONFISCATE:
Government seizure of property.

CONTRABAND:
Illegal goods/property subject to forfeiture.

ORDER OF FORFEITURE:
Court order allowing:
- Sale of property
OR
- Title transfer to law enforcement.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT VIN RULE
==================================================

If VIN:
- Destroyed
- Removed
- Altered
- Covered
- Defaced
OR
- Real identity cannot be determined

Vehicle becomes contraband.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT COURT ORDER RULE
==================================================

If VIN/identity issue exists:
Court must order:
- FLHSMV assigned VIN
before title issuance.

==================================================
GENERAL DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Typically required:
- HSMV 82040 MV/MH/VS
- Court Order of Forfeiture
- Bill of sale (if auction)
- Lien handling documentation
- Sales tax handling
- Plate/decal handling
- Title fees

==================================================
POLICE VEHICLE DESIGNATION RULE
==================================================

If agency certifies:
- Vehicle NOT used in law enforcement
AND
- WILL NOT be used in law enforcement

Then:
"Police Vehicle" designation NOT shown on title.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FUTURE USE RULE
==================================================

If agency later uses vehicle:
Title must be surrendered and reissued showing:
- "Police Vehicle"

==================================================
FLORIDA TITLE RECORD REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

If vehicle titled in Florida:
Need:
- HSMV 82040
- Court Order of Forfeiture
- Additional supporting documents

==================================================
COURT ORDER REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Court order usually states:
- Vehicle forfeited "to the use of"
OR
- "to be sold by"
law enforcement agency.

==================================================
PUBLIC AUCTION RULE
==================================================

If sold at auction:
Purchaser submits:
- Original bill of sale
OR
- Certified copy

==================================================
BILL OF SALE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Bill of sale must:
- Be on original agency letterhead
- Include year/make/model
- Include VIN
- Include full vehicle description

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT LIEN RULES
==================================================

Liens handled several ways.

==================================================
LIEN OPTION 1 — COURT EXTINGUISHES LIEN
==================================================

If court order:
- Shows lienholder party
AND
- Satisfies/discharges lien

Then:
Clear title may issue.

==================================================
LIEN OPTION 2 — LIEN PRESERVED / AUCTION SALE
==================================================

If lien preserved:
Agency may:
- Sell vehicle at auction
to satisfy lien.

Need:
- Proof lienholder notified

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NOTICE REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Lienholder notice:
- Certified mail
- Return receipt requested

Need:
- Green receipt attached

==================================================
LIEN OPTION 3 — AGENCY RETAINS VEHICLE
==================================================

If agency keeps vehicle:
Need:
- HSMV 82260
to satisfy lien.

==================================================
LIEN OPTION 4 — LIEN CONTINUES
==================================================

If lien preserved:
Lienholder remains on:
- New title application

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT IMPLIED SATISFACTION RULE
==================================================

If:
- Court order silent about lien
AND
- Lien exists

Lien considered:
- Satisfied

==================================================
SALES TAX REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Need:
- Florida sales tax paid
OR
- Sales tax exemption/specification on HSMV 82040

==================================================
PLATE / NON-USE RULES
==================================================

Need:
- Florida plate transfer/purchase
OR
- Non-use affidavit

==================================================
LAW ENFORCEMENT NON-USE AFFIDAVIT RULE
==================================================

Agency may submit affidavit stating:
- Vehicle not used in law enforcement
- Vehicle not used on Florida highways

==================================================
MOBILE HOME RULE
==================================================

Non-use affidavit:
- NOT acceptable for mobile homes

Need:
- Mobile home decal
OR
- RP decal

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE / NO FLORIDA TITLE RECORD
==================================================

If no Florida title record:
Need:
- HSMV 82042
OR
- VIN verification section on HSMV 82040

==================================================
ALTERNATIVE VIN VERIFICATION RULE
==================================================

Instead of HSMV 82042:
May use affidavit from:
- Seller
AND
- Purchaser

Affidavit must confirm:
- VIN on vehicle matches documents

==================================================
VIN VERIFICATION EXEMPTIONS
==================================================

Verification NOT required for:
- Mobile homes
- Trailers under 2,000 lbs
- Travel trailers
- Camping trailers
- Truck campers
- Fifth-wheel recreation trailers

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT DEALER RULE
==================================================

If dealer/individual buys forfeited vehicle:
Title must issue:
- In buyer name first
before resale.

IMPORTANT OPERATIONAL NOTES
==================================================

Forfeiture titles are:
- Court-order dependent
- Lien-sensitive
- Operation-of-law transactions

Most important document:
- Court Order of Forfeiture

Court order controls:
- Ownership disposition
- Lien handling
- VIN reassignment authority
- Agency authority to sell/retain
