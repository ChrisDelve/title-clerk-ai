FLORIDA TITLE & LIEN PROCEDURE
POWER OF ATTORNEY (POA) OPERATIONAL NOTES
SOURCE: TL-02
REVISION DATE: 03/25

PURPOSE:
This procedure governs the acceptance and operational handling of powers of attorney for motor vehicle, mobile home, and vessel title transactions in Florida.

==================================================
CORE OPERATIONAL CLASSIFICATION
==================================================

FIRST DETERMINE:

1. Is the POA:
   - Limited (HSMV 82053)
   - Secure Limited (HSMV 82995)
   - General
   - Durable
   - Court Ordered / Legal Instrument

2. Is odometer disclosure required?
   - YES = May require HSMV 82995
   - NO = HSMV 82053 may be acceptable

3. Is title physically available?
   - YES
   - NO (lienholder holding title)
   - LOST

4. Is POA being used:
   - Seller side only
   - Buyer side only
   - BOTH seller and buyer

==================================================
HSMV 82053 — NON-SECURE LIMITED POA
==================================================

USE FOR:
- Title transfer
- Duplicate title
- Lien recording
- Title application signing
- Odometer disclosure ONLY when acting as:
   - Seller ONLY
   - Buyer ONLY

NOT ALLOWED:
- Cannot sign as BOTH buyer and seller for odometer disclosure.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Vehicle specific
- Must identify unit information
- Must identify attorney-in-fact by FULL NAME

ACCEPTABLE COPY RULES:
- Clerk may accept photocopy IF original/certified copy viewed.
- Clerk may photocopy original for submission.
- Notary-certified copies acceptable when allowed.

IMPORTANT:
HSMV 82053 MUST accompany transaction paperwork.

==================================================
HSMV 82995 — SECURE POA
==================================================

USE ONLY WHEN:
1. Title held by lienholder and unavailable.
2. Title lost or destroyed.

PURPOSE:
Allows attorney-in-fact to complete BOTH sides of odometer disclosure legally.

STRICT REQUIREMENTS:
- ORIGINAL FORM REQUIRED
- NO photocopies
- NO certified copies

IMPORTANT:
Cannot assign alternate attorney-in-fact.

DO NOT USE IF:
Vehicle is odometer exempt.

==================================================
ODOMETER EXEMPTIONS
==================================================

ODOMETER DISCLOSURE NOT REQUIRED IF:
- 2011 or newer AND over 20 years old
- 2010 or older AND over 10 years old
- Not self-propelled
- GVWR exceeds 16,000 lbs
- Dealer swap using MCO

IF ODOMETER EXEMPT:
Use HSMV 82053 instead of 82995.

==================================================
GENERAL / DURABLE POA
==================================================

GENERAL POA:
- Broad authority while principal alive.
- May authorize title work and lien transactions.

DURABLE POA:
- Continues after incapacity.
- Must grant authority over personal property.

EXECUTION REQUIREMENTS:
POAs issued on/after 10/01/2011 require:
- Two witnesses
- Principal signature
- Notary acknowledgement OR acceptable perjury clause

COPY RULES:
Photocopies acceptable UNLESS:
- Attorney-in-fact transferring title TO themselves.

==================================================
DEATH OF OWNER RULE
==================================================

IMPORTANT:
General, limited, or durable POA becomes INVALID upon death of principal.

DO NOT PROCESS:
Standard POA after registered owner death.

INSTEAD REQUIRE:
- Estate documents
- Letters of Administration
- Court orders
- Successor trustee documentation

==================================================
ESTATE / PERSONAL REPRESENTATIVE RULES
==================================================

Personal representative may issue:
- LIMITED POA ONLY

DO NOT ACCEPT:
- General POA from estate representative

REQUIRED:
- Letters of Administration copy

==================================================
OUT-OF-STATE POA REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

OUT-OF-STATE POA MUST INCLUDE:
- Owner printed name
- Owner signature
- DL/ID/FEID
- DOB
- Address
- VIN
- Year
- Make
- Model
- Body
- Title number

OUT-OF-STATE POA MAY BE VALID:
If compliant with originating state's law.

==================================================
LEGAL INSTRUMENTS ACCEPTABLE
==================================================

ACCEPTABLE WHEN AUTHORIZING BUSINESS AFFAIRS:
- Guardianship
- Conservatorship
- Trust agreement
- Court order

VERIFY:
Document grants authority over titled property/business affairs.

==================================================
DEALERSHIP COMPLIANCE WARNINGS
==================================================

CRITICAL RULE:
Dealership employees CANNOT act as independent third party POA signers for odometer disclosures.

NOT ALLOWED:
- Same dealer group employees
- Sister rooftop employees
- DBA crossover employees

THIRD PARTY MUST:
Have NO association with dealership.

==================================================
LIEN SATISFACTION
==================================================

General, limited, or durable POA may satisfy lien IF authority specifically granted.

==================================================
COMPANY / CORPORATE POA RULES
==================================================

Corporation may grant POA.

REQUIRED:
- Officer signature
- Official title
- Vehicle identification

IF COMPANY AUTHORIZES EMPLOYEES:
Require:
- Letterhead authorization
- Authorized signer list
- Officer signature

==================================================
FAX / COPY PRACTICAL GUIDANCE
==================================================

ALWAYS VERIFY:
- Correct form version
- Signatures
- Witnesses if required
- Notary/perjury clause if applicable
- Vehicle information complete
- Odometer requirement applicability

RED FLAGS:
- POA after owner death
- Dealer employee acting as independent third party
- Missing witnesses
- Missing authority language
- Secure POA copy submitted instead of original
- Self-transfer using photocopy POA
- Incomplete VIN/unit description

==================================================
COMMON DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW CLASSIFICATIONS
==================================================

SCENARIO:
Title unavailable due to lienholder
→ Use HSMV 82995

SCENARIO:
Duplicate title application
→ HSMV 82053 acceptable

SCENARIO:
Seller only POA signature
→ HSMV 82053 acceptable

SCENARIO:
Dealer employee signing buyer and seller odometer disclosure
→ Requires HSMV 82995 under allowed conditions

SCENARIO:
Owner deceased
→ Standard POA invalid

SCENARIO:
Durable POA from elderly customer
→ Verify witness requirement and durable authority language

==================================================
OPERATIONAL BEST PRACTICE
==================================================

Before processing ANY POA transaction:
1. Verify title availability.
2. Verify odometer disclosure requirement.
3. Verify correct POA form type.
4. Verify signer authority.
5. Verify death/incapacity status.
6. Verify original vs copy requirements.
7. Verify dealership conflict/compliance issues.
