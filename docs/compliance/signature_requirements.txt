FLORIDA SIGNATURE REQUIREMENTS

IF TITLE STATES:
AND
→ All owners must sign.

IF TITLE STATES:
OR
→ Either owner may sign.

IF TITLE STATES:
AND/OR
→ Usually either owner may sign, but verify title wording carefully.

VERIFY:
- Signature matches printed owner name.
- Seller signatures match title ownership exactly.
- Buyer signatures are completed where required.
- Lienholder information matches paperwork and system.

COMMON ISSUES:
- One owner missing.
- Signature signed incorrectly.
- Nickname used instead of legal name.
- POA paperwork missing.
- Trust/entity documentation missing.
- Signature line skipped.
- Incorrect reassignment section used.

POWER OF ATTORNEY:
Escalate if:
- POA appears altered.
- POA is incomplete.
- POA signatures do not match.
- Vehicle ownership structure is unclear.

DECEASED OWNER:
Escalate immediately.
Additional documents may be required depending on ownership structure and state rules.

TRUST OR BUSINESS OWNERSHIP:
Verify:
- Business documents.
- Authorized signer.
- Supporting ownership paperwork.

ODOMETER DISCLOSURE:
Verify odometer disclosure requirements based on vehicle year and transaction type.

ESCALATE IF:
- Ownership wording is unclear.
- Title is altered.
- Signatures do not match.
- Crossed-out signatures exist.
- Whiteout used.
- Missing seller/owner signatures.
- Out-of-state title rules differ.
- Customer requests unusual ownership changes.

CONFIDENCE RULE:
If uncertain about ownership or signatures, stop processing and escalate before submission.
