============================================================
CORE EFS CONCEPT
============================================================

EFS allows authorized EFS agents, including dealerships and related entities, to electronically enter motor vehicle registration and title data through certified service provider systems.

For dealership operations, EFS creates two major compliance responsibilities:

1. Inventory responsibility:
   The dealership must properly handle plate/registration inventory when transactions are voided, corrected, returned, or not completed.

2. Records responsibility:
   The dealership must properly scan, submit, retain, destroy, or mark paper documents according to EFS records retention rules.

============================================================
EFS INVENTORY STATUS DEFINITIONS
============================================================

RS — RE-ISSUABLE

Use when:
- Buyer never took possession.
- Plate was not placed on the vehicle.
- Transaction was voided for correction and will be resubmitted.
- Inventory may be reused after correction.

Common clerk meaning:
RS means the inventory can be reissued if the facts support reuse.

------------------------------------------------------------

RT — EFS RETURNED

Use when:
- Transaction is voided and inventory should not be reused by the dealership.
- Plate was issued/placed or the transaction cannot be completed.
- Inventory must be returned to the tax collector or license plate agency.

Common clerk meaning:
RT means the inventory is in returned status and should not be treated as available dealer inventory.

------------------------------------------------------------

RR — RETURN RECEIVED

Use when:
- The tax collector or license plate agency physically receives returned inventory.
- Returned inventory is accepted and marked received.

Common clerk meaning:
RR means the agency/tax collector received the returned inventory.

------------------------------------------------------------

PN — PAID NOT RETURNED EFS PLATE

Use when:
- EFS agent does not return inventory.
- Bureau of Accounting invoices the EFS agent.
- The EFS agent pays for the non-returned inventory item.

Common clerk meaning:
PN means the plate/inventory was not returned and accounting/payment consequences may apply.

------------------------------------------------------------

IS — ISSUED

Use when:
- Void error occurs.
- System is not updated.
- Inventory remains issued.

Common clerk meaning:
If the void fails, do not assume inventory changed status. Contact the tax collector office.

============================================================
EFS INVENTORY COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

EFS inventory handling depends on what happened in the transaction. First determine whether the buyer took possession, whether the plate was placed on the vehicle, whether the vehicle left the dealership, and whether the transaction will be corrected and resubmitted. If the buyer never took possession and the plate was not placed on the vehicle, void the transaction and set inventory to available so the plate can become RS/re-issuable. If the plate was placed on the vehicle, the vehicle left the dealership, financing fell through, or the customer returned the vehicle, void the transaction and return the plate to the tax collector or license plate agency. Do not reuse plate inventory unless the facts support RS/re-issuable status. If an error occurs or the wrong inventory status was selected, contact the tax collector office or correct the transaction before proceeding.

============================================================

Documents may include:

- HSMV 82040 MV
- HSMV 82040 MH
- HSMV 82040 VS
- MSO / MCO
- Customer identification, other than Florida driver license or Florida ID card
- Florida Certificate of Title
- HSMV Form 82250
- Out-of-state title
- HSMV 82994, dealer title reassignment supplement
- HSMV 82995, power of attorney / odometer disclosure

============================================================
EFS RECORDS RETENTION RULES
============================================================

Licensed motor vehicle dealers must keep records of purchase, sale, exchange, or receipt for sale for 5 years.

This matters for:
- Audit trails
- Ownership chain
- Missing document issues
- Dealer compliance reviews
- Purchase/sale disputes
- Title transfer support
- Rejection prevention

After scanned EFS documents are submitted and the transaction is approved by the tax collector or license plate agent, the EFS Agent must either:

1. Destroy the paper documents; or
2. Render the documents invalid.

Deadline:
This must be completed within 5 business days of approval.

If destroyed:
Documents should be shredded and disposed of securely to protect confidential information.

If retained:
Documents must be stamped or marked on the face of the document as VOID or INVALID.

============================================================
EFS RECORDS RETENTION COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For EFS records retention, verify that the required documents were scanned into ORION-EFS and submitted to the tax collector or license plate agent. Required uploads may include HSMV 82040, MSO/MCO, non-Florida customer ID, Florida title, out-of-state title, HSMV 82994, or HSMV 82995 depending on the transaction. After the EFS transaction is approved, paper documents must either be destroyed or marked VOID/INVALID within five business days. Dealers must also keep purchase, sale, exchange, or receipt-for-sale records for five years. If an uploaded document, approval status, or retention record is unclear, hold and escalate before resubmission or audit response.

============================================================
COMBINED EFS WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify whether the issue is inventory, document upload, record retention, or audit trail.
2. If inventory/void issue, determine:
   - Did buyer take possession?
   - Was plate placed on vehicle?
   - Did vehicle leave dealership?
   - Will transaction be corrected and resubmitted?
   - Was inventory returned?
   - What status applies: RS, RT, RR, PN, or IS?
3. If document/upload issue, determine:
   - Was transaction processed through ORION-EFS?
   - Were all required documents scanned?
   - Was the transaction approved?
   - Is upload history available?
4. If paper document issue, determine:
   - Were paper documents destroyed after approval?
   - Were paper documents marked VOID or INVALID?
   - Was this completed within five business days?
5. If audit trail issue, determine:
   - Does the EFS history support the transaction?
   - Does the deal file support the transaction?
   - Are required dealer records retained for five years?
6. If unclear, hold and escalate to:
   - title lead
   - controller
   - tax collector
   - license plate agency
   - EFS support
   - compliance contact

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For EFS issues, first determine whether the problem involves inventory, a voided transaction, document uploads, records retention, or audit trail. If the issue involves EFS inventory, determine whether the buyer took possession, whether the plate was placed on the vehicle, whether the vehicle left the dealership, and whether the transaction will be corrected and resubmitted. Use RS only when the inventory is properly re-issuable. Use return workflows when the plate was placed, the vehicle left, financing fell through, or the customer returned the vehicle. If the issue involves EFS records, verify ORION-EFS uploads, submission history, approval status, and whether paper documents were destroyed or marked VOID/INVALID within five business days. Dealers must keep purchase/sale/exchange/receipt records for five years. If the EFS record, inventory status, uploaded document, or audit trail cannot be verified, hold and escalate before proceeding.
