PURPOSE
============================================================

This procedure explains records retention and destruction requirements for EFS Agents.

This file is important for dealership workflows involving:

- EFS document uploads
- ORION-EFS scanned documents
- Records retention
- Audit trail review

- Dealer compliance examiner review

============================================================
FIVE-YEAR DEALER RECORD RETENTION RULE
============================================================

Florida law requires licensed motor vehicle dealers to keep a record of the purchase, sale, exchange, or receipt for sale for 5 years.

Operational meaning:
Dealerships must maintain records supporting vehicle acquisition, sale, exchange, and receipt for sale.

This matters for:
- Audit trail review
- Missing ownership documentation
- Dealer compliance examiners
- Purchase/sale disputes
- Title transfer support
- Rejection prevention
- Ownership chain review

COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
Licensed motor vehicle dealers must keep records of purchase, sale, exchange, or receipt for sale for 5 years. If there is an audit trail or document retention issue, confirm the dealership can produce the required records supporting the transaction.

============================================================
ELECTRONIC RECORDS
============================================================

Licensed motor vehicle dealers may maintain electronic records as required by Chapter 319 for purchasers who will title and register the motor vehicle in Florida.

Operational meaning:
Electronic records may be used, but the dealership must still ensure required documents are properly scanned, submitted, approved, and retained according to procedure.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
Electronic records may be maintained, but the dealership must still verify that all required title and registration documents were scanned into ORION-EFS and submitted to the tax collector or license plate agent as required.

============================================================
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED TO BE SCANNED INTO ORION-EFS
============================================================

EFS Agents are required to scan applicable documents into ORION-EFS and submit them to the Tax Collector or License Plate Agent.

Documents include, as applicable:

1. One of the following:
   - HSMV 82040 MV
   - HSMV 82040 MH
   - HSMV 82040 VS

2. Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin (MSO) or Manufacturer’s Certificate of Origin (MCO).

3. Customer identification, other than a Florida driver license or Florida identification card.

4. Florida Certificate of Title, HSMV Form 82250, or out-of-state title.

5. HSMV Form 82994:
   Application for Motor Vehicle Dealer Title Reassignment Supplement.

6. HSMV Form 82995:
   Application for Motor Vehicle Power of Attorney / Odometer Disclosure.

============================================================
AFTER TAX COLLECTOR OR LICENSE PLATE AGENT APPROVAL
============================================================

After scanned documents are submitted and the transaction is approved by the Tax Collector or License Plate Agent, the EFS Agent must destroy or otherwise render the documents invalid.

Deadline:
This must be done within five (5) business days of approval.

If destroyed:
Documents should be shredded and disposed of properly to protect confidential information.

If retained:
The paper documents must be stamped or otherwise marked on the face of the document to render it VOID or INVALID.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
After the tax collector or license plate agent approves the EFS transaction, the dealership must either destroy the paper documents or render them invalid within five business days. If destroyed, shred them properly. If retained, stamp or mark the face of the document VOID or INVALID.

============================================================
DO NOT LEAVE APPROVED PAPER DOCUMENTS ACTIVE
============================================================

IF EFS_transaction_approved_by_tax_collector_or_license_plate_agent = TRUE
AND paper_documents_are_retained = TRUE
THEN paper_documents_must_be_marked_VOID_or_INVALID = TRUE

IF approved_paper_documents_not_destroyed_or_marked_invalid_within_5_business_days = TRUE
THEN compliance_risk_high = TRUE

IF paper_documents_destroyed = TRUE
THEN shred_and_dispose_securely_to_protect_confidential_information = TRUE

COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
Once an EFS transaction is approved, do not keep active original paper documents sitting in the deal file without marking them void or invalid. The dealership must destroy them or mark them VOID/INVALID within five business days of approval.

============================================================
COMPLIANCE EXAMINER REVIEW
============================================================

Compliance with this procedure may be verified by Department Compliance Examiners during dealer visits.

This means the dealership should be able to show:
- Required documents were scanned/submitted.
- Paper documents were destroyed or marked invalid after approval.
- Dealer records are maintained as required.
- Audit trail supports the transaction.
- Confidential information was protected.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
FLHSMV compliance examiners may verify EFS records retention during dealer visits. Keep a clear audit trail showing the required documents were scanned, the transaction was approved, and paper documents were destroyed or marked VOID/INVALID as required.

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Confirm the transaction was processed through EFS / ORION-EFS.
2. Identify which documents were required for the transaction.
3. Confirm all required documents were scanned and submitted.
4. Confirm tax collector or license plate agent approval.
5. If transaction approved, determine whether paper documents were destroyed or retained.
6. If destroyed, confirm they were shredded/disposed of securely.
7. If retained, confirm the documents were marked VOID or INVALID on the face of the document.
8. Confirm dealership retains required purchase/sale/exchange/receipt records for 5 years.
9. If an uploaded document is missing or the audit trail is incomplete, review ORION-EFS history and escalate.
10. Maintain compliance support for possible FLHSMV Compliance Examiner review.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER
============================================================

For EFS records retention, verify that the required documents were scanned into ORION-EFS and submitted to the tax collector or license plate agent. Required scans may include HSMV 82040, MSO/MCO, non-Florida customer ID, Florida or out-of-state title, HSMV 82994, and HSMV 82995 depending on the transaction. After the transaction is approved, paper documents must either be destroyed or marked VOID/INVALID within five business days. Licensed motor vehicle dealers must also keep records of purchase, sale, exchange, or receipt for sale for 5 years. If any uploaded document, approval status, or retention record is unclear, hold and escalate before resubmission or audit response.
