FLORIDA REGISTRATION PROCEDURE — GOVERNMENT LICENSE PLATES
PROCEDURE: RS-12
CATEGORY: GOVERNMENT PLATES / EXEMPT REGISTRATIONS / GOVERNMENT VEHICLES

==================================================
PROCEDURE SUMMARY
==================================================

This procedure governs:
- Government license plate issuance
- Government plate classifications
- Government plate transfers
- Government plate replacements
- Government leased vehicle plates
- Government loaned vehicle plates
- Government ownership verification
- Government exempt registrations
- State/county/city plate designation

Applies to:
- State agencies
- County agencies
- Municipal agencies
- Government entities
- Volunteer fire departments
- Water management districts
- Interlocal governmental entities
- Community development districts
- Government leased vehicles
- Government loaned vehicles
- Tax collectors
- License plate agencies
- FLHSMV personnel

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE RULE
==================================================

Permanent exempt government plates:
Issued for vehicles:
Owned or exclusively operated:
By qualifying Florida governmental entities.

==================================================
QUALIFYING GOVERNMENT ENTITIES
==================================================

Eligible entities:
- State agencies
- Counties
- Municipalities
- Government entities
- Volunteer fire departments

==================================================
DEPARTMENT-ISSUED GOVERNMENT PLATES
==================================================

Issued only by FLHSMV Department:
- AWR
- ACR
- PCR
- EPR
- OTR
- HPR
- FWR
- LER
- NBR
- PRR
- LSR
- FDR
- FRR

==================================================
COUNTY-ISSUED GOVERNMENT PLATES
==================================================

May issue through county agency:
- CVR
- CGR
- NVR
- SHR
- SVR
- CMR
- NCR
- SMR
- VFR
- WMR

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MOTORCYCLE RULE
==================================================

Government motorcycle plates:
Include:
- CMR
- NCR
- SMR

==================================================
PROOF OF OWNERSHIP RULES
==================================================

Require one of:
1. Florida registration certificate
2. Florida title
3. Title receipt
4. Florida title application with documents
5. Trailer weight proof if untitled trailer

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT GOVERNMENT DEALER RULE
==================================================

Government agency purchaser:
May receive:
Original MCO directly from dealer.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT DEALER TITLE RULE
==================================================

Dealer title filing requirement:
Normally 30 days:
Except governmental agency MCO exception.

==================================================
TRAILER RULES
==================================================

Trailer under 2,000 lbs:
Requires:
- Weight slip or weight letter
- Homemade statement or purchase proof

==================================================
LETTER OF REQUEST RULE
==================================================

Require:
Letter requesting:
- City series
- County series
OR
- State series plate.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT INTERLOCAL ENTITY RULE
==================================================

Interlocal governmental entities:
Must receive:
State series plate.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT DISTRICT RULE
==================================================

Community development districts:
Must receive:
State series government plate.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT UTILITIES AUTHORITY RULE
==================================================

Utility authority created by:
City and county consolidation:
Treated:
As interlocal governmental entity.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT SPECIALTY PLATE RULE
==================================================

Governmental entities:
NOT eligible:
For specialty plates.

==================================================
FEE RULES
==================================================

Government registrations:
Still require certain fees.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT EXEMPT FEE RULE
==================================================

Exempt registration:
Still requires:
- Original plate fee
- Service fee
- Branch fee if applicable
- State Transportation Surcharge
- Juvenile Justice fee
- Law Enforcement Radio Systems fee
- Air Pollution Control fee
- EMS fee
- FRVIS fee
- Reflectorization fee

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FEE CHART RULE
==================================================

Refer:
Registration Fees and Flat Taxes Distribution Chart:
For applicable fees.

==================================================
TRANSFER / REASSIGNMENT RULES
==================================================

Government plate transfer:
May process:
Through any county agency.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT HSMV 83036 RULE
==================================================

Require:
HSMV 83036:
Notice of Reassignment:
Completed by authorized agency personnel.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT RETENTION RULE
==================================================

HSMV 83036:
Retained:
By local county license plate agency.

==================================================
TRANSFER FEE RULES
==================================================

Transfer/reassignment fees include:
- Service fee
- Branch fee if applicable
- Air Pollution Control fee
- EMS fee
- FRVIS fee

==================================================

HSMV 83146.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE CONCEPT
==================================================

Florida government license plate procedures:
Allow qualifying governmental entities
to obtain permanent exempt registrations
for owned or exclusively operated vehicles,
but plate type,
documentation,
entity classification,
and fee rules
must be strictly followed.
