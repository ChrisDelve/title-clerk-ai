==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE RULE
==================================================

Forestry Products license plates:
Issued to truck tractors:
Used exclusively:
To haul:
- Forestry products
OR
- Forestry harvesting equipment.

==================================================
DEFINITION — FORESTRY PRODUCTS
==================================================

Forestry products:
Any material:
Originating from trees:
Harvested,
gathered,
or collected:
From:
- Forest
- Woods
- Tree land.

==================================================
DEFINITION — FORESTRY SITE
==================================================

Forestry site:
Initial point:
Of:
- Harvest
- Collection
- Gathering
Of forestry products.

==================================================
DEFINITION — HAULING
==================================================

Hauling:
Transportation:
By truck tractor:
Of forestry products or harvesting equipment:
From forestry site:
To another location.

==================================================
DEFINITION — HOME ADDRESS
==================================================

Home address:
Address shown:
On registration certificate.

==================================================
DEFINITION — NOT-FOR-HIRE
==================================================

Not-for-hire:
Means:
Truck tractor owner:
Operates vehicle.

==================================================
ELIGIBILITY RULES
==================================================

Customer eligible:
Only if:
Vehicle remains:
Within 150-mile radius:
Of home address.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 150-MILE RULE
==================================================

Crossing state lines:
Allowed:
If still:
Within 150-mile radius.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT IFTA RULE
==================================================

If:
- Power unit has 3 axles
OR
- Combined GVW exceeds 26,000 lbs

AND vehicle leaves Florida:
Vehicle must have:
- IFTA license
OR
- Temporary fuel permits
For each state:
Including Florida.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT INITIAL FORESTRY SITE RULE
==================================================

Vehicle cannot haul:
Forestry products:
To or from:
Any location other than:
Initial forestry site.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT HARVESTING EQUIPMENT RULE
==================================================

Harvesting equipment:
Must be:
Used by owner:
Of truck tractor.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT UNPROCESSED PRODUCT RULE
==================================================

Forestry products:
Must be:
Unprocessed
OR
Partially processed in forest.

==================================================
ELIGIBILITY EXAMPLES — QUALIFIES
==================================================

QUALIFIES:
- Chip truck hauling chips from forest portable chipper to pulp plant
- Truck hauling mulch, straw, bark from forest to processing plant
- Truck hauling sawdust/shavings directly from forest

==================================================
ELIGIBILITY EXAMPLES — DOES NOT QUALIFY
==================================================

DOES NOT QUALIFY:
- Chips from stationary chipper outside forest
- Peeled logs from stationary outside location
- Mulch/straw/bark to retail vendor
- Processed lumber from mill/lumberyard
- Finished products like furniture or paper
- Sawdust/shavings hauled from plant

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE CONCEPT
==================================================

Florida Forestry Products license plates:
Provide restricted commercial registration status
for truck tractors exclusively hauling unprocessed forestry products
or forestry harvesting equipment,
subject to strict:
- 150-mile operational limits
- forestry-site restrictions
- noncommercial ownership requirements
- insurance compliance
and product qualification standards.
