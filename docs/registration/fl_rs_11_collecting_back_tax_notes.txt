==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE RULE
==================================================

FLHSMV may withhold registration:
Until unpaid registration tax:
For prior required periods:
Is fully paid.

==================================================
ASSESSING BACK TAX RULES
==================================================

Back tax assessed:
Based on:
- Initial registration
OR
- Renewal transaction.

==================================================
INITIAL REGISTRATION BACK TAX RULE
==================================================

Initial registration:
Back tax due:
From purchase date.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PURCHASE DATE RULE
==================================================

Purchase date determined by:
Proof of ownership:
Transferred to new owner.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NEW OWNER RULE
==================================================

New owner:
Only responsible:
For tax due:
From their own purchase date.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PRIOR OWNER RULE
==================================================

Previous owner's unpaid back tax:
Cannot be assessed:
To new owner.

==================================================
RENEWAL TRANSACTION BACK TAX RULE
==================================================

Renewal transaction:
Back tax due:
From expiration date:
Of last registration.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT EXPIRATION RULE
==================================================

Renewal back tax calculated:
Beginning:
Date last registration expired:
In owner's name.

==================================================
BACK TAX EXEMPTION RULES
==================================================

Certain vehicles:
May qualify:
For exemption from back tax.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT STORAGE EXEMPTION RULE
==================================================

Vehicle exempt from back tax:
If continuously maintained:
In storage:
Entire claimed period.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NON-OPERATION RULE
==================================================

Vehicle must NOT have been:
- Operated
OR
- Removed from storage
At any time:
During claimed exemption period.

==================================================
AFFIDAVIT REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Require:
Notarized affidavit
OR
Perjury clause affidavit.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT AFFIDAVIT CONTENT RULE
==================================================

Affidavit must state:
- Vehicle continuously stored
- Vehicle not removed from storage
- Vehicle not operated
- Entire exemption period covered

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT EXCLUDED VEHICLE RULE
==================================================

Back tax exemption:
Does NOT apply:
To:
- Mobile homes
- Travel trailers registered as mobile homes
- Park trailers registered as mobile homes

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MOBILE HOME RULE
==================================================

Mobile homes:
Still subject:
To back tax assessment.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TRAVEL TRAILER RULE
==================================================

Travel trailers registered as mobile homes:
NOT exempt:
From back tax.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PARK TRAILER RULE
==================================================

Park trailers registered as mobile homes:
NOT exempt:
From back tax.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE CONCEPT
==================================================

Florida back tax rules:
Require unpaid registration taxes
to be collected for delinquent registration periods,
unless the owner properly proves
the vehicle remained continuously stored
and non-operational during the claimed exemption period.
