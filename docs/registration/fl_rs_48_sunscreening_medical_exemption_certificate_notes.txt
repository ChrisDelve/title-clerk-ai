==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE RULE
==================================================

Medical exemption certificates:
Allow otherwise prohibited sunscreening material:
Due to qualifying medical conditions requiring limited light exposure.

==================================================
ELIGIBLE MEDICAL CONDITIONS RULE
==================================================

Qualifying conditions include:
- Lupus
- autoimmune diseases
- other medically qualifying light-sensitive conditions.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT FLORIDA VEHICLE RULE
==================================================

Only vehicles:
Titled and registered in Florida:
Eligible for sunscreening exemption certificates.

==================================================
PROCESSING RULE
==================================================

FLHSMV:
Processes all sunscreening exemption transactions.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MAILING RULE
==================================================

All documentation:
Must be mailed to:

Florida Department of Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles
Room A201 MS#97
Neil Kirkman Building
2900 Apalachee Parkway
Tallahassee FL 32399

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NONTRANSFERABLE RULE
==================================================

Medical exemption certificate:
Is NON-transferable.

Certificate becomes invalid:
Upon:
- sale
OR
- transfer
Of vehicle listed on certificate.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT ONE CERTIFICATE RULE
==================================================

Only ONE certificate:
May issue:
Per motor vehicle.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MULTI-VEHICLE RULE
==================================================

Additional vehicles:
Require:
Additional fees:
Per vehicle.

==================================================
REGISTERED OWNER RULE
==================================================

Certificate:
Issued to:
Registered owner:
NOT necessarily:
Person with medical condition.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CHILD RULE
==================================================

Parent/guardian:
May apply:
For minor child:
With qualifying condition.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT TITLE RULE
==================================================

Person with medical condition:
Does NOT need:
Ownership interest:
On title or registration.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PHYSICIAN STATEMENT RULE
==================================================

Once physician certification:
On file:
Additional vehicle applications:
Do NOT require:
New physician certification.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT PERMANENT CERTIFICATION RULE
==================================================

Physician Statement of Certification:
Remains permanent:
On file.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT SUNSCREEN REMOVAL RULE
==================================================

Owner responsible:
For removing sunscreening material:
Before selling/transferring vehicle.

==================================================
COMPANY VEHICLE RULE
==================================================

If operating company vehicle:
Company owner:
Must provide:
Written authorization:
On company letterhead.

==================================================
APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

Application requires:
HSMV 83390:
Completed by:
Person with medical condition.

==================================================
CERTIFYING AUTHORITY RULES
==================================================

Approved certifying authorities include:
- physicians
- physician assistants
- dermatologists
- APRNs
- military physicians
- Veterans Administration adjudication offices.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT MILITARY PHYSICIAN RULE
==================================================

Military/state hospital/federal prison physician:
Must provide:
Detailed facility information:
On HSMV 83390.

==================================================
IDENTIFICATION RULES
==================================================

Acceptable identification includes:
- Florida DL/ID
- other state DL/ID
- U.S. territory DL/ID
- passport/passport card
- Canadian DL/ID/passport.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NON-US PASSPORT RULE
==================================================

Foreign passports:
Acceptable.

==================================================
FEE RULES
==================================================

Fee:
$6.45:
Per applicable vehicle.

==================================================
DUPLICATE CERTIFICATE RULES
==================================================

Duplicate certificate:
May issue:
If original lost or destroyed.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT DUPLICATE RULE
==================================================

Duplicate application:
Requires:
HSMV 83390:
With:
"Duplicate" box checked.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NO PHYSICIAN DUPLICATE RULE
==================================================

Duplicate applications:
Do NOT require:
Physician certification section completed.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT DUPLICATE FEE RULE
==================================================

Duplicate fee:
$6.45:
Per vehicle.

==================================================
LOST-IN-TRANSIT RULES
==================================================

Lost-in-transit replacement:
May issue:
At no charge.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT 180-DAY RULE
==================================================

Lost-in-transit application:
Must be submitted:
Within 180 days:
Of issue date.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT LOST-IN-TRANSIT BLOCK RULE
==================================================

Lost-in-transit applications:
Require:
"Lost-in-Transit" block checked:
On HSMV 83390.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT NO PHYSICIAN LOST-IN-TRANSIT RULE
==================================================

Lost-in-transit replacement:
Does NOT require:
Physician certification section completed.

==================================================
CORRECTION RULES
==================================================

Corrected certificates:
May issue:
At no charge.

==================================================
DISPLAY RULES
==================================================

Certificate:
Must remain:
In possession:
Of vehicle operator.

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT GLOVE BOX RULE
==================================================

Certificate:
Should remain:
Inside vehicle:
For verification purposes.

==================================================
COMMON TITLE CLERK / REGISTRATION ERRORS
==================================================

- Issuing certificate for non-Florida vehicle
- Requiring title ownership for medically affected person
- Requesting repeat physician certification unnecessarily
- Missing company authorization letter
- Rejecting valid out-of-state/foreign identification
- Missing Duplicate or Lost-in-Transit box
- Charging lost-in-transit replacement fee improperly
- Issuing transferable exemption
- Missing parent/guardian handling
- Missing physician facility information
- Accepting invalid certifying authority
- Missing glove compartment instruction
- Incorrect fee calculation
- Ignoring 180-day lost-in-transit limit
- Failing to advise owner to remove tint before transfer

==================================================
VERY IMPORTANT CORE CONCEPT
==================================================

Florida sunscreening medical exemption procedures:
Allow medically necessary window tint exemptions
for qualifying light-sensitive medical conditions,
while enforcing:
- strict medical certification
- vehicle ownership verification
- non-transferability
- replacement controls
- company vehicle authorization
and proper exemption display/documentation requirements.
