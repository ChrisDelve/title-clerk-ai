============================================================
SHORT VERSION
============================================================

Delve AI is a Florida dealership title and registration workflow assistant.

It helps dealership staff answer practical title, registration, plate, lien, insurance, odometer, EFS, ID, and rejection-prevention questions using uploaded Florida statutes, FLHSMV procedures, and internal operational notes.

It is designed to help clerks, F&I managers, controllers, office managers, and dealership staff understand:

- What workflow applies
- What documents are required
- Whether the transaction should be held
- Whether the issue needs escalation
- Whether the issue is title-only, registration, plate transfer, new plate, lien, odometer, EFS, or audit-trail related
- Which internal source files were used to generate the answer

============================================================
WHAT PROBLEM IT SOLVES
============================================================

Dealership title and registration questions are repetitive, time-sensitive, and often depend on specific Florida rules, FLHSMV procedures, dealership workflow, and tax collector expectations.

Common issues include:

- Transfer tag questions
- Expired registration
- No valid Florida insurance
- Foreign passport or ID issues
- Out-of-state titles
- Missing VIN verification
- Active liens or ELT issues
- Blank odometer disclosures
- Seller signed in the wrong area
- AND ownership with missing signatures
- Title-only exceptions
- Registration stops
- EFS plate inventory issues
- Third temporary tag approval workflows

This assistant gives dealership staff a fast operational answer instead of forcing them to search through statutes, PDFs, procedure manuals, notes, or ask the title office repeatedly.

============================================================
WHAT THE APP DOES
============================================================

The assistant reviews the user’s question and searches the dealership knowledge base.

It then provides:

1. Operational Guidance
   A practical explanation of how the dealership should handle the issue.

2. Required Documents
   A checklist of forms, title documents, insurance, ID, lien releases, odometer disclosures, approvals, or supporting documents when applicable.

3. Compliance Concerns
   Warnings about issues that may cause rejection, audit problems, fraud exposure, or improper processing.

4. Escalation Situations
   When the issue should be held and escalated to the title lead, controller, tax collector, FLHSMV, lender, or management.

5. Sources Used
   The internal statutes, FLHSMV procedures, law notes, operational notes, and logic maps used to generate the answer.

============================================================
WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT FROM GENERIC AI
============================================================

This is not a general chatbot.

It is built around Florida dealership title and registration workflows.

The app uses:

- Florida statute notes
- FLHSMV procedure notes
- EFS procedure notes
- Operational dealership notes
- Rejection-prevention logic maps
- Validation logic maps
- Real title clerk testing scenarios

It is designed to answer the way a dealership title clerk needs the answer:

- Can we process this?
- Should we hold it?
- What document is missing?
- Is this a plate transfer or new plate issue?
- Does IRF apply?
- Is insurance required?
- Is this title-only or registration?
- Does this need tax collector, FLHSMV, lender, controller, or manager approval?
- What source files support the answer?

============================================================
SCOPE NOTE
============================================================

This assistant supports dealership workflow guidance.

It does not replace:

- FLHSMV
- Tax collector guidance
- Lender approval
- Controller approval
- Management approval
- Compliance review
- Legal advice

When ownership, lien, odometer, fraud, legal authority, registration stops, or compliance issues are unclear, the transaction should be held and escalated before submission.
