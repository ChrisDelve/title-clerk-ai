STATUTE: 319.35
TITLE: Unlawful Acts in Connection with Motor Vehicle Odometer Readings; Penalties
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.35.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: ODOMETER TAMPERING / FALSE MILEAGE / ODOMETER FRAUD / CONTRABAND / TITLE BRAND

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs unlawful acts connected to motor vehicle odometer readings.

It is the core Florida legal reference for:
- odometer tampering
- rollback
- false mileage statements
- knowingly bringing altered odometer vehicles into Florida
- knowingly selling/possessing vehicles with tampered odometers
- odometer replacement notice requirements
- title/registration stamping when displayed mileage is inaccurate
- contraband and forfeiture risk

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

It is unlawful to knowingly tamper with, adjust, alter, set back, disconnect, or fail to connect an odometer so that it reflects lower mileage than the vehicle has actually been driven.

It is unlawful to knowingly provide a false written odometer statement or a statement based on an altered/tampered odometer.

It is unlawful to knowingly bring into Florida a motor vehicle with an illegally altered odometer.

It is unlawful to knowingly provide false odometer information required by title or registration statutes.

It is unlawful to knowingly possess, sell, offer for sale, conceal, or dispose of a motor vehicle with a tampered odometer showing lower mileage, except under limited statutory conditions.

Odometer repair/replacement is allowed only if the mileage remains the same. If it cannot show the same mileage, the odometer must be set to zero and a written notice must be attached to the door frame showing prior mileage and repair/replacement date.

A vehicle with an odometer tampered to show lower mileage may not be knowingly operated unless title and registration are conspicuously stamped to indicate displayed mileage is inaccurate and written notice is placed on the vehicle.

Intentional violation is a third-degree felony.

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

- Discrepancy
- Incorrect entry
- Odometer replacement

- Rollback/tampering concern
- Kilometer/miles issue

2. Compare all records.
- Title
- Odometer disclosure
- Buyer order
- Prior title records
- Auction announcement
- Vehicle odometer
- Repair records

3. Determine risk level.
- Clerical correction
- Unclear mileage
- Brand issue
- Fraud/tampering concern

4. Escalate if serious.
- Title lead
- Supervisor
- Tax collector / FLHSMV
- Law enforcement if fraud/tampering is suspected

5. Hold submission if unresolved.
- Do not submit conflicting mileage documents.
- Do not promise brand removal.
