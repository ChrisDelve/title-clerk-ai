STATUTE: 319.21
TITLE: Necessity of Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin and Certificate of Title
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.21.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: TITLE LAW / MCO / MSO / CERTIFICATE OF ORIGIN / TITLE REQUIREMENTS / NEW VEHICLES / MOBILE HOMES

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs when a Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin / Manufacturer’s Certificate of Origin and certificate of title are required.

It is a core legal reference for new vehicle titling, new mobile home titling, dealer title workflows, ownership evidence, and initial registration eligibility.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

A new motor vehicle or new mobile home generally cannot be sold, disposed of, purchased, acquired, or brought into Florida for sale without a properly executed Manufacturer’s Statement of Origin.

The MSO/MCO must show the assignments needed to establish title in the purchaser.

The statement of origin must be in English and must identify/describe the vehicle or mobile home and show the name/address of the original purchaser from the manufacturer.

If a vehicle is built in two or more stages, each manufacturer must provide a statement of origin for each stage.

A person generally may not sell or dispose of a motor vehicle or mobile home without delivering a certificate of title properly assigned to show title in the purchaser, unless a statutory dealer/MSO exception applies.

A licensed dealer may reassign an existing title instead of titling in the dealer’s name, except as otherwise limited by odometer/reassignment rules.

A new motor vehicle or mobile home is not eligible for initial Florida registration unless this section has been complied with.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- MCO/MSO is missing
- MCO/MSO assignments are incomplete
- VIN/description does not match title application
- purchaser is not supported by assignment chain
- multi-stage vehicle lacks all required manufacturer statements
- dealer fails to attach MCO/MSO to purchaser title application
- initial registration is attempted before title evidence requirements are satisfied
- MCO/MSO appears altered or used to misrepresent model year
