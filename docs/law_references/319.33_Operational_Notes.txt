TITLE: Offenses Involving Vehicle Identification Numbers, Applications, Certificates, Papers; Penalty
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.33.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: FRAUD / VIN OFFENSES / FORGED TITLE / FALSE APPLICATION / STOLEN VEHICLE / CONTRABAND / TITLE DOCUMENT OFFENSES

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute identifies criminal offenses involving Florida titles, VINs, applications, assignments, lien cancellations, bills of sale, sworn ownership statements, identification plates, counterfeit documents, stolen vehicles, and related fraudulent title activity.

It is a legal anchor for rejection prevention, fraud detection, VIN discrepancy escalation, title-document integrity review, and suspicious ownership-document workflows.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

It is unlawful to alter, forge, retain, or use a forged certificate of title, title assignment, or lien cancellation.

It is unlawful to obtain or attempt to obtain/pass title documents when the person knows or has reason to believe the vehicle or mobile home is stolen.

It is unlawful to possess, sell, offer for sale, conceal, or dispose of a vehicle/mobile home or major component part when the manufacturer/state VIN or motor number has been destroyed, removed, covered, altered, or defaced, with knowledge of that condition.

It is unlawful to use a false/fictitious name or address, make false statements in title applications, affidavits, bills of sale, sworn statements of ownership, or commit fraud in an application.

It is unlawful to knowingly obtain goods, services, credit, or money using invalid, duplicate, fictitious, forged, counterfeit, stolen, or unlawfully obtained ownership/title/registration documents.

It is unlawful to knowingly and with intent to defraud possess, sell, offer, counterfeit, or supply blank/forged/fictitious/counterfeit/stolen/fraudulent ownership documents.

Counterfeit VIN/serial plates or decals are also prohibited.

Violations are third-degree felonies, and vehicles used in violations may be treated as contraband and subject to forfeiture.

If all identifying numbers are missing/destroyed/altered/defaced or the vehicle identity cannot be determined, the vehicle/mobile home may be contraband and may not be operated unless a court orders the department to assign a replacement VIN.

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify the suspicious issue.
- Altered title
- Forged assignment
- Forged lien release
- VIN issue
- False bill of sale
- False ownership statement
- Stolen vehicle concern

2. Stop normal processing.
- Do not submit questionable documents.
- Do not rely on altered or suspicious ownership evidence.

3. Verify documents.
- Compare title, VIN, bill of sale, lien release, customer ID, and audit trail.
- Confirm ownership chain and document integrity.

4. Escalate.
- Title lead / supervisor
- FLHSMV
- Tax collector
- Law enforcement if theft/VIN tampering/fraud concern exists

5. Hold transaction.
- Do not finalize title/registration until identity and document integrity are resolved.
