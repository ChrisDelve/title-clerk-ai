STATUTE: 319.24
TITLE: Issuance in Duplicate; Delivery; Liens and Encumbrances
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.24.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: TITLE DELIVERY / DUPLICATE TITLE / LIEN DELIVERY / LIEN SATISFACTION / SUBSEQUENT LIEN / ELT

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs certificate of title issuance, duplicate/corrected certificate handling, delivery of title when liens exist, lienholder title retention, subsequent lien handling, lien satisfaction duties, duplicate title without liens after lien satisfaction, and electronic lien transmission.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

The department assigns a number to each certificate of title and corrected title.

If no liens or encumbrances exist, the certificate may be delivered to the applicant or another directed person.

If liens or encumbrances exist, title delivery depends on the notice of lien and department records.

If lienholder information conflicts between the application and department records, the department may withhold issuance and notify affected parties by certified mail.

The first lienholder may retain title until the first lien is satisfied.

When a lien is satisfied, the owner or person satisfying the lien may demand lien satisfaction.

A lienholder who fails/refuses to furnish satisfaction after demand may be liable for costs, damages, expenses, and attorney fees.

A dealer acquiring a vehicle with an outstanding purchase money lien must pay and satisfy the lien within 10 working days.

Electronic lien transmission may allow title issuance to be waived until the last lien is satisfied and clear title is issued.

REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- duplicate title application lacks odometer reading
- dealer duplicate title lacks dealer license number
- active lien conflict is ignored
- first lienholder mismatch is not resolved
- lien satisfaction proof is missing
- title delivery direction is not reviewed
- customer requests clean title while department record still shows lien
- subsequent lien process is skipped
- ELT status is ignored
- dealer payoff status is not verified

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify title action.
- Original title
- Duplicate title
- Corrected title
- Lien add/delete/reassign
- Clean title after lien satisfaction

2. Review lien status.
- No lien
- First lienholder
- Subsequent lienholder
- Judgment lien
- ELT lien

3. Verify delivery path.
- Applicant/owner
- First lienholder
- Owner per notice of lien
- Department conflict process

4. Review duplicate requirements.
- Current odometer reading.
- Dealer license number if dealer duplicate.
- Existing liens shown on record.

5. Review lien satisfaction.
- Has lienholder provided satisfaction?
- Is title available?
- Are subsequent liens present?
- Is ELT involved?

6. Decide proceed/hold/escalate.
- Proceed only if title delivery, lien, odometer, and application requirements are satisfied.
- Escalate if lien conflict, active ELT issue, missing satisfaction, or department record mismatch exists.
