STATUTE: 320.02
TITLE: Registration Required; Application for Registration; Forms
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.02.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS / INSURANCE / VIN VERIFICATION / FALSE INFORMATION / REGISTRATION STOPS

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs when Florida vehicle registration is required, what information is required on a registration application, insurance/financial responsibility proof, VIN verification for out-of-state vehicles, false registration information, registration withholding, and certain registration-related stops or refusal conditions.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

Every owner or person in charge of a motor vehicle operated or driven on Florida roads must register the vehicle unless an exception applies.

A vehicle not operated on Florida roads during the registration period does not require registration for that period.

Registration applications require owner address and personal or business identification information.

For vehicles registered outside Florida before Florida registration, VIN verification is generally required, unless an exception applies.

Proof of required insurance or financial responsibility must be provided at registration when applicable. The issuing agent must refuse registration if required proof is not provided.

False or fraudulent registration information can result in penalties and plate cancellation.

The department may withhold registration or reregistration if the owner or co-owner has a driver license suspended for failure to pay certain fines.

Imported vehicles not manufactured to federal standards must be certified by federal authorities before being sold, titled, and registered in Florida.

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Determine if registration is required.
- Is vehicle operated or driven on Florida roads?
- Is this a non-use/not-operated situation?

2. Verify applicant identity.
- Individual ID / license / passport.
- Business FEIN or business authority.

3. Verify insurance.
- PIP / PDL / required BI or financial responsibility.
- Confirm VIN and vehicle match.

4. Review VIN verification.
- Required for vehicle registered outside Florida unless exception applies.

5. Check registration stops.
- Driver license suspension / unpaid fines.
- Insurance cancellation.
- Other department stops.

6. Review imported vehicle issues.
- Federal certification may be required before sale/title/registration.

7. Decide proceed/hold/escalate.
- Hold if insurance, VIN verification, ID, business authority, federal compliance, or stop issue is unresolved.
