TITLE: Temporary Tags

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs Florida temporary tags.

It is the legal reference for:
- when a temporary tag may be issued
- dealer temporary tags
- casual/private sale temporary tags
- driveaway/common carrier temporary tags
- financial institution repossession demonstration tags
- out-of-state purchaser tags
- VIN verification / weigh / emission test 10-day tags
- lien/out-of-state title 60-day tags
- deployed military 60-day tags
- personalized/specialty plate 90-day tags
- off-premise sales transport tags
- temp tag limits, fees, display, misuse, and electronic issuance

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

The department may design, issue, and regulate temporary tags for specific authorized situations.

Unless a specific exception says otherwise, a temporary tag under this section is generally valid for 30 days and no more than two may be issued to the same person for the same vehicle.

A 10-day temporary tag applies where the vehicle must be weighed, emission tested, or have VIN verified before registration.

A 60-day temporary tag applies when an existing owner has applied to transfer a valid out-of-state title subject to lien, and also for certain deployed active-duty military servicemembers with valid Florida driver licenses.

A 90-day temporary tag applies while a personalized prestige or specialty plate is being manufactured.

Temporary tags must be conspicuously displayed in the rear license plate bracket, or front location if the vehicle requires front plates.

Temporary tag misuse has penalties, and willful abuse to avoid registration is a first-degree misdemeanor. Issuing a temporary tag to a fictitious person/entity to hide the true owner is a third-degree felony.

Dealers must use the department’s electronic temporary tag system and may face license discipline for noncompliance.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High compliance/rejection risk if:
- temp tag is issued without authorized statutory basis
- more than two temp tags are issued without allowed exception
- wrong duration is used
- 10-day tag is not limited to weigh/emissions/VIN verification purpose
- 60-day tag conditions are not met
- 90-day specialty/personalized plate purpose is not met
- temp tag is used to avoid registration
- tag is issued to fictitious person/entity
- dealer does not use electronic issuance system
- dealer records are missing
- temp tag not displayed correctly

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify reason for temp tag.
- Dealer plate cannot be used
- Private/casual sale
- VIN verification / weigh / emissions
- Out-of-state purchaser
- Out-of-state title subject to lien
- Deployed military
- Specialty/personalized plate pending
- Consumer complaint
- Off-premise dealer transport

2. Determine allowed duration.
- 10 days
- 30 days
- 60 days
- 90 days

3. Check two-tag limit.
- No more than two for same person/same vehicle unless exception applies.

4. Verify display and issuance.
- Proper location.
- Proper media.
- Electronic system if dealer.

5. Check abuse/fraud risk.
- Do not use temp tags to avoid registration.
- Do not issue to fictitious person/entity.

6. Hold/escalate if unclear.
- Temp tag is not a workaround for unresolved registration/title blocks.
