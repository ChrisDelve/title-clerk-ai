STATUTE: 319.28
TITLE: Transfer of Ownership by Operation of Law
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.28.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: OPERATION OF LAW / DECEASED OWNER / INHERITANCE / REPOSSESSION / STORAGE OR REPAIR SALE / COURT ORDER / BANKRUPTCY

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs title transfer when ownership passes by operation of law rather than a normal sale.

It applies to situations such as inheritance, devise or bequest, bankruptcy, insolvency, replevin, attachment, execution, judicial sale, engine replacement, storage/repair sale, and repossession.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

When ownership of a motor vehicle or mobile home transfers by operation of law, the department may issue a certificate of title after proper proof, application, fees, and surrender of prior title if possible.

If prior title cannot be surrendered, satisfactory proof of ownership and right of possession may be used.

For intestate deceased-owner situations, an heir may not need probate court order if an affidavit is provided stating the estate is not indebted and heirs/surviving spouse agree.

For testate deceased-owner situations, the application generally needs a certified copy or sworn copy of the will plus affidavit regarding estate solvency/not indebted, unless the Florida attorney affidavit route applies.

A surviving spouse disposing of a vehicle may assign the decedent’s title to the transferee without first obtaining title in the surviving spouse’s name, with required supporting documentation.

Repossession has its own affidavit/proof requirements and subsequent lienholder notice rules.

COMMON DEALERSHIP ISSUES
============================================================

- Customer says owner died but has no estate documents
- AND owner deceased and surviving owner cannot sign alone
- Heirs disagree or estate debt status unknown
- Surviving spouse wants to sell without retitling
- Will exists but is not probated
- Probate documents missing
- Attorney affidavit used for testate estate
- Repossession packet missing affidavit
- Subsequent lienholder not notified before repo sale
- Prior title cannot be surrendered
- Customer has court order but no application/title fees
- Storage/repair sale documentation incomplete

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- deceased owner documents are missing
- estate affidavit does not address required facts
- heirs/surviving spouse agreement is unclear
- will/certified copy/sworn copy requirements are not reviewed
- surviving spouse assignment lacks required supporting documentation
- repossession affidavit is missing
- subsequent lienholder certified mail notice is missing
- protest/waiting periods are ignored
- right of possession is not documented
- prior title unavailable and proof is insufficient
- court order/instrument is missing when required

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify whether this is operation of law.
- Death/inheritance
- Repossession
- Court order
- Bankruptcy/judicial sale
- Storage/repair sale
- Other legal transfer

2. Determine whether prior title is available.
- If available, surrender/review title.
- If unavailable, obtain satisfactory proof of ownership and right of possession.

3. For deceased owner cases:
- Determine intestate vs testate.
- Review surviving spouse/heir documents.
- Review will/probate/affidavit/attorney affidavit as applicable.

4. For repossession:
- Confirm repossession affidavit.
- Review security agreement/default basis.
- Check subsequent lienholders.
- Confirm certified mail notice and waiting periods.

5. Validate application and fees.
- Title application required.
- Fees required.
- Supporting legal instrument required if applicable.

6. Hold/escalate if unclear.
- Escalate estate disputes, missing proof, lienholder protests, unclear right of possession, or court-order issues.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER STYLE
