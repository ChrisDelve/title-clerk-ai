COMMON CLERK ANSWER:
If the customer has no valid Florida insurance, do not complete registration, new plate issuance, or transfer plate issuance. Verify valid Florida insurance proof before submitting the registration application. If the transaction is title-only and no plate or registration is being issued, it may be handled separately, but do not issue registration or plates without valid insurance proof. Hold and escalate if insurance validity, VIN, effective dates, or coverage proof is unclear.

REJECTION PREVENTION SUMMARY
============================================================

High rejection/compliance risk exists when:
- registration is attempted without required insurance proof
- VIN verification is missing for out-of-state vehicle registration
- registration stop is ignored
- tax collector refusal condition is ignored
- customer driver license surrender issue blocks registration
- registration record is not verified before relying on it
- plate is not in customer’s name
- plate transfer eligibility is assumed without owner match
- IRF is skipped when new plate is required
- plate classification mismatch is ignored
- replacement plate documentation is missing
- stolen plate police report is missing for no-charge replacement
- registration expiration/delinquent fee is missed
- advance renewal is attempted outside allowed window
- exact fee is quoted without vehicle classification and fee table review
- exemption is claimed without documentary proof
- dealer plate is used outside allowed inventory/demo/business purpose
- temporary tag is issued without statutory basis
- more than two temp tags are issued without allowed exception
- transporter plate is used like normal registration
- refund/credit is promised without surrender/timing review
- back tax/dead storage affidavit issue is ignored
- nonresident exemption is applied despite Florida employment/public school/commercial/for-hire/RV/mobile home exceptions
