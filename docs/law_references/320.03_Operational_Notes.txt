STATUTE: 320.03
TITLE: Registration; Duties of Tax Collectors; International Registration Plan
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.03.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: TAX COLLECTOR AUTHORITY / REGISTRATION ISSUANCE / FRVIS / DRIVER LICENSE STOP / LICENSE PLATES / VALIDATION STICKERS

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs the role of county tax collectors as authorized agents of the department for registration issuance.

It also covers registration certificates, license plates, validation stickers, mobile home stickers, FRVIS / Florida Real Time Vehicle Information System, tax collector duties, and driver license stop checks during registration processing.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

County tax collectors act as authorized agents of the department for issuing registration certificates, license plates, validation stickers, and mobile home stickers.

Tax collectors must keep records and account for validation stickers, mobile home stickers, and other department property.

Tax collectors, third parties contracted with a tax collector, or license tag agents with online access must follow department access requirements.

When processing registration, unless the department issued a renewal notice, the tax collector or agent must check whether the applicant’s driver license has been canceled, suspended, or revoked.

If the applicant has not surrendered the license when required, the tax collector must refuse to register the vehicle until the license is surrendered.

FRVIS must be installed in tax collector/license tag agent offices according to department scheduling and funding.

A 50-cent fee is charged on every license registration sold to help fund FRVIS.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- applicant driver license issue is ignored
- required license surrender has not occurred
- dealer promises registration can be issued despite tax collector refusal condition
- registration stop is not resolved
- validation sticker/mobile home sticker issue is mishandled
- tax collector/FRVIS system requirement is ignored

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify registration issue.
- Tax collector refusal
- Tag office stop
- FRVIS/system issue
- Validation sticker/mobile home sticker issue

2. Check driver license status issue.
- Canceled
- Suspended
- Revoked
- Surrender requirement

3. Determine whether refusal condition applies.
- If license surrender required and not surrendered, registration cannot issue.

4. Do not override.
- Dealer should not promise registration can be completed if tax collector refusal condition exists.

5. Hold/escalate.
- Resolve stop/surrender issue before submission or delivery if registration is required.
