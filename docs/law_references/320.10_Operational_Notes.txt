TITLE: Exemptions

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute identifies certain vehicles and mobile homes that are exempt from the license taxes imposed under section 320.08.

It is the legal reference for:
- license tax exemptions
- nonresident military exemption
- federal government vehicle exemption
- certain nonprofit / public service vehicle exemptions
- exempt plate/sticker issuance
- documentary proof required for exemption

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

Section 320.10 provides exemptions from section 320.08 license taxes for certain vehicles and mobile homes.

Examples include:
- certain nonresident United States Armed Forces members stationed in Florida under military orders
- vehicles owned or operated exclusively by the Federal Government
- certain nonprofit or public service organizations
- church/temple/synagogue community service or religious transport vehicles
- Civil Air Patrol and United States Coast Guard Auxiliary vehicles
- nonprofit mobile blood bank units
- mobile X-ray/public health vehicles
- certain nonprofit school buses
- search and rescue vehicles
- certain transportation disadvantaged vehicles
- exempt marine boat trailers under section 320.102

Except for vehicles owned or operated exclusively by the Federal Government, exempt vehicles/mobile homes still receive a plate, validation sticker, or mobile home sticker after proper application and payment of $3.

The applicant must provide sufficient documentary proof of exemption eligibility at initial issuance and each renewal period.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- exemption is claimed without documentary proof
- military exemption is applied to a Florida resident without proper basis
- military orders/nonresident status are not verified
- nonprofit eligibility is assumed without proof
- exempt plate/sticker issuance is confused with no registration action
- $3 exempt plate/sticker cost is missed where applicable
- exemption is not reviewed again at renewal
- vehicle use does not match exemption category
