STATUTE: 319.29
TITLE: Lost or Destroyed Certificates
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.29.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: DUPLICATE TITLE / LOST TITLE / DESTROYED TITLE / LOST IN TRANSIT / OWNERSHIP VERIFICATION

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs duplicate certificates of title when a Florida certificate of title has been lost or destroyed.

It also governs reissuance when an original, duplicate, or corrected title was issued but lost in transit before delivery.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

If a certificate of title is lost or destroyed, the owner of the motor vehicle/mobile home or the lienholder may apply to the department for a duplicate title on the department-prescribed form.

The application must include the required fee and must be signed and sworn to by the applicant.

The department issues the duplicate title to the person entitled to receive the certificate of title under Chapter 319.

Duplicate titles and later titles in that duplicate-originated chain must be plainly marked “duplicate copy.”

If the original title is later recovered, the owner must surrender the original title to the department for cancellation.

If a title is lost in transit and not delivered to the addressee, the owner or lienholder may apply for reissuance within 180 days from the date of issuance, and no additional fee may be charged for that reissuance.

The department must verify that an application is signed by a person authorized to receive the duplicate title if the address on the application is different from the department’s records.

REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- applicant is not owner/lienholder/person entitled to receive title
- application is not signed/sworn properly
- required duplicate title form is missing or incomplete
- active lien status is ignored
- ELT status is ignored
- address mismatch is not reviewed
- duplicate title requested by unauthorized party
- original recovered title is not surrendered for cancellation
- lost-in-transit reissuance is requested outside 180-day window
- duplicate with transfer is processed without complete transfer documents
