STATUTE: 319.241
TITLE: Removal of Lien from Records
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.241.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: LIEN REMOVAL / OLD LIEN / 5-YEAR LIEN RULE / CERTIFIED MAIL / LIENHOLDER PROTEST

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs removal of a lien from department records or from a certificate of title when the lien has been filed with the department or noted on a certificate of title for a period of 5 years.

It is a core legal reference for old lien removal, unavailable lienholder issues, certified mail proof, and lienholder protest review.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

If a lien has been filed with the department or noted on a certificate of title for 5 years, the owner may apply in writing for the lien to be removed from department files or from the certificate of title.

The application must include satisfactory evidence that the applicant notified the lienholder by certified mail at least 20 days before the application date.

After receiving the application, the department may remove the lien after 10 days if no written protest is received from the lienholder.

If the lienholder files a written statement within the 10-day period saying the lien is still outstanding, or that a second judgment lien certificate has been filed with the Department of State, the department must not remove the lien until the lienholder presents satisfaction of lien.

If a second judgment lien certificate was filed, the department removes the first judgment lien notice and adds the second judgment lien certificate notation at the end of all noted liens.

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Confirm lien age.
- Determine how long the lien has been filed or noted.
- If under 5 years, do not treat 319.241 as available without other authority.

2. Confirm lienholder status.
- Available
- Unavailable
- Unresponsive
- Out of business
- Successor lienholder possible

3. Confirm certified mail.
- Certified mail notice must be sent to lienholder.
- Notice must be mailed at least 20 days before application.

4. Check protest period.
- Department may remove lien after 10 days if no written protest is received.
- If protest is received, lien removal is blocked until lienholder presents satisfaction.

5. Cross-check ELT status.
- If active ELT lien exists, review TL-33 ELT limitations before recommending alternate removal.

6. Hold or escalate.
- Escalate unclear lienholder, active ELT, protest, judgment lien, or missing proof issues.

COMMON CLERK ANSWER STYLE

4. Whether the department 10-day protest period applies.
5. Whether the lienholder protested.
6. Whether active ELT limitations or TL-33 review applies.
7. Whether the transaction can proceed, hold, or escalate.
