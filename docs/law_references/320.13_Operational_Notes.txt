STATUTE: 320.13
TITLE: Dealer and Manufacturer License Plates and Alternative Method of Registration
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.13.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: DEALER PLATES / MANUFACTURER PLATES / INVENTORY VEHICLES / DEMONSTRATION / DEALER BUSINESS USE / HEAVY TRUCK DEALER PLATES

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs dealer and manufacturer license plates and an alternative method of registration for licensed dealers, mobile home dealers, marine boat trailer dealers/manufacturers, heavy truck dealers, manufacturers, importers, and distributors.

It is the legal reference for:
- dealer plate use
- manufacturer plate use
- inventory vehicle use
- demonstration vehicle use
- dealer business use
- dealer plate restrictions
- heavy truck dealer demonstration limitations
- dealer regular plate transfer between comparable vehicles

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

Licensed motor vehicle dealers and licensed mobile home dealers may obtain dealer plates after paying the required license tax.

Dealer plates are valid for use on motor vehicles or mobile homes owned by the dealer while the units are in inventory and for sale, or while operated in connection with the dealer’s business.

Dealer plates are not valid for use for hire.

Dealer plates may not be used on a tow truck or wrecker unless the tow truck or wrecker is being demonstrated for sale.

Dealer plates may not be used on a vehicle used to transport another motor vehicle for the dealer.

Marine boat trailer dealers and manufacturers may obtain dealer plates for eligible boat trailers used in connection with their business, but not for hire.

Heavy truck dealers may obtain dealer plates for heavy trucks in inventory and for sale, used in Florida only for demonstration purposes, for no more than 24 hours.

Manufacturers, importers, and distributors may obtain manufacturer plates for vehicles owned by them while in inventory and for sale, demonstration, or business use, but not for hire.

A dealer who registers a vehicle or boat trailer with a regular plate may, upon sale, transfer that plate to a comparable dealer-owned vehicle or boat trailer of the same weight series after paying the transfer fee and submitting application.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High compliance risk if:
- dealer plate is used after sale/delivery improperly
- dealer plate is used for hire
- dealer plate is used on ineligible tow truck/wrecker
- dealer plate is used to transport another vehicle
- vehicle is not dealer-owned/in inventory/business use
- heavy truck dealer plate exceeds 24-hour demonstration rule
- heavy truck demo form is not retained in vehicle
- dealer regular plate transfer is to non-comparable vehicle or wrong weight series
