============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

The registration license plate and certificate of registration are issued to and remain in the name of the owner of the registered vehicle.

The owner may transfer the plate from the old vehicle to a newly acquired vehicle within the same classification.

If the replacement vehicle is a different classification, the plate may need to be surrendered in exchange for the appropriate classification.

When a vehicle is sold, traded, transferred, or otherwise disposed of, the owner must remove the plate and either return it or transfer it to a replacement vehicle.

A plate cannot be attached to a replacement vehicle without filing a transfer application and paying the $4.50 transfer fee unless an exception applies.

Plate transfer does not constitute a new registration.

A surviving spouse may request transfer of the registration certificate and plate after presenting the death certificate or if the department/agent verifies death record information.

When a dealer transfers a plate to a replacement/substitute vehicle, the dealer must timely provide transfer information to the department electronically unless department records are otherwise updated on the date of transfer.
