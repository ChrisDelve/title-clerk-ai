STATUTE: 319.23
TITLE: Application for, and Issuance of, Certificate of Title

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs applications for Florida certificates of title and the conditions required before the department may issue title.

It is one of the most important legal anchors for:
- title applications
- title issuance
- out-of-state title conversions
- used vehicle originals
- VIN verification
- odometer verification
- dealer responsibility to obtain title
- trade-in notice of sale
- bonded title review
- applicant ID/business verification
- taxes and registration requirements before title issuance

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

A Florida title application must be filed on the department’s required form and accompanied by required fees.

If a Florida title was previously issued, the application generally must include the assigned/reassigned title unless another Chapter 319 rule provides otherwise.

If the vehicle or mobile home is new and requires Manufacturer’s Statements of Origin under 319.21, all required MSO/MCO documents must be included.

Applicants must disclose certain vehicle uses/brands, including taxicab, police vehicle, lease vehicle, rebuilt vehicle, and rebuilt mobile home/RV-type units when applicable.

If the vehicle/mobile home was not previously titled in Florida, the application generally requires evidence of ownership from the prior jurisdiction and may require VIN verification and odometer verification.

Out-of-state title applications must show previous registration/title information and surrender foreign/out-of-state evidence in the applicant’s possession or control.

Licensed dealers selling to Florida purchasers must obtain title in the purchaser’s name.

Title/corrected title/assignment/reassignment applications must be filed within 30 days after delivery or mobile home sale completion, or a $20 fee applies in addition to other fees/penalties.

If a licensed dealer acquires a vehicle/mobile home as a trade-in, the dealer must file a notice of sale within 30 days.

If an applicant cannot provide prior title assigning ownership, bonded title review may apply if statutory bond and affidavit requirements are satisfied.

The department will not issue title until required taxes, registration, RP/mobile home notification, and odometer/title disclosure rules are satisfied.

The application/title must contain applicant identity information, and individuals must provide valid ID/passport. Business applicants must provide FEIN or business authorization/license information.

COMMON DEALERSHIP ISSUES
============================================================

- Missing title application form
- Missing assigned/reassigned prior title
- Missing MCO/MSO for new vehicle or mobile home
- Out-of-state title not surrendered when available
- VIN verification missing on out-of-state motor vehicle
- Used car original missing odometer affidavit
- Brand/use not disclosed correctly
- Dealer late title application over 30 days
- Dealer did not obtain title in purchaser’s name
- Dealer acquired trade but did not file notice of sale
- Out-of-state purchaser affidavit missing
- Bonded title incorrectly suggested when active lien exists
- Missing valid ID for individual applicant
- Missing FEIN or business authority for business applicant
- Taxes or required registration not satisfied before title issuance
- RP mobile home/property appraiser notification issue
- Mobile home missing serial/identification number

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- required ownership evidence is missing
- assigned/reassigned title is missing
- MCO/MSO is missing for new vehicle/mobile home
- VIN verification is missing when required
- odometer affidavit is missing for used car original
- dealer misses 30-day filing requirement
- out-of-state documentation is incomplete
- applicant identification is missing or invalid
- business applicant authority is missing
- sales/use taxes are not paid
- registration requirement is not satisfied
- title brand/use information is missing or wrong
- bonded title is attempted despite known active lien
- applicant cannot explain ownership/possession path

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify title application type.
- New MCO/MSO
- Florida title transfer
- Out-of-state title
- Used car original
- Dealer sale
- Trade-in
- Bonded title review

2. Verify ownership evidence.
- Prior Florida title assigned/reassigned
- MCO/MSO
- Out-of-state title/registration evidence
- Bill of sale/sworn ownership statement where applicable

3. Verify required supporting documentation.
- VIN verification if required
- Odometer affidavit if used car original
- Brand/use disclosure
- Valid ID/business authority
- Taxes and registration requirements

4. Review dealer obligations.
- Dealer title application within required timing
- Trade-in notice of sale
- Out-of-state purchaser affidavit if applicable

5. Review bonded title only when appropriate.
- Prior title assigning ownership cannot be provided
- No known liens/security interests/encumbrances
- Bond and affidavit requirements satisfied

6. Decide proceed/hold/escalate.
- Hold if ownership evidence, VIN verification, ID, lien, tax, or registration requirements are missing.

6. Whether bonded title is appropriate or blocked by known liens.
7. Whether the transaction can proceed, hold, or escalate.
