PURPOSE

This file is a consolidated legal-reference layer for Florida title ownership and transfer issues.

It groups the most important Chapter 319 statutes related to:
- title applicability

- certificate of origin
- title application
- title transfer
- AND / OR ownership

- operation of law
- deceased owner transactions
- repossession

- transfer without title

This file is designed for AI retrieval and workflow routing.

It does NOT replace individual detailed statute files.

Use this file to help the AI determine which specific law reference, procedure note, logic map, or dealership workflow should be retrieved next.

This file should be treated as a MASTER TITLE LAW ROUTING FILE.

When a user asks a broad title/ownership/transfer question, this file helps classify the issue and route to the correct detailed statute file.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION SUMMARY
============================================================

High rejection risk exists when:
- ownership evidence is missing
- assigned title is missing
- title says AND but not all required owners signed
- seller is not owner of record
- purchaser name is missing from title assignment
- MCO/MSO is missing for new vehicle/mobile home
- VIN verification is missing when required
- VIN mismatch exists
- active lien is not resolved
- ELT status is ignored
- lien satisfaction is missing
- duplicate title applicant is not entitled to receive title
- deceased owner documents are incomplete
- repossession affidavit or lienholder notice is missing
- title appears altered or forged
- customer relies only on bill of sale when title is required
- canceled title issue is ignored
- destroyed/dismantled vehicle title surrender issue exists
- bonded title is used incorrectly to bypass known lien or ownership defects
