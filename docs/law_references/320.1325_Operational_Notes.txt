STATUTE: 320.1325
TITLE: Registration Required for the Temporarily Employed
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.1325.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: TEMPORARILY EMPLOYED / NONRESIDENT REGISTRATION / 90-DAY TEMPORARY REGISTRATION / TEMPORARY PLATE

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs registration for nonresident persons who are temporarily employed in Florida and own or lease motor vehicles.

It is the legal reference for:
- temporarily employed nonresident registration
- 90-day temporary registration plate
- one 90-day renewal
- 180-day maximum temporary registration period
- permanent registration requirement after 180 days
- no commercial motor vehicle temporary registration under this statute
- later Florida insurance and IRF review if permanent registration/titling is needed

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

A person who is temporarily employed in Florida but is not a Florida resident must register a motor vehicle owned or leased by that person.

After payment of required fees and proof of insurance coverage required by the applicant’s resident state, the department provides a temporary registration plate and registration certificate valid for 90 days.

The temporary registration plate may be renewed one time for an additional 90 days.

At the end of 180 days, if the person still needs to remain in Florida, they must apply for permanent registration.

This temporary registration plate may not be issued for a commercial motor vehicle.

The fee for the 90-day temporary registration plate is $40 plus applicable service charge.

If the applicant later permanently registers and titles the vehicle in Florida, Florida insurance proof and Initial Registration Fee review may apply.

COMMON DEALERSHIP ISSUES
============================================================

- Nonresident customer is working temporarily in Florida
- Customer wants Florida temp registration but not permanent registration
- Customer already used 90-day temporary registration once
- Customer wants more than 180 days temporary registration
- Customer only has out-of-state insurance
- Customer needs to permanently register after temporary period
- Vehicle is commercial and not eligible under this section
- IRF not reviewed when customer switches to permanent Florida registration

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- customer is not actually temporarily employed in Florida
- customer is a Florida resident
- proof of resident-state insurance is missing
- temporary registration exceeds 180 days
- commercial motor vehicle is processed under this section
- permanent registration is needed but Florida insurance is missing
- IRF is skipped on subsequent permanent registration when applicable
