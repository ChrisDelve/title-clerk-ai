STATUTE: 320.05
TITLE: Records of the Department; Inspection Procedure; Lists and Searches; Fees
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.05.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: REGISTRATION RECORDS / RECORD LOOKUP / PUBLIC RECORDS / RECORD SEARCH / CERTIFIED COPIES / DEALER ACCESS

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs department registration records, inspection procedures, record searches, lists, certified/noncertified copies, electronic access, fees, and identity requirements for access to registration information.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

The department may release records as provided by law.

When a registration application is received, the department registers the motor vehicle, vessel, or mobile home under the distinctive number assigned by the department.

Electronic registration records are open for inspection during business hours, but motor vehicle or vessel registration information may not be released unless the person requesting the information provides positive proof of identification.

Agencies furnishing motor vehicle or vessel registration information must record the name and address of the person requesting/receiving information and the name/address or identifying information of the subject of the inquiry.

Inquiry records must be kept for 6 months.

Certain businesses and professionals, including motor vehicle dealers, financial institutions, insurance companies, attorneys, and other right-to-know agencies may obtain records for professional/business use through department-approved telecommunications access if applicable fees are paid.

The department may provide lists, searches, copies, certified copies, printouts, electronic access by tag/VIN/title/decal, and other records for statutory fees.

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify record need.
- Tag lookup
- VIN lookup
- Title number lookup
- Decal lookup
- Certified copy
- Noncertified copy
- Audit trail support

2. Verify access authority.
- Dealer business use
- Customer request
- Tax collector / FLHSMV
- Government/court request

3. Confirm identity/privacy requirements.
- Positive proof of identification may be required.
- Record request/inquiry may need to be logged.

4. Use record to support transaction.
- Verify registration status.
- Verify plate/decal assignment.
- Confirm owner/vehicle relationship.
- Support audit trail or correction.

5. Hold/escalate if unclear.
- Do not rely on unsupported assumptions.

============================================================
COMMON CLERK ANSWER STYLE
============================================================

When answering a 320.05 question, the AI should explain:

1. What record needs to be searched.
2. What identifier is available: tag, VIN, title number, decal number.
3. Whether certified/noncertified copy is needed.
4. Whether ID/privacy/access rules apply.
5. Whether the record supports proceeding, holding, or escalating.
