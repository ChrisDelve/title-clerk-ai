STATUTE: 320.18
TITLE: Withholding Registration
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.18.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: WITHHOLDING REGISTRATION / BACK TAX / DEAD STORAGE AFFIDAVIT / DISHONORED CHECK / WEIGHT OR SAFETY PENALTY / MOBILE HOME REPOSSESSION

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs withholding or canceling registration in certain back-tax, unpaid-fee, dishonored-check, unpaid-penalty, dead-storage, and mobile-home repossession situations.

It is the legal reference for:
- registration withheld for prior unpaid registration periods
- back tax
- dead storage affidavit exemption
- cancellation due to dishonored check
- cancellation due to unpaid weight/safety penalty
- impound of commercial motor vehicle after canceled plate/fuel-use decal
- mobile home repossession registration exemption

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

The department may withhold registration of a motor vehicle or mobile home if the owner or co-owner failed to register it for a previous period when registration should have been made, until the tax for that prior period is paid.

The department may cancel a vehicle or vessel registration, driver license, identification card, or fuel-use tax decal if the owner/co-owner paid with a dishonored check or failed to pay certain weight or safety violation penalties.

DOT and DHSMV may impound a commercial motor vehicle with a canceled plate or fuel-use tax decal until the tax liability, penalties, interest, license tax, fuel-use decal fee, and administrative fees are paid by certified funds.

An owner may be exempt from paying license tax for prior periods if the owner presents a notarized or certified affidavit stating the vehicle was continuously maintained in dead storage and was not operated during the registration period claimed.

In repossession, a mobile home is exempt from registration when the dwelling is not transferred or titled for occupancy.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- back tax is ignored
- prior registration period was missed
- dead storage affidavit is missing or defective
- vehicle was operated during claimed storage period
- dishonored check stop is not resolved
- unpaid weight/safety penalty is ignored
- commercial vehicle canceled plate/decal is ignored
- mobile home repossession exemption is misapplied
- dealer promises registration despite department withholding condition

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify registration block.
- Back tax
- Prior period not registered
- Dishonored check
- Unpaid weight/safety penalty
- Commercial vehicle canceled plate/fuel-use decal
- Mobile home repossession exemption

2. Determine if prior tax is due.
- Was vehicle required to be registered for prior period?
- Was it actually operated?

3. Review dead storage claim.
- Notarized/certified affidavit.
- Continuous dead storage.
- No operation during claimed period.

4. Resolve cancellation/stop.
- Dishonored check.
- Certified funds if required.
- Unpaid penalties.

5. Mobile home repossession review.
- Exempt only when dwelling is not transferred or titled for occupancy.

6. Hold/escalate if unresolved.
- Do not submit registration until withholding reason is resolved.
