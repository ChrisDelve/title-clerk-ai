STATUTE: 319.34
TITLE: Transfer Without Delivery of Certificate; Operation or Use Without Certificate; Failure to Surrender; Other Violations
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.34.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: TRANSFER WITHOUT TITLE / TITLE DELIVERY / OPERATING WITHOUT TITLE / FAILURE TO SURRENDER / CANCELLED TITLE / DISMANTLED VEHICLE

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute establishes penalties for selling/transferring a motor vehicle or mobile home without delivering a properly assigned certificate of title, operating/using a vehicle or mobile home that requires title without obtaining title, failing to surrender canceled titles/registrations/plates/stickers, failing to surrender title when a vehicle/mobile home is destroyed/dismantled/changed, and violating Chapter 319 or lawful rules adopted under it.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

A person generally may not sell or transfer a motor vehicle or mobile home without delivering a properly assigned certificate of title to the purchaser/transferee.

A person generally may not operate or use a motor vehicle or mobile home in Florida when a certificate of title is required but has not been obtained, or when the title has been canceled.

A person must surrender title, registration, license plate, or sticker after cancellation when required by the department.

A person must surrender title when the vehicle/mobile home is destroyed, dismantled, or changed so that it is no longer the same vehicle/mobile home described in the title.

Violations may result in a fine up to $500, imprisonment up to 6 months, or both, for each offense.

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- properly assigned title is missing
- title was not delivered to purchaser/transferee
- title assignment chain is incomplete
- vehicle is used/operated without required certificate of title
- canceled title issue is ignored
- required surrender items were not surrendered
- destroyed/dismantled vehicle title was not surrendered
- clerk processes transfer based only on bill of sale when title is required
- title jumping is not escalated

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify title delivery issue.
- Was title delivered?
- Was title properly assigned?
- Is title missing?
- Is title canceled?

2. Determine transaction type.
- Trade-in
- Private sale
- Dealer sale
- Duplicate title before transfer
- Salvage/COD/dismantled vehicle

3. Verify title ownership chain.
- Confirm seller is owner of record.
- Confirm assignment is complete.
- Confirm purchaser/transferee is properly listed.

4. Hold if title is missing.
- Do not submit as normal transfer.
- Review duplicate title/title-obtainment workflow.

5. Escalate high-risk cases.
- Canceled title
- Title jumping
- Destroyed/dismantled vehicle
- Failure to surrender title/plate/sticker
- Fraud concern
