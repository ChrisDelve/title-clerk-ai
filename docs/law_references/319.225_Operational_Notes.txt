STATUTE: 319.225
TITLE: Transfer and Reassignment Forms; Odometer Disclosure Statements
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.225.html

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs odometer disclosure requirements during transfer or reassignment of title.

It is the core Florida law reference for:
- odometer disclosure statements
- dealer reassignment odometer disclosure
- secure power of attorney for odometer disclosure
- lienholder-held titles
- lost or unavailable titles
- electronic title reassignment
- auction notation requirements
- odometer disclosure exemptions

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

Florida certificates of title must contain odometer disclosure language and odometer disclosure fields.

When a used motor vehicle is transferred or reassigned, the transferor must complete the odometer disclosure statement and the transferee must acknowledge the disclosure by signing and printing their name.

The department may not issue title unless required odometer disclosure rules are satisfied.

Dealer reassignment forms must contain odometer disclosure statements and dealer copies must be retained for 5 years.

If title is physically held by a lienholder, or if title is lost or otherwise unavailable, the transferor may use a department-authorized power of attorney for odometer disclosure.

If a title is electronic and the transferee agrees to keep it electronic, transferor and transferee must complete a secure reassignment document with odometer disclosure at the tax collector or license plate agency.

COMMON DEALERSHIP ISSUES
============================================================

- Odometer disclosure missing
- Transferee did not sign/print name
- Transferor did not complete mileage
- Mileage entered differently from secure POA
- Dealer reassignment spaces full
- Dealer failed to keep reassignment copy
- Secure POA used incorrectly
- Title held by lienholder and mileage disclosure incomplete
- Title lost/unavailable and mileage disclosure not handled correctly
- Electronic title reassignment not completed at proper office
- Auction information missing
- Odometer exemption applied incorrectly

============================================================
REJECTION PREVENTION
============================================================

High rejection risk if:
- odometer disclosure is blank
- transferor mileage statement is missing
- transferee acknowledgment/signature is missing
- dealer reassignment form is incomplete
- secure POA is missing when required
- mileage copied from POA does not match exactly
- auction information is missing
- odometer exemption is applied incorrectly
- electronic reassignment process is skipped
- dealer fails to submit or retain required POA/reassignment documents

============================================================
DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Determine whether odometer disclosure is required.
- Used motor vehicle transfer or reassignment
- Check exemptions by vehicle type, GVWR, self-propelled status, and model year age

2. Verify disclosure.
- Transferor completes mileage disclosure
- Transferee signs and prints name
- Mileage is clear and consistent

3. Review reassignment.
- Check dealer reassignment spaces
- Use department reassignment form if needed
- Retain dealer copy as required

4. Review secure POA if title is unavailable.
- Lienholder-held title
- Lost/unavailable title
- Mileage on title must match POA exactly

5. Review ELT/electronic reassignment.
- Secure reassignment document may be required at tax collector/license plate agency

6. Hold/escalate if inconsistent.
- Mileage mismatch
- missing disclosure
- incorrect exemption
- questionable odometer reading
