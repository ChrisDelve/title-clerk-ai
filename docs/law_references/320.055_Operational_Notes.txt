STATUTE: 320.055
TITLE: Registration Periods; Renewal Periods
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0320/Sections/0320.055.html
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Florida Law Reference / Operational Notes
CATEGORY: REGISTRATION PERIODS / RENEWAL PERIODS / BIRTH MONTH / FLEET / APPORTIONED / COMMERCIAL / SHORT-TERM RENTAL

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute establishes Florida registration periods and renewal periods for different vehicle classes.

It is the legal anchor for questions about registration expiration timing, renewal period, birth month registration, co-owner birth month rule, 6-month registration after DUI-related license reinstatement, calendar-year registration, June-to-May registration periods, and apportioned/commercial registration periods.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

For many vehicles owned by a natural person, the registration period begins on the first day of the owner’s birth month and ends on the last day of the month immediately before the owner’s birth month the next year.

If the vehicle is registered in more than one person’s name, the birth month of the person whose name first appears on the registration is used.

For these vehicles, the renewal period is the 30-day period ending at midnight on the vehicle owner’s date of birth.

Some vehicles are eligible for extended registration periods.

Certain owners whose licenses were suspended for DUI-related reasons must obtain 6-month registration as a condition of reinstatement during the applicable financial responsibility period.

Vehicles not owned by a natural person under certain classes may have January 1 through December 31 registration periods.

Certain vehicle classes, short-term rental vehicles, and unspecified periods may run June 1 through May 31.

Apportioned and certain commercial vehicles have separate registration periods assigned by the department.

DEALERSHIP WORKFLOW
============================================================

1. Identify vehicle/owner category.
- Natural person
- Multiple natural persons
- Business/entity
- Commercial/apportioned
- Short-term rental
- Mobile home or special class

2. Determine registration period rule.
- Birth month
- Calendar year
- June-to-May
- December-to-November
- Department-designated apportioned period
- Six-month period

3. Determine renewal period.
- Birthday-based renewal
- 31-day before expiration
- Assigned commercial renewal period

4. Confirm before quoting customer.
- Do not assume every registration expires on birthday.

5. Hold/escalate if unclear.
- Commercial/apportioned or special class questions should be reviewed carefully.
