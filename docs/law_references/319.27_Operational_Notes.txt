TITLE: Notice of Lien on Motor Vehicles or Mobile Homes; Notation on Certificate; Recording of Lien
SOURCE: https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&Search_String=&URL=0300-0399/0319/Sections/0319.27.html

CATEGORY: NOTICE OF LIEN / LIEN RECORDING / LIEN PERFECTION / OUT-OF-STATE LIEN / LIEN ASSIGNMENT / ELT

============================================================
PURPOSE
============================================================

This statute governs notice of lien, notation of liens on Florida certificates of title, lien perfection, recording of liens on motor vehicles and mobile homes, out-of-state lien recording before Florida title issuance, lien priority, assignment of lien, and electronic lien transmission.

It is a core legal reference for lien add, lien recording, lienholder substitution, lien assignment, ELT requirements, and lien priority.

============================================================
PLAIN ENGLISH SUMMARY
============================================================

Each lien, mortgage, or encumbrance on a motor vehicle or mobile home titled in Florida must be noted on the Florida certificate of title or on a duplicate/corrected copy.

A lien or nonpossessory lien is generally not enforceable against creditors or subsequent purchasers without notice unless a sworn notice of lien has been filed with the department and the lien is noted on the certificate of title.

The notice of lien must include:
- date of lien
- name and address of registered owner
- vehicle/mobile home description including make, type, and VIN
- lienholder name and address

A notice of lien may be filed before the security agreement is executed.

For secured-party rights, perfection occurs when the notice of lien is filed with the department, tax collector, or agents, with a 15-day relation-back rule if filed within 15 days after debtor receives possession and executes the security agreement.

For vehicles/mobile homes previously titled or registered outside Florida where no Florida title has been issued, lienholders may file sworn notice of lien with the department.

When Florida title is first issued on an out-of-state vehicle/mobile home, the department notes:
- liens shown on the Florida title application
- liens previously filed with the department
- liens shown on the existing out-of-state title

Liens take priority according to the order in which they are noted, except the lien shown on an original title application takes priority over later-filed liens.

Lien assignments may be substituted on the certificate of title after delivering original title and a sworn statement of assignment.

The department must administer an electronic titling program, and lienholders must electronically transmit liens and lien satisfactions unless exempt.

============================================================

- lienholder name/address is missing
- owner information is missing
- vehicle description or VIN is missing
- out-of-state lien is ignored
- lien assignment documentation is missing
- original title is not provided for lienholder substitution where required
- sworn assignment statement is missing
- ELT requirement is ignored
- active ELT lien is treated as paper lien
- lien priority/order is not reviewed
- Florida title is issued without noting required liens
